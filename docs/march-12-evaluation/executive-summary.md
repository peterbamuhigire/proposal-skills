## The Verdict

**Overall readiness: 80–82% of what is needed for consistent million-dollar wins.**

The repository began the day at 65–70% readiness with excellent architecture but significant depth gaps. Six remediation sessions (Sessions 6–11) added 8,870 lines across 24 new files, filling the most critical gaps in domain knowledge, procurement frameworks, and sector content. The result is a **materially stronger platform** — one that can now produce competitive proposals across all four procurement frameworks and nine sector directories. The remaining 18–20% gap lies in five domain skills still lacking references, the absence of worked examples and evaluation scoring guidance, and the need for generic sector methodology skills alongside the Uganda-specific data now in place.

### The Gap Between "Competent" and "Compelling" — Updated

The original evaluation identified five areas where the repository fell short of Big Four depth. Three of those five have now been substantially addressed:

- **Change management** — Three reference files now cover process models (Satir, Lewin, Kübler-Ross, Burke-Litwin), hard/soft integration (project charter, four-team model, RACI, resistance management), and lean/adaptive approaches (canvases, digital CM, adoption measurement). Proposals can now cite specific readiness assessment tools and change saturation models.
- **M&E frameworks** — Four reference files now cover results frameworks, evaluation design (including impact evaluation with RCT, quasi-experimental methods, and contribution analysis), monitoring systems, and economic analysis (CBA, shadow pricing, distributional analysis). This is a step change from "just a logframe."
- **Stakeholder engagement** — Three reference files now cover Mitchell-Agle-Wood salience, sociodynamics, hidden stakeholders, political economy awareness, conflict modes, and 17 cognitive biases. Proposals can now demonstrate political economy sophistication.

Two areas remain at the original gap level:

- **GESI** — Still limited to SKILL.md instructions. No reference depth on intersectionality analysis, gender budgeting, or women's agency measurement.
- **Sector-specific language** — Uganda-specific data now exists for all 9 sectors, but generic sector methodology skills are not yet written. Proposals for non-Uganda assignments in these sectors will still rely on general consulting knowledge.
