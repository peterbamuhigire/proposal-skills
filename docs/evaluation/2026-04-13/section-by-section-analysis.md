# Section-by-Section Analysis

## 01 Cover Letter

### Strengths

- Strong rejection of generic boilerplate.
- Good emphasis on client-specific hook, differentiators, and comparable evidence.

### Weaknesses

- Still mostly prose-led rather than score-led.
- Does not explicitly map cover-letter claims to later evidence blocks.

### Improvements

- Require 3-4 named win themes from the brief.
- Add a claim-to-proof cross-check before finalisation.

## 02 Executive Summary

### Strengths

- One of the stronger section skills in the repo.
- Good use of client problem first, differentiators, scope summary, and benefits-forward close.

### Weaknesses

- No mandatory one-page decision-maker version.
- Differentiators are defined, but not explicitly tied to scoring criteria.

### Improvements

- Add a governing-idea field from `BRIEF.md`.
- Add a score-backed differentiator grid.

## 03 Understanding Of Assignment

### Strengths

- Strong conceptual ambition.
- Goes beyond paraphrase and pushes for diagnosis, overriding question, and problem facets.

### Weaknesses

- High conceptual sophistication may be hard to operationalise consistently.
- Less guidance on converting diagnosis into criterion-winning text.

### Improvements

- Add a clause-by-clause ToR analysis matrix.
- Add a problem -> implication -> method linkage table.

## 04 Firm Profile

### Strengths

- Sensible insistence on bid-specific relevance rather than brochure content.
- Useful handling of individual vs company proposer formats.

### Weaknesses

- Less differentiated than top-tier firm profiles.
- Limited guidance on proprietary assets, thought leadership, and institutional delivery proof.

### Improvements

- Add a requirement-to-capability table.
- Add an institutional differentiator panel.

## 05 Relevant Experience

### Strengths

- Correctly treats experience as highly weighted and outcome-driven.
- Strong insistence on quantified outcomes and relevance ordering.

### Weaknesses

- Relies heavily on the user having strong quantified case data ready.
- Less support for aggregated proof of scale.

### Improvements

- Build a reusable case-study registry.
- Add an experience score strategy mode that selects cases by criterion weight.

## 06 Methodology

### Strengths

- The strongest section skill in the repository.
- Rich consulting logic: hypothesis-driven design, logic trees, P-I-P, deliverables, risk, QA, governance, knowledge transfer.

### Weaknesses

- Strong conceptually, but still operator-dependent.
- Not enough enforcement of coherence with team, work plan, and pricing.

### Improvements

- Add a methodology control sheet with phases, deliverables, roles, client inputs, risks, and payment links.
- Require at least one clear differentiator in each phase.

## 07 Team Composition

### Strengths

- Good understanding of donor-style CV scoring, especially World Bank.
- Correctly emphasises role fit, person-days, and credibility.

### Weaknesses

- Strong on formatting, less strong on team strategy.
- Limited support for proving complementarity and delivery resilience.

### Improvements

- Add task-to-expert traceability.
- Add a capabilities matrix and bench-strength note.

## 08 Work Plan

### Strengths

- Good realism on review periods, dependency logic, and staffing consistency.
- Correctly insists on consistency with pricing.

### Weaknesses

- Still mainly a static planning artefact.
- Less guidance on using the work plan to reinforce differentiators.

### Improvements

- Add a schedule stress-test.
- Add a deliverable review and approval matrix.

## 09 Expression Of Interest

### Strengths

- Clear recognition that EOI logic differs from full proposal logic.
- Good focus on shortlisting criteria, relevance, and credentials.

### Weaknesses

- Slightly too compliance-oriented for very competitive EOIs.
- Limited guidance on shortlist strategy relative to likely competitors.

### Improvements

- Add an EOI-specific value thesis.
- Add route variants by procurement method.

## 10 Financial Proposal

### Strengths

- Good baseline structure for fees, reimbursables, payment schedule, and assumptions.
- Correct emphasis on separation from the technical envelope.

### Weaknesses

- Not yet strong enough on commercial strategy.
- Limited differentiation between pricing for QCBS, FBS, LCS, or private-sector RFPs.

### Improvements

- Add a pricing-strategy layer by procurement method.
- Add a phase-cost matrix and commercial options mode.

## Overall Section Verdict

The numbered section skills are generally strong and unusually thoughtful.

The main weakness across the set is not section quality in isolation. It is the lack of a stronger system to ensure:

- one strategy
- one story
- one score logic
- one commercial posture

across all ten sections.
