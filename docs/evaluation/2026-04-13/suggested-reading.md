# Suggested Reading

## How To Use This List

This list is targeted at the repository's main gaps:

- strategy control
- narrative architecture
- evaluator persuasion
- methodology design
- pricing logic
- procurement alignment

## Proposal Strategy And Narrative

### 1. *Writing Winning Business Proposals* by Richard C. Freed, Shervin Freed, and Karen Hart

Why it matters:

- It is the clearest direct bridge between consulting logic and proposal structure.
- Much of the repository's best doctrine already traces back to this book.

What it improves:

- brief quality
- proposal logic
- objection handling
- coherence across sections

### 2. *The Pyramid Principle* by Barbara Minto

Why it matters:

- This is the foundation for executive-level communication structure.
- Top-tier consulting proposals rely on top-down message discipline, not paragraph accumulation.

What it improves:

- executive summary quality
- section openings
- exhibit logic
- ghost-pack development

### 3. *The McKinsey Mind* by Ethan Rasiel and Paul Friga

Why it matters:

- It gives a practical summary of issue trees, hypothesis-driven work, and structured problem solving.

What it improves:

- methodology depth
- problem structuring
- analytical credibility

### 4. *Strategic Selling* by Robert Miller and Stephen Heiman

Why it matters:

- It sharpens the distinction between economic, technical, user, and coach buyers.

What it improves:

- buyer mapping
- section targeting
- internal advocacy logic

### 5. *The Trusted Advisor* by David Maister, Charles Green, and Robert Galford

Why it matters:

- Winning proposals are not only about analysis; they are about trust transfer.

What it improves:

- cover letters
- tone
- trust framing
- relationship positioning

## Pricing And Commercial Logic

### 6. *Pricing with Confidence* by Reed Holden and Mark Burton

Why it matters:

- The repository needs stronger commercial strategy, not just cost tables.

What it improves:

- QCBS pricing choices
- private-sector commercial offers
- value articulation in financial proposals

## Procurement And Donor Frameworks

### 7. World Bank Procurement Resources

Why it matters:

- World Bank scoring and rated-criteria guidance changes over time.

Recommended official sources:

- World Bank procurement hub: https://www.worldbank.org/procurement
- Rated Criteria page: https://www.worldbank.org/en/about/rated-criteria

What it improves:

- evaluation alignment
- technical scoring strategy
- compliance accuracy

### 8. PPDA Uganda Official Sources

Why it matters:

- A Uganda-focused proposal engine should stay close to PPDA primary sources.

Recommended official sources:

- PPDA home page: https://www.ppda.go.ug/
- PPDA legislation resource centre: https://www.ppda.go.ug/download-reports/resource-center/procurement-legislation/

What it improves:

- pass/fail compliance
- domestic preference understanding
- government bid packaging accuracy

### 9. African Development Bank Procurement And Safeguards

Why it matters:

- AfDB scoring and safeguards should not be treated as interchangeable with World Bank practice.

Recommended official sources:

- AfDB Integrated Safeguards System 2023: https://www.afdb.org/en/documents/african-development-bank-groups-integrated-safeguards-system-2023
- AfDB ISS effectiveness notice dated 30 May 2024: https://www.afdb.org/en/news-and-events/announcement-effectiveness-bank-groups-updated-integrated-safeguards-system-iss-71365

What it improves:

- safeguards credibility
- AfDB compliance
- African participation logic

### 10. UN Procurement And Supplier Standards

Why it matters:

- UN procurement is less standardised than World Bank procurement.

Recommended official sources:

- UNGM Supplier Code of Conduct: https://www.ungm.org/Public/CodeOfConduct
- UN Procurement Practitioner's Handbook: https://www.ungm.org/Shared/KnowledgeCenter/Pages/PPH2
- UNDP procurement strategy page: https://www.undp.org/procurement/strategy

What it improves:

- UN/UNDP compliance discipline
- ethics and supplier positioning
- differentiation between grant and consulting pathways

1. *Writing Winning Business Proposals*

3. *The McKinsey Mind*
4. *Strategic Selling*
