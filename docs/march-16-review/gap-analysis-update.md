## New Observations (16 March)

While no new structural gaps have emerged, Session 13's work reveals several observations worth noting:

### N1. Environmental and Social Safeguards Are WB-Specific

The three new ESS reference files are based entirely on World Bank ESS Guidance Notes. While the World Bank ESF is the gold standard that other frameworks reference, proposals for AfDB-funded assignments should cross-reference the AfDB ISS 2023 (already covered in `sectors/afdb/references/safeguards-and-financial-terms.md`). The two frameworks are complementary but not identical — AfDB expanded from 5 to 10 Operational Safeguards in 2023. A proposal writer should load both when working on AfDB assignments.

**Action:** Consider adding a cross-reference note to the ESS SKILL.md pointing to AfDB ISS for AfDB-funded work. Minimal effort (~5 lines).

### N2. Data Protection Coverage Is East Africa-Focused

The data-protection-and-privacy.md reference covers Uganda DPA 2019, Kenya DPA 2019, and GDPR principles. It does not cover Rwanda's data protection framework or Tanzania's Electronic and Postal Communications Act provisions on data. For assignments in those countries, supplementary research would be needed.

**Action:** Low priority. The framework is sound; country-specific details can be researched per assignment.

### N3. Capacity Building References Are Strong on Theory, Light on Sector Application

The two capacity building reference files cover frameworks (ECDPM 5C, USAID HICD, Senge) and institutional strengthening comprehensively. However, they do not include sector-specific capacity building patterns — what capacity building looks like in health vs ICT vs agriculture differs substantially.

**Action:** This will be partially addressed by the sector SKILL.md files (C5). No separate action needed.

### N4. Sustainability File Is the Thinnest New Domain Reference

At 601 lines from a single primary source (Hester & Harrison), sustainability-planning has the smallest reference library of any domain skill. By comparison, environmental and social safeguards has 1,381 lines from 8 sources. Sustainability is a scored criterion in most donor evaluations, particularly for AfDB (which includes 8 sustainability sub-factors in its PCR framework).

**Action:** Consider supplementing with additional sustainability sources in a future session. Not blocking — the file is substantive and covers exit strategies, institutional embedding, financial sustainability, and environmental sustainability. But it could benefit from donor-specific sustainability assessment tools.

### N5. Domain Reference Depth Is Now Uneven

The 12 domain skills range from 232 lines (risk management, 1 file) to 1,381 lines (environmental and social safeguards, 3 files). This unevenness reflects the complexity of each domain rather than a quality gap — risk management is inherently more compact than safeguards. However, project management (356 lines, 1 file) and risk management (232 lines, 1 file) are the two thinnest domain references and were created early in the remediation process (Session 5). They may benefit from enhancement in a future session.

**Action:** Low priority. Both files are functional. Enhancement would improve but not transform proposal quality.
