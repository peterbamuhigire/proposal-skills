## 1. Country Overview

- Population: 45.9 million (2024), 51% women, 72% under 30, 70%+ rural
- Landlocked, 241,551 km², borders Kenya, Tanzania, Rwanda, DRC, South Sudan
- Low-income country, GDP per capita USD 1,072.71 (2024)
- Nominal GDP: USD 53.7 billion (2024), up from USD 4.5 billion in 1986
- Life expectancy: 68.7 years (AfDB 2024); UN breakdown: total 68.2, females 70.1, males 66.9 (2024)
- Fertility rate trend: 5.8 (2014) → 4.5 (2024)
- Birth registration: only 40.4% of children registered (2022)
- Urban population growth rate: 8.8% (2014–2021); urban day population: 5.5 million (2024)
- Fiscal year: 1 July – 30 June
- Exchange rate: 1 USD = 3,621.12 UGX (December 2025)
- S&P sovereign credit rating: B-/B (outlook revised to Positive, November 2025)

## 2. Development Framework

- Vision 2040: transform from low-income to upper-middle-income (per capita USD 9,500)
- NDP IV (2025/26–2029/30): current medium-term plan
- 10-Fold Growth Strategy (2025–2040): doubling economy every 5 years
- Four development phases since independence (1962): post-independence, stabilisation/recovery (1986–1996), PEAP (1997–2009), growth/transformation (2010–present)
- Current model shift needed: from debt-financed public spending to private-sector-led growth

## 3. Macroeconomic Indicators

- Real GDP growth: averaged 4.9% (2019–2024); projected 6.4% (2025), 7.5% (2026)
- Services: 43.1% of GDP; Industry: 24.9%; Agriculture: 24.7%
- Fiscal deficit: 7.6% of GDP (FY2024/25), projected to narrow to 6.5% then 5.5%
- Tax revenue: 13.8% of GDP (below SSA average of 16%)
- Public debt: USD 32.3 billion (51.3% of GDP, June 2025); 52% domestic, 48% external
- Interest payments: 31.1% of domestic revenue
- IMF debt distress rating: moderate risk, limited shock-absorption space
- Inflation: 3.4% (October 2025), CBR at 9.75%
- Current account deficit: 6.4% of GDP (FY2024/25), expected to balance by FY2027/28
- FDI: USD 3.6 billion (FY2024/25, 5.4% of GDP); inflows rose from USD 1.19 billion (2020) to USD 2.99 billion (2023)
- Remittances: USD 1.43 billion (2023)
- Net ODA: 4.74% of GNI (2022)
- Government revenue (excl. grants): 14.4% of GDP (up from 11.6% in 2015)
- GDP per capita: USD 1,146 (2023/24, UN estimate) vs USD 1,072.71 (2024, AfDB estimate)
- SDG score: 56.1 (above sub-Saharan Africa average of 53.7)
- Manufacturing value added: 15% of GDP (2024, UN estimate)
- Forex reserves: USD 4.3 billion (3.4 months import cover)

## 4. Structural Transformation

- Services overtook agriculture in 1999; economy now services-led
- Agriculture employs 65.6% of workforce despite only 24.7% of GDP
- Manufacturing: 7.5% of GDP, shallow industrialisation
- Industry employment only 7.3% despite GDP share of 24.9% (capital-intensive)
- TFP contribution less than 1pp average — growth remains input-driven
- Economic complexity: ranked 97th/133 (Harvard Growth Lab 2023, score -0.63)

## 5. Spatial Distribution

- 4 districts (Kampala, Wakiso, Mukono, Jinja) generate 50%+ of GDP with 11% of population
- Urbanisation rate: 37% (5.2% annual growth, among fastest in Africa)
- Northern Corridor (Mombasa–Nairobi–Kampala–Kigali) is primary economic axis
- Albertine Graben: emerging growth pole (USD 20bn+ oil investments)
- Regional disparities: Karamoja 74.2% poverty vs Kampala 1.1%

## 6. Priority Reform Areas (AfDB CDN Recommendations)

1. Bridge infrastructure gaps to foster private sector development
2. Modernise agriculture and promote agro-industrialisation
3. Enhance human capital and skills development
4. Strengthen governance, fiscal efficiency, and domestic resource mobilisation
5. Boost regional integration and trade competitiveness

Cross-cutting: climate change, gender equality, fragility, youth empowerment

## 7. Poverty and Inequality

- Poverty: fell from 20.3% (2019/20) to 16.1% (2023/24); longer-term trend from 21.4% (2016/17)
- Gini coefficient: improved from 0.413 to 0.382 (2023/24)
- 57% still below international poverty line
- Non-poor but economically insecure: 17.4 million people (2023/24)
- Multidimensional poverty: 44.3% (2016/17) → 42.1% (2019/20); rural 50%, urban 19.7%
- Multidimensional child poverty: 56% (2016/17) → 44% (2019/20)
- HDI: 0.582 (2023)
- Richest 10% capture 35.7% of income; poorest 10% receive 2.5%
- Urban poverty 7.6%, rural poverty 18.7%
- Female-headed households: 17.4% poverty vs 15.2% male-headed

## 8. Employment and Labour Market

- Working-age population: 23.5 million; labour force: 11.3 million
- Unemployment: 12.3% national (2024); female 14.1%, male 10.4%
- Youth unemployment (18–30): 16.5%; NEET rate (15–24): 42.6%
- 88% informal employment; 90.9% among youth
- 600,000 youth enter labour market annually vs 80,000 formal jobs created
- Private sector: ~80% of GDP, 77% of formal employment, 3.1 million employed
- MSMEs: 70%+ of GDP, 90% of jobs

## 9. Gender

- Women earn 32.3% less per month across all education levels
- 55%+ of working women in informal employment (61% in rural areas)
- Women's labour force participation: 39.3% vs 57.9% men
- 29.7 hours/week unpaid care work for women vs 13.9 hours for men
- Maternal mortality: 189 per 100,000 live births (2022), down from 336 (2016)
- 33.9% of parliamentary seats held by women (AfDB); 34% (UN 2025 estimate)
- Gender Inequality Index: 0.524 (2023)
- Global Gender Gap Index: 0.717 (2020) → 0.706 (2024) — declining trend
- Women in Cabinet: 33% (2016) → 45% (2025)
- Women in Parliament: 34% (2025)
- GBV: 23.3% of women experienced sexual violence in past year
- Key programmes: UWEP, GROW Project, National Financial Inclusion Strategy 2023–2028

## 10. Climate and Environment

- ND-GAIN 2023: ranked 172nd/191 (vulnerability 0.536, readiness 0.224)
- Temperature up 1.5°C since 1980, projected +2°C in 50 years
- Forest cover: fell from 24% (1990) to 13.3% (2022)
- NDC target: reduce GHG emissions by 24.7% by 2030
- Climate finance gap: USD 28.1 billion by 2030 (only 15% from domestic sources)
- National Climate Change Act 2021; Updated NDC (September 2022)
- National Green Taxonomy launched 2025
- Land degradation: 41% of land area affected, costing USD 2.3 billion annually
- Deforestation: 122,000 hectares annually; total forest cover loss of 1.2 million hectares (2001–2024), equivalent to 15% of year-2000 tree cover
- GHG emissions: 90.1 MtCO₂e (2015), projected 148.8 MtCO₂e (2030 BAU), mitigation scenario 112.1 MtCO₂e
- Air quality: 39 µg/m³ PM2.5 (8th most polluted country globally, 2024)
- INFORM Risk Index: ranked 16th/191 (2023) → 12th/191 (2024) — worsening risk
- Disaster-related impacts: 103 per 100,000 affected (2018); USD 150 million economic losses (2020/21)
- Climate damages could cost USD 2–4 billion annually by 2030

## 11. Governance

- Ibrahim Index of African Governance: 49.1/100 (rank 26/54, 2023)
- AfDB CPIA: overall 4.254 (2023); governance cluster 3.55 (declining)
- Economic Management cluster: 5.0 (strong)
- PEFA 2022: improved since 2016, strong in fiscal transparency and debt management
- Key PFM systems: IFMS (385 entities), HCM (175 MDAs/LGs), IRAS, e-GP
- 20 major SOEs (13 profitable, 7 loss-making in FY2023/24)
- Removed from FATF grey list February 2024

## 12. Financial Sector

- 22 commercial banks, total assets UGX 52.98 trillion (26.6% of GDP)
- Top 5 banks control 56.1% of assets (Stanbic 19.5%, Centenary 13.4%)
- Capital adequacy ratio: 24.64% (2024)
- NPL ratio: 3.98% (2024), down from 5.27% (2020)
- Mobile money: 34.6 million active users, UGX 253.7 trillion annual transactions
- USE market capitalisation: UGX 32.3 trillion (USD 8.6bn, ~4% of GDP)
- Insurance penetration: below 1%
- Lending rates: 18.8% (2024); interest rate spread: 10.9%
- Minimum bank capital: UGX 150 billion (USD 39.6 million) by June 2024

## 13. Regional Integration and Trade

- Member: EAC, COMESA, IGAD; ratified AfCFTA (2018)
- EAC market: 331 million people, USD 312.9 billion GDP
- Trade deficit: exports USD 7.12 billion vs imports USD 10.5 billion (2024)
- Top exports: gold (39.6%), coffee (20.8%), cocoa (5.8%)
- Top export destinations: UAE (33.5%), Italy (8.8%), Hong Kong (7.2%)
- Top import sources: China (17.7%), Tanzania (16.8%), India (9.2%)
- Lost AGOA preferential access January 2024
- Untapped export potential: USD 1.1 billion (coffee, dairy, gold, horticulture)

## 14. National Statistical System

- UBOS (Uganda Bureau of Statistics) coordinates the NSS
- PNSD IV (2025/26–2029/30) is current statistical development plan
- 981 National Standard Indicators (793 with at least one datapoint)
- 2024 National Population and Housing Census completed
- Key data frameworks: national accounts (2008 SNA), CPI, PPI, GFS (2014 manual)
- Challenges: limited skilled manpower, inadequate financing, data gaps in NSI

## 15. Fragility Dimensions

- Political: centralised governance, long-standing leadership since 1986
- Economic: narrow tax base, high debt, reliance on low-value agricultural exports
- Social: ethnic divisions, youth bulge risk, urban-rural service delivery gap
- Environmental: deforestation, disaster preparedness gaps, climate vulnerability
- Resilience: young population, EAC membership, oil reserves, diaspora remittances

## 16. SDG Acceleration Pathways (UN Country Analysis 2025)

Uganda's SDG score stands at 56.1, above the sub-Saharan Africa average of 53.7 but still reflecting significant ground to cover by 2030.

**Three strategic imperatives:**

1. Anchor growth in competitive value chains and productive jobs
2. Protect people and the economy from recurrent shocks through climate resilience and adaptive social protection
3. Strengthen institutions — public financial management, local government capacity, and transparent data systems

**Five high-acceleration pathways:**

1. Inclusive and sustainable industrialisation
2. Full employment and decent work
3. Climate resilience and disaster risk management
4. Universal social protection and human capital development
5. Effective, accountable, and transparent institutions

**Five enabling pathways:**

1. Sustainable natural resource management and biodiversity conservation
2. Water, sanitation, hygiene (WASH) and healthy environment
3. Digital transformation and energy access
4. Regional integration and refugee-host community solutions
5. Partnerships, financing, and data for development

**Cross-cutting principles:** Leave No One Behind, human rights and accountability, gender equality, delivery and learning, resilience and sustainability.
