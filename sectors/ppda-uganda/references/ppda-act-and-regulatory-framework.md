# PPDA Act and Regulatory Framework

Reference material for the ppda-uganda sector skill. Provides the legal and regulatory framework governing all public procurement in Uganda — institutional structures, procurement methods, preference schemes, contract types, complaints mechanisms, and compliance requirements. Use when understanding the legal basis for procurement decisions or when proposals need to demonstrate PPDA compliance.

Sources: Public Procurement and Disposal of Public Assets Act (Cap. 205, as amended through Act 15/2021 and S.I. 96/2023), PPDA Frequently Asked Questions.

---

## 1. Application and Scope

**Applies to:**
- All public finances from the Consolidated Fund and related special finances (capital and recurrent budgets)
- Counterpart transfers, co-financing, and pre-contract financing within development cooperation agreements
- Procurement by any Procuring and Disposing Entity (PDE), within or outside Uganda
- Entities not of Government that receive specific public funds (unless PPDA confirms their own system is satisfactory)
- Companies where a PDE has majority interest

**Does NOT apply to:**
- Acquisition of assets being disposed of by another PDE
- Services provided by another PDE (except fee-based services)
- Recruitment of individuals as employees

**International obligations override:** Where the Act conflicts with an international agreement, the agreement prevails. Donor conditions prevail over the Act for donor-funded procurement. Where the Financing Agreement specifies development partner regulations, those regulations apply.

**Currency point:** 1 currency point = UGX 20,000.

## 2. Key Definitions for Consultants

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Consultancy service** | A service of an intellectual or advisory nature provided by a consultant or consulting firm skilled and qualified in a particular field or profession. Includes engineering design/supervision, accountancy, auditing, financial services, procurement services, training and capacity building, management advice, policy studies, and institutional reform assistance. |
| **Consultant** | An individual providing consultancy services to a PDE |
| **Consulting firm** | A company, corporation, organisation, or partnership providing consultancy services |
| **Non-consultancy service** | A service of skilled or non-skilled nature that is NOT a consultancy service (e.g., cleaning, security, maintenance) |
| **National provider** | A provider registered in Uganda and wholly owned and controlled by Ugandans |
| **Resident provider** | A provider registered in Uganda who is not a national provider |
| **Listed provider** | A provider registered by the PPDA Authority |
| **Best evaluated bid** | The bid ascertained as best on the basis of methodology and criteria detailed in the bidding documents |
| **Emergency situation** | Circumstances that are urgent or unforeseeable and not caused by dilatory conduct |

## 3. Institutional Framework — Five Bodies per PDE

Every Procuring and Disposing Entity comprises five independent bodies:

### Accounting Officer

- Overall responsibility for execution of procurement/disposal process
- Establishes and appoints Contracts Committee
- Certifies availability of funds and price conformity
- Signs contracts (but NOT before CC approval, except emergencies)
- Cannot sign contracts where best evaluated bid price exceeds PPDA guideline prices
- Investigates complaints; submits complaint reports to PPDA

### Contracts Committee

- Chairperson + max 3 other members (one must be a lawyer, except local governments) + Secretary
- Nominated by accounting officer, approved by Secretary to Treasury
- 3-year tenure, one re-appointment
- **Functions:** Adjudicate PDU recommendations and make award decisions; approve evaluation committees and negotiation teams; approve bidding and contract documents; approve procurement procedures
- Decision within **10 working days** of PDU submission
- Head of PDU, head of finance, and internal audit staff are NOT eligible for CC membership

### Procurement and Disposal Unit (PDU)

- Manages ALL procurement/disposal activities except adjudication and contract award
- Prepares procurement plans, bid documents, advertisements, contract documents
- Maintains provider list and procurement records
- Recommends evaluation/negotiation committee composition
- Reports to CC monthly

### User Department

- Initiates procurement/disposal requirements
- Proposes technical specifications and inputs
- Provides technical evaluation input
- Manages contracts post-award (payments, conformity assessment, change orders)

### Evaluation Committee

- Minimum 3 members; number depends on value and complexity
- Recommended by PDU, approved by CC
- Members may be external to the PDE where skills unavailable internally
- Must sign Code of Ethical Conduct declaring no conflict of interest
- A member of the PDU sits on the Evaluation Committee (required by law)

**Independence:** Each body acts independently in its respective functions.

## 4. Procurement Methods

| Method | Description | Use Case |
|---|---|---|
| **Open domestic bidding** | Open to all providers through advertisement; preferred default method | Maximum competition and value for money |
| **Open international bidding** | Open to all providers, specifically seeks foreign providers | Where national providers may not achieve maximum competition |
| **Restricted domestic bidding** | Direct invitation without open advertisement | Value or circumstances do not justify open bidding |
| **Restricted international bidding** | Direct invitation including foreign providers | Foreign expertise needed; value does not justify open bidding |
| **Quotation method** | Simplified method comparing price quotations | Low value; works and supplies only |
| **Direct procurement** | Sole source | Exceptional circumstances preventing competition |
| **Micro procurement** | Very low value | Supplies/consultancy/non-consultancy below UGX 5M; works below UGX 10M |
| **Electronic reverse auction** | Competitive electronic bidding within specified time period | Lowest price within time period wins |
| **Competitive dialogue / negotiation** | For complex, specialised, strategic requirements | Cannot define technical specs upfront; requires AG and Minister approval |

**Key rules:**
- Choice of method must be approved by Contracts Committee first
- PPDA consent required for any method not listed in the Act
- Procurement must NOT be split to defeat appropriate method

## 5. Preference and Reservation Schemes

### Preference Margins

| Category | Margin Applied to Foreign Bidder's Price |
|---|---|
| **Goods** (domestically manufactured) | 15% |
| **Works or services** (Ugandan contractor/consultant) | 7% |
| **Joint ventures** (Ugandan + foreign partner) | 4% |

### Qualifying as Ugandan Consultant for Preference

- Incorporated or registered in Uganda
- If individual: Ugandan citizen
- If company: more than 50% of capital owned by Ugandan citizens
- If legal entity: more than 50% of capital owned by Government or PDE
- Must be legally and financially autonomous, established as commercial venture

### When Preference Applies

- Open domestic or open international bidding
- For consultancy services: QCBS and Least Cost selection where proposals are invited from both national and foreign consultants

### Reservation Schemes

- Promote local expertise, materials, and participation of local communities, SMEs, women, youth, persons with disabilities
- Applied to specific contracts in specified geographic areas
- Requires PPDA Authority permission

**Proposal application:** Ensure your firm's registration, ownership structure (>50% Ugandan), and incorporation documents are current to claim the 7% preference margin. This is a significant competitive advantage.

## 6. Basic Procurement Principles

1. **Non-discrimination** — no exclusion based on nationality, race, religion, gender, or non-qualification criteria
2. **Transparency, accountability and fairness**
3. **Competition** — maximise competition, achieve value for money
4. **Confidentiality** — no disclosure of evaluation/comparison before best evaluated bidder notice
5. **Economy and efficiency**
6. **Ethics** — Code of ethics specified by PPDA Authority
7. **Best evaluated bid** — contract awarded on basis of methodology and criteria in bidding documents
8. **Sustainable procurement** — environmental protection, social inclusion, stimulating innovation

---

## 7. Evaluation Rules

- Choice of evaluation methodology determined by type, value, and complexity
- Solicitation documents must **fully and comprehensively detail** evaluation methodology and criteria
- **No evaluation criteria other than those stated in the bidding documents** may be used
- Evaluation must be done expeditiously per regulations
- All evaluations conducted by Evaluation Committee (minimum 3 members)

---

## 8. Bidding Process Rules

- Annual procurement plan submitted to Secretary to Treasury and PPDA Authority each financial year
- Quarterly review and update of procurement plan
- Plan displayed on notice board for minimum **20 working days**
- **Bidding period** must be sufficient for bidders to prepare; not reduced to limit competition
- Sealed written bids required
- Bidders may modify or withdraw bids before deadline
- **No alterations to bids after closing**, including price changes
- Clarification of bids permitted to assist evaluation
- Missing historical eligibility documents may be requested by the Evaluation Committee chairperson

### Basic Qualifications of All Bidders

All bidders must demonstrate:
- Legal capacity to enter the contract
- Not insolvent, in receivership, bankrupt, or being wound up
- Business activities not suspended
- Fulfilled obligations to pay taxes and social security contributions

## 9. Contract Types

| Contract Type | When Used |
|---|---|
| **Lump sum** | Content, duration, and outputs well defined |
| **Time-based** | Scope and duration difficult to define |
| **Admeasurement** (re-measurement, unit rate, BOQ) | Works not well defined, likely to change |
| **Framework** (schedule of rates, indefinite delivery) | Repeated/continuous needs over a period |
| **Percentage-based** | Fee related to estimated or actual cost |
| **Cost reimbursable** | Emergency work; high-risk works |
| **Target price** | Alternative to cost reimbursable with incentive for savings |
| **Retainer** | Services over a period without defined levels |
| **Success fee** | Fees linked to achieved objective |

**For consulting firms:** Lump sum for well-defined assignments; time-based for open-ended engagements.

## 10. Negotiations

- Only with best evaluated bidder
- Only where best evaluated bid/proposal **exceeds the budget** of the PDE
- PDE must investigate why costs exceed budget
- May cancel process and request new proposals, OR negotiate to reduce scope/quantities
- Negotiation Committee constituted and negotiates based on parameters approved by CC

**Proposal application:** Do not expect routine price negotiations. Price negotiation happens only when the winning bid exceeds the PDE's budget. Structure your financial proposal to be competitive from the outset.

---

## 11. Contract Award Process

- Award decision is NOT a contract
- Best evaluated bidder notice must be displayed for **10 working days** (exception: emergencies)
- Award not confirmed until: (a) prescribed standstill period has lapsed, and (b) full funding committed
- Written contract signed by both provider and PDE only after conditions satisfied
- Advance payments above **30% of contract sum** require approval from the Permanent Secretary/Secretary to Treasury

## 12. Complaints and Review Mechanism — Three Tiers

### Tier 1: Administrative Review by Accounting Officer

- Complaint in writing within **10 working days** of becoming aware
- PDE must suspend procurement process immediately on receipt
- Accounting Officer decides within **10 working days** and communicates in writing with reasons
- PDE must provide rejection report on request (stating reasons and stage of rejection)

### Tier 2: PPDA Appeals Tribunal

- Application if AO decision unsatisfactory, AO fails to decide within 10 days, or AO has conflict of interest
- Application within **10 working days** of receiving AO decision
- Tribunal has powers of High Court: take evidence on oath, summon witnesses, award costs
- Must issue decision within **15 working days**
- Procurement remains suspended during Tribunal proceedings

### Tier 3: High Court Appeal

- Within **30 days** of Tribunal notification
- On **questions of law only**
- Procurement process **resumes** during High Court appeal
- High Court may hold accounting officer personally liable and award damages
- High Court decision is **final and conclusive**

---

## 13. Suspension of Providers

PPDA Authority may suspend a provider from all public procurement where:
- Provider breaches code of ethics
- Provider debarred by an international agency of which Uganda is a member
- Unsatisfactory performance record (after AG or independent investigation)
- Convicted of corrupt or fraudulent practice
- Fails to substantially perform contract obligations
- Suspended by own professional body for misconduct

Only PPDA has the power to suspend. PDEs cannot suspend providers — they can only recommend suspension to PPDA.

## 14. Offences and Penalties

| Offence | Penalty |
|---|---|
| General offences (obstruction, collusion, causing loss of public funds) | Fine UGX 5M–20M or up to 3 years imprisonment, or both |
| Accounting officers/CC/evaluation committee engaging in corrupt practices | Fine UGX 5M–20M or up to **5 years** imprisonment, or both |
| Signing contract before CC approval | Fine up to UGX 20M or up to 5 years imprisonment + refund of price difference |
| Conflict of interest violations | Fine up to UGX 2.4M or up to 5 years imprisonment, or both |

---

## 15. Conflict of Interest Rules

A PDE shall NOT enter into a contract with:
- CC members, evaluation committee members, PDE employees
- Political or administrative controllers (Ministers, accounting officers, governing body members)
- Companies where such persons have a controlling interest
- "Conflict of interest" includes the personal interest of a **relation** (child, spouse, parent) or **business associate**

---

## 16. Records Retention and Compliance

- Records retained for **7 years** from contract completion
- Records open to inspection by PPDA Authority
- PPDA compliance audit checks: PDU staffing, standard bidding documents used, procurement files maintained, reference numbers generated, monthly reports submitted, independence of roles, ethical code signed at every CC/evaluation meeting

---

## 17. Practical Tips for Consulting Firms

1. **Register on the eGP system** (egpuganda.go.ug) — the official provider database
2. **Claim the 7% preference** — ensure >50% Ugandan ownership is documented
3. **Study the bidding document evaluation criteria** — no undisclosed criteria are permitted
4. **Submit complete bids** — missing eligibility documents may be requested but do not rely on this
5. **Structure payment schedules** with advances ≤30% unless Treasury approval is anticipated
6. **Maintain bid validity and security** throughout the evaluation period; be prepared to extend
7. **Check the Financing Agreement** for donor-funded procurements — donor rules may override PPDA
8. **The 2023 Regulations are now in effect** — ensure compliance with current law (2006 Local Government regulations were revoked February 2024)
9. **Sign ethical conduct declarations** — required at every evaluation
10. **Know your complaint rights** — 10 working days to file, clear three-tier escalation path
