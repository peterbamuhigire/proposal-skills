# AfDB Procurement and Consultant Selection

Reference material for the afdb sector skill. Provides detailed procurement procedures, consultant selection methods, evaluation methodology, contract types, and registration requirements for AfDB-funded operations.

Sources: AfDB Procurement Policy for Bank Group Funded Operations (2015, revised 2023), Rules and Procedures for the Use of Consultants (2023), AfDB Standard Procurement Documents.

---

## 1. Procurement Principles

AfDB procurement is governed by five core principles:

1. **Economy and efficiency** — achieve value for money
2. **Competition** — maximise participation through fair, open processes
3. **Transparency** — clear rules applied consistently
4. **Fairness** — equal treatment of all bidders
5. **Fit for purpose** — procurement method appropriate to the context

The borrower (Executing Agency) conducts procurement; AfDB provides oversight through prior review (for larger contracts) or post review (for smaller contracts).

---

## 2. Procurement Methods for Goods, Works, and Non-Consulting Services

| Method | Threshold / Use Case | Key Features |
|---|---|---|
| **Open Competitive Bidding (OCB) — International** | Above country-specific thresholds | Advertised internationally; open to all eligible countries |
| **Open Competitive Bidding (OCB) — National** | Below international thresholds | Advertised nationally; may use national procedures if acceptable to AfDB |
| **Limited Competitive Bidding** | Specialised goods/works, limited suppliers | Direct invitation to a limited number of suppliers |
| **Request for Quotations** | Low-value procurement | Comparison of price quotations from at least 3 suppliers |
| **Direct Contracting** | Emergency, proprietary, continuation | Single source; requires AfDB prior approval |
| **Community Participation** | Community-driven development projects | Simplified procedures appropriate to community capacity |

## 3. Consultant Selection Methods — Detailed

### 3.1 Quality and Cost-Based Selection (QCBS)

The default method for most consulting assignments.

**Process:**
1. Preparation of ToR and cost estimate
2. Publication of Expression of Interest (EoI) on AfDB portal and UNDB online
3. Receipt and evaluation of EoIs; preparation of shortlist (typically 3-6 firms)
4. Issuance of Request for Proposals (RFP) to shortlisted firms
5. Receipt of proposals in two sealed envelopes
6. Opening and evaluation of technical proposals
7. Public opening of financial proposals (firms above technical threshold only)
8. Combined evaluation and ranking
9. Negotiation with first-ranked firm
10. Contract award

**Shortlisting rules:**
- Minimum 3 firms, maximum 6
- No more than 2 firms from the same country (unless insufficient qualified firms from other countries)
- At least 1 firm from the region (Africa) where qualified firms exist
- AfDB encourages inclusion of firms from RMCs
- Shortlist must include only firms, not individuals (for firm-based assignments)

**Technical evaluation scoring:**
- Evaluators score independently; arithmetic average used
- Sub-criteria must be disclosed in the RFP
- No undisclosed criteria permitted
- Strengths and weaknesses documented for each proposal

**Financial evaluation:**
- Financial proposals of firms below technical threshold returned unopened
- All prices converted to a single currency at the exchange rate specified in the RFP
- Arithmetical errors corrected
- Financial Score = (Lowest Price / Your Price) x 100
- Combined Score = (Tech Score x Tech Weight%) + (Fin Score x Fin Weight%)

**Typical weightings:**
- Technical 70-80%, Financial 20-30%
- Weights specified in the RFP Data Sheet
- Technical weight must not exceed 80% or fall below 70%

### 3.2 Quality-Based Selection (QBS)

Used for highly complex or specialised assignments where the scope is difficult to define precisely.

**Key differences from QCBS:**
- Only the highest-scoring technical proposal's financial envelope is opened
- No financial scoring or weighting — quality alone determines selection
- Financial proposal is the basis for negotiations, not comparison
- Appropriate for innovation, policy/strategy advisory, complex institutional reform

### 3.3 Fixed Budget Selection (FBS)

Used when the budget is fixed and the scope well-defined.

**Key features:**
- Budget disclosed in the RFP
- Proposals exceeding the budget are rejected outright
- Among proposals within budget, highest technical score wins
- Financial proposals are opened but only to confirm they fall within budget

### 3.4 Least Cost Selection (LCS)

Used for routine, standardised assignments (standard audits, routine engineering supervision).

**Key features:**
- All proposals above the minimum technical qualifying mark ranked by cost
- Lowest cost among technically qualified firms wins
- Technical quality must meet minimum standard, not maximised

### 3.5 Consultant's Qualifications Selection (CQS)

Used for small assignments below the monetary threshold.

**Key features:**
- EoIs evaluated; most qualified firm invited to submit technical and financial proposal
- No competition on proposal — selection based on qualifications
- Negotiation of technical and financial terms

### 3.6 Single Source Selection (SSS)

Non-competitive selection; requires AfDB prior approval.

**Justifications accepted:**
- Only one firm has the required qualifications or experience
- Continuity of services (follow-on assignment with same firm)
- Emergency situations
- Very small assignments below micro-threshold
- When the assignment is a natural continuation of previous Bank-funded work

## 4. Expression of Interest (EoI) Requirements

When responding to AfDB EoI requests:

- **Firm profile**: legal entity, year established, ownership, countries of operation
- **Relevant experience**: projects of similar nature, scope, and complexity in the last 5-10 years
- **Technical capacity**: key staff qualifications (names not always required at EoI stage)
- **Geographical experience**: experience in the target country or region
- **Joint ventures / associations**: clearly state lead firm, division of responsibilities
- **Financial capacity**: annual turnover, financial statements if required

**Scoring at shortlisting:**
- General experience of the firm: 20-30%
- Specific relevant experience: 40-50%
- Staff qualifications (if required): 20-30%
- Local/regional knowledge: 10-20%

## 5. Request for Proposals (RFP) Structure

AfDB RFPs follow a standard structure:

1. **Letter of Invitation** — assignment description, submission details
2. **Instructions to Consultants (ITC)** — general terms, evaluation methodology, conflict of interest rules
3. **Data Sheet** — assignment-specific parameters (weights, minimum scores, currency, submission requirements)
4. **Terms of Reference** — scope, objectives, tasks, deliverables, timeline
5. **Standard Forms** — technical proposal forms, financial proposal forms, contract forms
6. **Contract Conditions** — General Conditions and Special Conditions of Contract

**Critical Data Sheet items to check:**
- Technical evaluation weights per criterion
- Minimum qualifying technical score
- Technical/financial weight split
- Currency for price comparison
- Maximum number of pages (if specified)
- Expected start date and duration
- Source of funding (ADB/ADF/NTF/co-financing)

## 6. Contract Types

| Contract Type | When Used | Risk Allocation |
|---|---|---|
| **Lump sum** | Well-defined scope, clear deliverables, predictable level of effort | Risk on consultant — fixed price regardless of actual effort |
| **Time-based** | Scope difficult to define, duration uncertain | Risk shared — consultant paid for actual time spent |
| **Percentage-based** | Fee related to estimated or actual project cost (design/supervision) | Risk shared — fee scales with project size |
| **Retainer** | Ongoing advisory services over a period | Risk on client — minimum fee regardless of usage |
| **Success fee** | Fee contingent on achieving defined outcomes | Risk on consultant — no outcome, no fee (or reduced fee) |

**Default for consulting firms:** Lump sum for well-defined assignments; time-based for open-ended engagements. AfDB prefers lump sum where feasible.

## 7. Negotiation Procedures

After selection, the Executing Agency negotiates with the first-ranked firm:

**Technical negotiations:**
- Clarify methodology, work plan, and staffing
- Agree on deliverables and milestones
- Confirm key staff availability (substitution only with equivalent or better qualifications)
- Agree on ToR modifications if needed

**Financial negotiations:**
- For QCBS: financial proposal is the basis; no major reductions expected
- For QBS: financial proposal negotiated more extensively since it was not competitively evaluated
- Unit rates and reimbursable expenses negotiated
- Tax treatment clarified (consultants typically exempt from taxes on Bank-funded contracts, but varies by country agreement)
- Payment schedule agreed

**If negotiations fail:** The Executing Agency notifies the first-ranked firm, invites the second-ranked firm to negotiate, and so on.

## 8. Procurement Notices and Registration

### AfDB Procurement Portal

All procurement notices are published on the AfDB website:
- General Procurement Notices (GPNs) — published when the project is approved
- Specific Procurement Notices (SPNs) — published for individual contracts
- Expressions of Interest — published for consulting assignments

### UNDB Online

Major consulting opportunities are also published on the United Nations Development Business website.

### Registration

- Firms should register on the AfDB procurement portal
- Registration is free
- No automatic advantage from registration, but ensures receipt of notices
- AfDB also maintains a database of African consulting firms

## 9. Prior Review and Post Review

| Review Type | When Applied | What AfDB Reviews |
|---|---|---|
| **Prior review** | Above defined thresholds | TOR, shortlist, RFP, evaluation report, negotiations, draft contract — all before proceeding |
| **Post review** | Below thresholds | AfDB reviews documentation after contract award on a sample basis |

Prior review thresholds are set in the Procurement Plan and approved during project preparation.

**Implication for consultants:** Prior review contracts take longer (AfDB review adds 2-4 weeks per step). Build this into timeline expectations.

---

## 10. Complaint and Protest Mechanism

Bidders who believe the procurement process was conducted improperly may:

1. **Complain to the Executing Agency** — first step, allows correction
2. **Escalate to AfDB** — if the EA does not address the concern
3. **Independent Review Mechanism (IRM)** — AfDB's accountability mechanism for affected communities and stakeholders
4. **Sanctions Procedures** — for fraud, corruption, or other integrity violations, handled by the Office of Integrity and Anti-Corruption (PIAC)

---

## 11. Eligible Countries and Firms

**Eligibility:**
- Firms from all AfDB member countries (54 RMCs + 28 non-regional members) are eligible
- Firms from non-member countries are NOT eligible
- Individuals from member countries are eligible
- Goods must be manufactured in member countries (for goods procurement)

**Ineligibility:**
- Firms debarred by AfDB or cross-debarred by other MDBs
- Firms in countries under UN sanctions
- Firms with conflicts of interest
- Government-owned entities (unless commercially operated and legally/financially autonomous)

## 12. Association of Consultants (Joint Ventures)

AfDB encourages associations between international and African firms:

**Types:**
- **Joint venture** — firms jointly and severally liable
- **Sub-consultancy** — lead firm contracts the sub-consultant
- **Association** — formal agreement between firms

**Requirements:**
- Clear lead firm identified
- Division of responsibilities clearly stated
- All members meet eligibility requirements
- Association agreement submitted with proposal
- Key staff from any member firm may be proposed

**Strategic advantage:** Associating with a qualified African firm strengthens shortlisting chances and demonstrates commitment to capacity building and knowledge transfer.
