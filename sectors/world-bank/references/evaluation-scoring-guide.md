# World Bank Evaluation Scoring Guide

How World Bank evaluators actually score consulting proposals. Use this to write every section with scoring awareness — know what earns a 2 (meets) vs. a 4 (significantly exceeds).

---

## The 0–4 Scoring Scale

| Score | Label | What the Evaluator Sees | Proposal Quality |
|---|---|---|---|
| **0** | Feature absent | No relevant information on this criterion | Criterion not addressed at all |
| **1** | Present but deficient | Information provided but insufficient, unclear, or not credible | Generic, vague, or boilerplate response |
| **2** | Meets requirements | Sufficient information demonstrating the requirement will be met | Competent, responsive, but not distinctive |
| **3** | Marginally exceeds | Sufficient information showing the requirement will be marginally exceeded | Tailored, specific, shows deeper understanding |
| **4** | Significantly exceeds | Information significantly exceeds requirements and/or contributes significant value addition | Exceptional — offers genuine added value beyond the ToR |

### What Moves a Score from 2 to 3–4

| Score 2 (Meets) | Score 3–4 (Exceeds) |
|---|---|
| Restates the ToR objectives | Names the client's problem more precisely than the ToR does |
| Describes a standard methodology | Proposes a named, structured framework with clear rationale for why it fits THIS assignment |
| Lists team qualifications | Shows how each person's specific experience directly maps to specific ToR tasks |
| Mentions risks generically | Identifies risks the ToR did not mention and proposes credible, specific mitigations |
| States "we will conduct stakeholder consultations" | Specifies who, how many, by what method, in which locations, and how findings feed into which deliverable |
| Provides a Gantt chart | Provides a Gantt chart with critical path, dependencies, milestones, and client review points clearly marked |

## Comparative Scoring Methodology

Evaluators do not score firms in isolation — scores are comparative.

### Technical Score Calculation

1. Each evaluator scores independently on the 0–4 scale
2. Panel moderation produces a consensus score per criterion
3. Score × weight = weighted score per criterion
4. Sum of weighted scores = total technical score

### Converting to Comparative Technical Score

The highest-scoring firm sets the ceiling at 100:

```
Comparative Technical Score = (Firm's Total Score / Highest Total Score) × 100
Final Weighted Technical Score = Comparative Score × Technical Weighting %
```

**Example (Technical weight 80%):**

| Firm | Raw Score | Comparative | Weighted (80%) |
|---|---|---|---|
| A (highest) | 280 | 100.0 | 80.00 |
| B | 240 | 85.7 | 68.57 |
| C | 210 | 75.0 | 60.00 |
| D | 190 | 67.9 | 54.29 |

### Financial Score Calculation

The lowest-priced qualifying firm sets the ceiling at 100:

```
Comparative Financial Score = (Lowest Price / Firm's Price) × 100
Final Weighted Financial Score = Comparative Score × Financial Weighting %
```

**Example (Financial weight 20%):**

| Firm | Price ($) | Comparative | Weighted (20%) |
|---|---|---|---|
| C (lowest) | 380,000 | 100.0 | 20.00 |
| B | 420,000 | 90.5 | 18.10 |
| D | 450,000 | 84.4 | 16.89 |
| A | 480,000 | 79.2 | 15.83 |

### Combined Score

| Firm | Tech (80%) | Financial (20%) | Combined | Rank |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **A** | 80.00 | 15.83 | **95.83** | **#1** |
| B | 68.57 | 18.10 | 86.67 | #2 |
| C | 60.00 | 20.00 | 80.00 | #3 |
| D | 54.29 | 16.89 | 71.18 | #4 |

**Firm A wins despite having the highest price** because its technical superiority (80.00 vs. 60.00) more than compensates for its financial disadvantage (15.83 vs. 20.00). The 20-point technical gap dwarfs the 4-point financial gap.

## Scoring Strategy by Criterion

### Firm Experience (TECH-2B) — Typically 10–20 Points

**Score 1–2:** Lists many projects but they are only tangentially related to the ToR. Generic descriptions.

**Score 3–4:**
- Projects are highly analogous to THIS assignment (same type, sector, complexity, geographic context)
- Each project card states the quantified outcome, not just what was done
- Client references are named with verifiable contact details
- Recent projects (last 5–7 years) are prioritised
- Demonstrates pattern of repeat clients (signals quality)

### Comments on ToR (TECH-3) — Embedded in Methodology Score

**Score 1–2:** "We have reviewed the ToR and agree with its contents." or minor formatting suggestions.

**Score 3–4:**
- Identifies specific technical gaps in the ToR (e.g., "The ToR does not specify a baseline data collection methodology; we recommend...")
- Proposes well-reasoned adjustments to scope, sequencing, or deliverables
- Identifies unrealistic timelines and suggests alternatives with justification
- Comments on counterpart staff requirements are specific and realistic
- Each comment structured as: ToR Reference → Observation → Recommendation

### Methodology (TECH-4) — Typically 30–50 Points (The Decisive Section)

**Score 1–2:** Generic description of phases. Could apply to any assignment. "We will review documents, conduct interviews, analyse data, and prepare a report."

**Score 3–4:**
- **Conceptual framework** named and justified — not just "our approach" but a specific analytical framework with a rationale for why it fits this assignment
- **Each phase uses P-I-P structure:** opens with WHY this approach (persuasion), describes WHAT specifically will be done (information), closes with WHAT it produces (deliverables and value)
- **Tools and techniques named specifically** — not "we will conduct analysis" but "we will apply a maturity model assessment across the five dimensions of [specific framework]"
- **Hypothesis stated** — "Our initial assessment suggests [X]; Phase 1 will test this through [method]"
- **Risks identified beyond the ToR** — the proposal anticipates risks the client did not mention
- **Innovation offered** — better ways to achieve objectives, value-added deliverables, efficiency gains
- **Logical flow** — each phase's outputs feed clearly into the next phase's inputs
- **Deliverables are specific** — named, formatted, with review/approval points

### Key Personnel (TECH-5/6) — Typically 30–60 Points

**Score 1–2:** CVs that show general sector experience but no direct match to the specific ToR tasks the person is assigned to. Junior staff on complex tasks.

**Score 3–4:**
- **CV-to-task match:** Each person's listed experience directly corresponds to the ToR tasks they are assigned. The evaluator should be able to draw a line from the task description to a specific project in the CV.
- **Senior staff on complex tasks:** The Team Leader and key technical positions show 15+ years relevant experience
- **Regional experience explicit:** Named projects in the same country or East Africa, with outcomes stated
- **Qualifications exceed minimums:** If the ToR asks for a Master's degree, the proposed expert has a PhD and relevant certifications
- **Availability confirmed:** CV states the person is available for the required period
- **Complementary skills:** The team covers all ToR disciplines without gaps

### Work Plan (TECH-7/8) — Embedded in Methodology Score

**Score 1–2:** A basic Gantt chart with generic phase names and uniform durations.

**Score 3–4:**
- **Critical path identified** — dependencies between tasks are clear
- **Client interaction points marked** — review meetings, approval gates, workshop dates
- **Staffing schedule consistent** with methodology and financial proposal
- **Milestones tied to deliverables** — each milestone produces a named output
- **Realistic timing** — allows for client review periods, holiday calendars, fieldwork logistics
- **Consistency test passes** — the work plan, methodology, staffing schedule, and financial proposal all tell the same story

## Minimum Quality Thresholds

Three types of threshold may apply:

| Threshold Type | What It Means | Risk |
|---|---|---|
| **Total score threshold** (e.g., 75/100) | Score below this = eliminated entirely | The survival line — must pass |
| **Selected criteria threshold** | Combined score on chosen criteria must exceed a floor | Weak performance on key criteria = eliminated even if overall score is acceptable |
| **Individual criterion threshold** | A single criterion must score above a floor | Ignoring any criterion, even low-weighted, risks elimination |

**Rule:** Never leave any criterion unaddressed. Even a 5-point criterion can have a sub-threshold that eliminates you.

## Evaluation Committee Process

Understanding how evaluators work helps write better proposals:

1. **Independent scoring first** — each evaluator reads and scores alone
2. **Moderation** — panel discusses, reconciles, agrees final scores
3. **Documentation** — every score must be justified in writing
4. **Evidence trail** — the Bid Evaluation Report must detail strengths and weaknesses of each proposal

**Implication:** Proposals must be self-evidently strong. Evaluators cannot award points for things they have to infer or search for. If it is not clearly stated in the proposal, it will not be scored. Make the evaluator's job easy — organise content to match the evaluation criteria exactly.

---

## Abnormally Low Proposals

A financial proposal that appears too low to be credible will be:
1. Flagged as potentially Abnormally Low
2. Examined — the firm is asked for written clarification
3. Rejected if the firm cannot demonstrate capability to deliver at the offered price

**Implication:** Do not underprice to win the financial score. A rejected proposal scores zero.

---

## Post-Award: Standstill Period and Debriefs

After the Notification of Intention to Award:
- **10 business day standstill period** — no contract signing
- Unsuccessful firms may request debriefs within 3 business days
- Borrower must provide debriefs within 5 business days
- Debriefs explain how the firm scored on each criterion

**Implication:** Evaluators know they will have to defend their scores. Vague proposals receive vague, low scores that are hard to challenge but equally hard for the evaluator to justify awarding high marks. Specific, evidence-backed proposals make it easy for evaluators to justify high scores.
