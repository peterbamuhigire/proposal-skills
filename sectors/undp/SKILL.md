# United Nations System Procurement Framework — Consulting Services and Grants

## Use When
- Use this skill when the solicitation follows UNDP or wider UN-system procurement rules.
- Load it before drafting when you need form, scoring, threshold, or compliance guidance.

## Do Not Use When
- The task only needs sector-domain framing without procurement mechanics.
- A different procurement framework clearly applies.

## Required Inputs
- The solicitation package, especially instructions, forms, and evaluation criteria.
- Any known selection method and technical threshold.
- The target proposal sections that must comply with the framework.

## Workflow
1. Confirm that UNDP or UN-system procurement applies from explicit cues in the solicitation.
2. Read this skill and the local references to understand forms, scoring, administrative compliance, and constraints.
3. Map those requirements into the numbered proposal section skills before drafting.
4. Draft to maximize technical quality without creating compliance risk.
5. Check the final output against thresholds, form placement, and submission rules.

## Quality Standards
- Treat UN instructions as hard constraints, not optional style guidance.
- Keep terminology, form names, and evaluation assumptions aligned to the actual solicitation.
- Optimize for evaluator behavior where the framework supports it.

## Anti-Patterns
- Do not rely on generic donor assumptions when the UN materials give exact instructions.
- Do not ignore administrative compliance gates, thresholds, or mandatory declarations.
- Do not mix UN assumptions with World Bank, AfDB, or PPDA rules.

## Outputs
- A UNDP- or UN-compliant drafting and scoring approach for the relevant proposal sections.

## References
- Local `references/` files for UN procurement variants, scoring logic, and programme-specific requirements.
- `../SKILL.md` to combine framework and sector context.

This skill covers procurement across the entire UN system: UNDP, UNICEF, UNFCCC, UNEP, UN Women, FAO, WHO, UNIDO, ILO, and all other UN bodies. While each agency has its own procedures, they share common patterns in proposal structure, evaluation, and compliance requirements. Read this before writing any UN-funded proposal.

## When to Read This Skill

- The ToR or Call for Proposals references any UN agency (UNDP, UNICEF, UNFCCC, UNEP, FAO, WHO, etc.)
- The assignment is funded through GEF Small Grants Programme (SGP)
- The RFP uses UN project document format or Annex-based templates
- The proposal is a grant application to a UN-managed fund
- The assignment is funded by a bilateral donor channelled through a UN agency (EU, DFID, SIDA, etc.)
- You need to understand how UN evaluators score differently from World Bank
- The RFP is a competitive consulting services procurement issued by a UN secretariat or agency

## Key Difference from World Bank

| Dimension | World Bank | UN System |
|---|---|---|
| **Standardisation** | Single procurement framework, universal forms (TECH/FIN) | Multiple instruments varying by agency, programme, country office, and funding source |
| **Primary instruments** | Consulting services contracts (QCBS, QBS, etc.) | Grants to CSOs, project documents, consulting contracts, institutional contracts, RFPs for services |
| **Two-envelope system** | Mandatory — sealed technical and financial envelopes | Grants: single submission. Consulting RFPs: separate technical and financial files |
| **Minimum technical score** | Hard gate (typically 75/100) | Grants: administrative verification is the pass/fail gate. RFPs: varies by agency |
| **Scoring scale** | 0-4 per sub-criterion, weighted to 100 | Varies: 1000-point scales with tier bands, qualitative expert appraisal, NC/NSC committee review, or cumulative point scoring |
| **Price competition** | Formula: (Lowest Price / Your Price) x 100 x Weight | Grants: budget assessed for "economy and relevance". RFPs: varies by agency |
| **Key personnel** | Individual CVs scored per position (qualifications, adequacy, regional experience) | Grants: team evaluated as a whole. RFPs: individual CVs with standardised UN CV template |
| **Eligible entities** | Primarily consulting firms | CSOs, NGOs, CBOs, IPOs, community groups, academic institutions, consulting firms |
| **Co-financing** | Not typically required from bidders | Grants: strongly encouraged or required. RFPs: not applicable |
| **Gender/inclusion** | Cross-cutting but rarely a scored criterion | Often a standalone scored criterion with significant weight |
| **Government endorsement** | Not required from bidders | Required for NGO applicants to UN Trust Funds |
| **IPR ownership** | Varies by contract | Defaults to the UN body — secretariat/agency holds exclusive IPR on all work produced |
| **Tax exemption** | Client-side | UN is exempt from all taxes and VAT — all prices must be quoted exclusive of tax |

## UN System Instrument Types

The UN system uses different procurement modalities depending on the agency, programme, and funding source. Each has its own templates, evaluation approach, and strategic requirements.

### Modality 1: Competitive Grants (UNDP-Managed Re-Granting)

**When used:** UNDP acts as fund manager for a donor (EU, bilateral) and re-grants to CSOs through competitive calls.

**Typical features:**
- Open call to all eligible organisations
- Annex-based application package (proposal + budget + declarations)
- Scored evaluation by Local and/or Regional Selection Committee
- Single submission — no two-envelope system
- Budget is one scored criterion among several (typically 10%)
- Grant agreements (Low Value Grant Agreement or similar)

**Evaluation approach:** Numerical scoring against published criteria, typically on a 1000-point scale with three-tier bands (somewhat/mostly/fully correspond).

**See:** `references/competitive-grants-guide.md`

### Modality 2: GEF Small Grants Programme (SGP)

**When used:** GEF-funded grants to CSOs and community-based organisations for environmental and sustainable development projects, administered by UNDP globally.

**Typical features:**
- Standard project proposal template (10-page limit)
- Reviewed by National Coordinator (NC) and National Steering Committee (NSC)
- Dual objectives required: environmental AND development/livelihood
- Co-financing strongly encouraged
- Mandatory indicators framework (global environmental + socio-economic)
- Projects generally capped at 2 years

**Evaluation approach:** Committee-based qualitative assessment by NC and NSC against alignment with SGP Country Programme Strategy, national priorities, and template quality.

**See:** `references/gef-sgp-guide.md`

### Modality 3: UN Trust Fund Project Proposals

**When used:** Project proposals to UN Trust Funds administered through UNDP channels (Fund for Ageing, Voluntary Fund on Disability, Trust Fund for Family Activities, Youth Fund).

**Typical features:**
- Five-part project document pro forma
- Government concurrence mandatory for NGO applicants
- Seed money orientation (US$5,000-20,000) — fund supplements, not replaces
- Three-party signature required (Government, Executing Agent, UN)
- Prescribed budget line numbering system
- Completion evaluation mandatory

**Evaluation approach:** Qualitative appraisal by interdisciplinary team of UN specialists against fund mandate, national strategy alignment, and design logic.

**See:** `references/un-trust-funds-guide.md`

### Modality 4: UN Consulting Services RFPs (UNICEF, UNFCCC, and Other Agencies)

**When used:** A UN agency issues a competitive RFP to procure consulting firms, IT service providers, or individual consultants for implementation, evaluation, system development, or advisory services.

**Typical features:**
- Annex-based RFP package (multiple annexes covering terms, requirements, templates)
- Technical and financial proposals submitted as **separate files** (not sealed envelopes, but distinct documents)
- Standardised technical proposal template provided by the agency (varies by agency)
- UN CV template for key personnel
- IPR defaults to the UN body — secretariat/agency holds exclusive rights on all work produced
- All prices quoted exclusive of taxes and VAT (UN exemption)
- Staged payments tied to deliverables (not time-based billing)
- Third-party reviews may be mandated (project management and technical quality)

**Common technical proposal structure (synthesised from UNICEF and UNFCCC patterns):**

| Section | Content | Maps to Skill |
|---|---|---|
| **Value statement** | Half-page statement on why the firm is most suitable | `01-cover-letter/` |
| **General information** | Organisation name, registration, contact details | `04-firm-profile/` |
| **Organisational capability** | Size, project management strength, financial stability, global networking | `04-firm-profile/` |
| **Past and present experience** | Relevant projects, collaborative efforts, regional experience | `05-relevant-experience/` |
| **Specialised knowledge** | Domain expertise relevant to the ToR | `03-understanding-of-assignment/` |
| **Engagement model / approach** | How the firm will organise and partner with the UN agency | `06-methodology/` |
| **Management approach** | Project management, risk management, progress reporting, change management | `06-methodology/` |
| **Quality approach** | Quality environment, certifications, formal methodologies | `06-methodology/` |
| **Technical solution** | Detailed technical response to requirements (varies by assignment) | `06-methodology/` |
| **Key personnel and CVs** | Team structure, supervisory relationships, individual CVs | `07-team-composition/` |
| **Project plan and schedule** | Milestones, deliverables, critical path, dependencies | `08-work-plan/` |
| **Previous experience** | Track record in analogous assignments | `05-relevant-experience/` |
| **References** | Minimum 3 at organisation level + 3 per CV | `05-relevant-experience/` |

**Evaluation approach:** Cumulative point scoring (technical + financial), with technical and financial proposals evaluated separately. Technical weight typically 60-70%, financial 30-40%.

**See:** `references/un-consulting-rfp-guide.md`

### Modality 5: UNDP Direct Consulting Procurement

**When used:** UNDP procures consulting firms or individual consultants directly for implementation support, evaluations, assessments.

**Typical features:** Similar to World Bank but with UNDP-specific templates and procedures. Uses UNDP Procurement Manual (not World Bank SPDs). Individual consultant procurement uses a different process from firm procurement.

## UN System-Wide Rules

These rules apply across all UN agencies and instruments:

| Rule | Implication |
|---|---|
| **Tax exemption** | All prices must exclude taxes and VAT. The UN is exempt. Including VAT signals unfamiliarity. |
| **IPR ownership** | All work product, data, and deliverables belong to the UN body. Proposals must not claim IP rights over outputs. |
| **Adverse judgments disclosure** | Mandatory disclosure of any adverse legal judgments or arbitration awards. Omission can disqualify. |
| **UN sanctions** | Firms/individuals on UN sanctions lists are ineligible. |
| **Conflict of interest** | Standard COI declarations required. Firms involved in preparing the ToR may be excluded. |
| **Currency** | Financial proposals in the currency specified in the RFP. Some calls specify local currency. |
| **UN experience valued** | Experience with UN organisations and major multilateral/bilateral programmes is explicitly "highly desirable" across agencies. Prioritise UN project references. |

## Evaluation Criteria — Cross-Modality Patterns

Despite variation across agencies and instruments, UN evaluations consistently weight these areas:

### Universal Scoring Areas

| Area | Typical Weight | What Evaluators Look For |
|---|---|---|
| **Methodology and approach** | 40-50% | Tailored strategy, clear activities, logical results chain, realistic implementation plan |
| **Organisational experience** | 20-30% | Years of relevant project management, number of analogous past projects, team qualifications |
| **Gender and social inclusion** | 5-15% | Standalone criterion in most calls. Must be woven throughout, not an afterthought |
| **Sustainability** | 5-10% | Post-project viability, institutional embedding, self-financing provisions |
| **Budget economy** | 5-10% | Realistic, efficient, justified costs. Administrative costs typically capped at 15-20% |
| **Innovation** | 5-10% | New approaches, methods, or delivery models. Catalytic potential. |
| **Risk management** | 3-5% | Identified risks with specific mitigations. Many applicants skip this — easy differentiator |

### The Three-Tier Scoring Band (Competitive Grants)

UNDP competitive grants typically score on three-tier bands rather than the World Bank's 0-4 scale:

| Band | Label | Score Range | What It Means |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Band 1** | Somewhat corresponds | 30-40% of max | Criterion addressed but insufficiently |
| **Band 2** | Mostly corresponds | 60-75% of max | Criterion adequately addressed |
| **Band 3** | Fully corresponds | 100% of max | Criterion comprehensively addressed |

**Critical insight:** There is no partial credit within bands. The jump from Band 1 to Band 2 is massive (often doubling the score). Write to clearly cross the Band 3 threshold on every criterion.

## Scoring Strategy by Section

### Methodology (40-50% — The Decisive Section)

**Band 1 (Somewhat):** Restates the call objectives. Generic activities. Could apply to any project.

**Band 3 (Fully):**
- Strategy and approach explicitly linked to the call's thematic priorities
- Activities logically produce the stated results — clear results chain
- Implementation plan with timeline, responsibilities, and milestones
- Risk register with specific mitigations (not generic "we will manage risks")
- Monitoring plan integrated into implementation

### Gender and Social Inclusion (Standalone Criterion)

**Band 1:** "The project will ensure gender equality" — vague language, no specifics.

**Band 3:**
- Gender analysis informs the project design
- Specific activities targeting women, youth, persons with disabilities
- Sex-disaggregated indicators and targets
- Budget line items for gender-specific activities
- Results framework includes gender-specific outcomes

**In GEF SGP:** At least 30% of funded projects must be implemented by indigenous women and youth — proposals led by these groups have a structural advantage.

### Organisational Experience

**Low score:** General description of the organisation. No specific project references.

**High score:**
- Named past projects with dates, values, and outcomes
- Years of experience quantified (5+ years scores maximum in most rubrics)
- Team members identified with contracts or CVs attached
- Partnership history documented (long-standing partnerships score higher)
- Previous engagement with the funding programme noted

### Sustainability

**Band 1:** "The project results will be sustained after completion."

**Band 3:**
- Specific institutional arrangements for post-project continuation
- Exit strategy with handover milestones
- Self-financing provisions or alternative funding sources identified
- Capacity built in local institutions to maintain results
- Government commitment to continuation (letter or endorsement)

### Budget

**Band 1:** Unrealistic costs, unexplained line items, administrative costs exceeding cap.

**Band 3:**
- Every line item justified and realistic
- Administrative costs well within the cap (typically 15-20%)
- Co-financing clearly documented (source, type, amount, committed vs. projected)
- Annual breakdown aligned with the work plan
- VAT excluded where UNDP exemption applies

## Administrative Compliance — The Pass/Fail Gate

Unlike World Bank (where the minimum technical score is the survival line), UNDP grants use administrative verification as the hard gate. **Failing administrative verification means instant disqualification — no amount of proposal quality can recover.**

### Common Disqualification Triggers

| Requirement | Risk |
|---|---|
| **Incomplete documentation** | Missing annexes, declarations, or registration certificates = disqualified |
| **Incorrect entity type** | Organisation not eligible under the call criteria = disqualified |
| **Missing mandatory documents** | Registration certificate, financial reports, tax compliance certificate = disqualified |
| **Budget format errors** | VAT included when UNDP is exempt, administrative costs exceeding cap = disqualified or scored down |
| **Late submission** | No grace period in most calls |
| **Special eligibility documents** | E.g., GEF SGP indigenous recognition document — missing = instant disqualification |

**Rule:** Complete the administrative checklist before investing time in proposal content. If any document cannot be obtained, the proposal may not be worth writing.

## Co-Financing — A UNDP Differentiator

Unlike World Bank consulting procurements (where the client pays the consultant), UNDP grants expect the applicant to contribute resources:

| Type | Description | How to Present |
|---|---|---|
| **Cash co-financing** | Direct monetary contribution from the applicant or third parties | State source, amount, committed vs. projected |
| **In-kind co-financing** | Staff time, office space, equipment, volunteer labour | Quantify in monetary terms at market rates |
| **Government contribution** | Government inputs in cash or kind | Secure written commitment before submission |
| **Third-party co-financing** | Contributions from other donors or partners | Attach commitment letters where possible |

**Strategic insight:** Co-financing signals commitment and sustainability. A proposal requesting 100% funding from UNDP is weaker than one showing 30-50% co-financing. In UN Trust Funds, the explicit instruction is that funds provide "seed money" — proposals requesting full funding are disadvantaged.

---

## Government Endorsement

For UN Trust Fund proposals and many UNDP country programme grants, NGO applicants must demonstrate government concurrence:

- Contact the relevant government ministry early in the proposal development process
- Obtain a letter of endorsement or no-objection
- The local UNDP office can advise on procedures and the appropriate government counterpart
- Without government concurrence, the proposal cannot be processed

**This does not apply to all UNDP instruments** — competitive CSO grants may not require it. Check the specific call requirements.

## Page Limits and Formatting

UNDP grants often impose strict page limits (unlike World Bank, which typically does not):

| Instrument | Typical Limit |
|---|---|
| GEF SGP project proposal | 10 pages + 10 pages attachments |
| Competitive CSO grants | Varies by call — check the guidelines |
| UN Trust Fund proposals | No explicit limit, but seed-money scale implies conciseness |

**Implication:** Conciseness is essential. Use tables, bullet points, and structured formats. Every sentence must earn its place.
