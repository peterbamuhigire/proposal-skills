# UN Consulting Services RFP Guide

Detailed guide for responding to competitive Request for Proposals (RFPs) issued by UN agencies for consulting services, IT services, technical advisory, and professional services. Synthesised from UNICEF and UNFCCC procurement patterns, with principles applicable across all UN agencies.

---

## How UN Consulting RFPs Differ from UN Grants

| Dimension | UN Grants (Modalities 1-3) | UN Consulting RFPs (This Guide) |
|---|---|---|
| **Applicant** | CSOs, NGOs, CBOs | Consulting firms, IT service providers, individual consultants |
| **Instrument** | Grant agreement | Services contract |
| **Proposal structure** | Project document / proposal template | Technical proposal + separate financial proposal |
| **Budget** | Embedded in the proposal | Separate sealed/file financial proposal |
| **Evaluation** | Three-tier bands or qualitative appraisal | Cumulative point scoring (technical + financial weight) |
| **Co-financing** | Expected from applicant | Not applicable — the UN pays for services |
| **IPR** | Varies | Defaults to the UN body |
| **Deliverables** | Project results and reports | Service deliverables tied to staged payments |

## Technical Proposal Structure

UN consulting RFPs typically provide an Annex-based template. While the exact structure varies by agency, the following sections appear consistently:

### Section 1: General Information and Value Statement

**What to include:**
- Organisation name, country of registration, legal entity type
- Contact person name, address, phone, email
- **Value statement** (typically half a page): why the firm is the most suitable to carry out the assignment

**Strategy for the value statement:**
- Mirror the RFP's own language — if they say "most suitable," use that phrase
- Lead with the client's problem, not your credentials
- Reference the specific assignment, not generic capabilities
- Keep to the prescribed length (half a page means half a page)

### Section 2: Organisational Capability and Experience

Five standard sub-sections:

#### 2.1 General Organisational Capability
- Size of the organisation (staff count, offices)
- Strength of project management support (controls, methodologies)
- Global networking capability
- Financial stability

#### 2.2 Past and Present Experience
- Projects with direct relationship to the ToR
- Collaborative efforts (consortia, JVs, partnerships)
- **Quantified outcomes for every project** (the outcomes rule applies here too)

#### 2.3 Specialised Knowledge
- Domain expertise relevant to this specific ToR
- **Regional experience** — include experiences in the target region
- Experience performing similar services for similar clients
- **UN experience is "highly desirable"** — UNICEF explicitly states this. Prioritise UN/multilateral project references.

#### 2.4 Adverse Judgments
- Mandatory disclosure of any adverse legal judgments or arbitration awards
- If none exist, state explicitly: "No adverse judgments or awards"
- **Omission can be grounds for disqualification** — never skip this section

#### 2.5 References
- Minimum 3 references at organisation level
- Name, title, organisation, phone, email for each
- Select references the evaluator could actually contact — relevance matters more than seniority

### Section 3: Engagement Model and Technical Approach

This is the core of the technical proposal — equivalent to TECH-4 in World Bank proposals.

#### Engagement Model
- Type of engagement (development, operations, both, advisory)
- Overall approach to organisation and partnering with the UN agency
- Relationships with sub-contractors (if any)
- Previous experience in equivalent engagements

#### Management Approach
- **Project management methodology** — name it, state when you started using it, and how much experience you have gathered. UN agencies want proven, mature methodologies.
- **Issue management** — how issues are identified, escalated, and resolved
- **Risk management** — describe issues you expect to encounter and your approach to dealing with them. This phrase appears repeatedly in UN RFPs — proactive risk identification is heavily valued.
- **Progress reporting** — format, frequency, feedback mechanisms
- **Change management** — how changes are managed during each project phase

#### Quality Approach
- Quality environment for service delivery
- Quality certifications held (ISO 9001, CMMI, etc.)
- Formal processes and methodologies used
- **Third-party review acceptance** — many UN RFPs require acceptance of independent peer reviews. Demonstrate openness and a structured approach to external review.

#### Technical Solution
- Detailed response to the specific technical requirements in the ToR
- Named tools, frameworks, and methodologies
- **Future-proofing** — UN RFPs explicitly ask how the solution addresses process change, technology change, and market developments. Long-term thinking is rewarded.

### Section 4: Key Personnel and CVs

#### Team Structure
- Team composition with clear roles and responsibilities
- **Supervisory relationships** — who supervises whom (org chart recommended)
- Work tasks assigned to each person
- Approach to maintaining expertise level over time (staff retention/continuity)
- **Relationship management** — UN agencies "place much value on the relationship with the vendor." Personnel continuity is a differentiator.

#### CV Template (UN Standard Format)

| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| **Name** | Full name |
| **Position for this assignment** | Proposed role |
| **Nationality** | Country of citizenship |
| **Language skills** | Languages and proficiency levels |
| **Educational and other qualifications** | Degrees, certifications, specialised training |
| **Employment record** | From/To, Employer, Positions held |
| **Relevant experience** | From most recent. For each assignment: period, project/organisation name, job title, main project features, activities undertaken. Select assignments that best illustrate capability for THIS ToR. |
| **References** | Minimum 3 per person: name, title, organisation, phone, email |

**Strategy:**
- Use the agency's exact CV template format — do not substitute your own
- Select experience entries that directly map to the ToR tasks for this person
- Education, qualifications, and specialised training should be highlighted (not buried)
- 3 references per CV is the minimum — provide 4-5 strong ones where possible

### Section 5: Project Plan and Schedule

Must include:
- Deliverables and deliverable dates tied to payment milestones
- Major and minor milestones (internal and external)
- Client input timing (information gathering, reviews, feedback, acceptance)
- Critical paths, dependencies, and areas of float
- Review points (both internal and third-party)

### Section 6: Previous Experience

Distinct from Section 2 — this is a focused demonstration of:
- Experience in the specific technical domain of the assignment
- Track record with similar-scale engagements
- Successful project management and quality management history
- Experience with 24/7 operations, multi-timezone work, or other special requirements as relevant

## Financial Proposal

### Separation Rule
The financial proposal **must be a separate file** from the technical proposal. This is a hard compliance requirement across UN agencies. Embedding financial information in the technical proposal may disqualify.

### Pricing Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Exclude all taxes and VAT** | The UN is exempt. Quote net prices only. |
| **Currency** | As specified in the RFP (may be USD, EUR, or local currency) |
| **Price evolution** | For multi-year contracts, indicate how prices will evolve. UN agencies expect **fixed or declining prices** over the contract period. |
| **Separate cost components** | If bidding for multiple components (e.g., development + operations), costs must be kept separate |
| **Staged payments** | Payments tied to deliverables, not time elapsed |

### Multi-Year Contract Pricing

UN agencies increasingly use multi-year contracts (e.g., 3 years + 2-year option). For these:
- Show price stability or modest decreases in years 3-5
- Justify any price increases with objective factors (inflation indices, market data)
- UN agencies explicitly state they expect "fixed or declining prices" due to "the evolving competitive market"
- A proposal showing 2-3% annual decreases signals alignment with UN expectations

## IPR, Data, and Asset Ownership

### Intellectual Property Rights
- The UN body holds **exclusive IPR** on all work developed under the contract
- Proposals must clearly identify what is the agency's IP vs. third-party IP (e.g., licensed software)
- Clarify boundaries for tools, utilities, and code libraries with pre-existing ownership
- **Do not claim IP rights over project deliverables** — this is a non-negotiable UN position

### Data Ownership
- The UN body asserts **full rights over all data** generated, collected, or processed
- Proposals must identify all data held, its location, and storage media

### Asset Novation
For contracts involving assets (hardware, licences):
- Default depreciation: 3-year straight-line, zero residual value
- All licences and assets must be itemised with ownership status
- Novation fees (transfer costs at contract end) must be specified

## Strategic Signals — What UN Evaluators Value

These patterns emerge consistently across UN RFP templates:

### 1. Proactive Risk Identification
"Describe issues you expect to encounter and your likely approach to deal with them" appears in nearly every section of UN RFPs. **Do not present a rosy picture.** Show you understand the challenges and have thought through solutions.

### 2. Proven, Mature Methodologies
"Indicate when you started to use this approach and how much experience you have gathered." UN agencies want methodologies you have actually used, not aspirational ones. State the year you adopted the approach and number of projects delivered using it.

### 3. Openness to External Review
Third-party reviews of both project management and technical quality are often mandated. Demonstrate comfort with independent scrutiny and describe how you integrate review findings.

### 4. Value for Money
"Good value to the secretariat" and "cost-effective operation" appear repeatedly. Technical excellence alone is insufficient — demonstrate efficiency.

### 5. Relationship Quality and Continuity
UN agencies explicitly value the vendor relationship. Propose named individuals who will be the consistent points of contact. Address staff retention and how you maintain expertise over the contract period.

### 6. Future-Proofing
Explicit requirement to address how your solution handles process change, technology change, and evolving requirements. Short-term thinking scores low.

### 7. UN/Multilateral Experience
Experience with UN organisations and major multilateral/bilateral programmes is consistently described as "highly desirable." Lead with UN project references in every experience section.

## Common Mistakes in UN Consulting RFPs

| Mistake | Impact |
|---|---|
| Financial information in the technical proposal | May disqualify — must be separate files |
| Prices include VAT or taxes | Signals unfamiliarity with UN procurement |
| Own CV format instead of agency template | Non-compliance — may be scored down |
| No adverse judgments section | Omission can trigger disqualification |
| Fewer than 3 references per CV | Below minimum requirement |
| Generic risk section ("we will manage risks") | Misses the UN emphasis on proactive risk identification |
| No mention of third-party review readiness | Misses a compliance requirement in many RFPs |
| Claiming IP rights over deliverables | Conflicts with UN standard contractual terms |
| Rising prices in multi-year proposals | Contradicts UN expectation of fixed or declining prices |
| No UN/multilateral experience highlighted | Misses the "highly desirable" signal across agencies |

## Agency-Specific Notes

### UNICEF
- Uses "Annex E" format for technical and financial proposals
- Value statement is half a page (prescribed length)
- Financial proposal in printed AND electronic versions may be required
- 3-year contracts with 2-year extension option are common
- Local currency (country-specific) may be required

### UNFCCC
- Highly structured Annex-based RFP (10+ annexes)
- Separate annexes for requirements, parameters, SLAs, contractual terms
- Technical proposal template (Annex 9) is detailed and prescriptive — fill in every section
- Emphasis on system design, architecture, security, auditability
- Developer-Operator separation for IT services
- Steering group oversight of SLA performance
- IPR and data ownership provisions are prominent

### General UN Pattern
- Most UN agencies follow UNDP procurement procedures or similar
- Individual consultant procurement uses a different (simpler) process from firm procurement
- Evaluation committees are multi-disciplinary
- Best-value-for-money is the overriding principle (not lowest price)
