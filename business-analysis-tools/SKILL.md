# Business Analysis Tools

Top-tier proposals reference named analytical frameworks and tools — they show the evaluator how the firm thinks, not just what it will do. This skill provides a library of business analysis tools that proposal sections draw from, particularly methodology (06), understanding of assignment (03), and executive summary (02).

## When to Read This Skill

- Drafting methodology sections that describe how the firm will analyse problems
- Proposing a diagnostic or assessment approach
- Structuring the conceptual framework section of a methodology
- The ToR asks for specific analytical deliverables (gap analysis, benchmarking, options appraisal)
- Strengthening a proposal with named, credible frameworks that demonstrate structured thinking

## Strategic Analysis Tools

### SWOT Analysis
**Use for**: organisational assessments, sector diagnostics, baseline studies.

| | Positive | Negative |
|---|---|---|
| **Internal** | Strengths | Weaknesses |
| **External** | Opportunities | Threats |

Best practice: never present a SWOT alone — always follow with "So what?" — which strengths to leverage, which weaknesses to address, which opportunities to prioritise, and which threats to mitigate.

### PESTLE Analysis
**Use for**: environment scanning, policy analysis, sector assessments, feasibility studies.

| Factor | Description | Proposal Application |
|---|---|---|
| **Political** | Government policy, political stability, regulatory direction | Public sector reform, governance assignments |
| **Economic** | Growth rates, inflation, exchange rates, fiscal environment | Financial sector, economic development assignments |
| **Social** | Demographics, cultural factors, health, education levels | Health, education, social protection assignments |
| **Technological** | Digital infrastructure, technology adoption, innovation | ICT, digital transformation assignments |
| **Legal** | Legislation, regulatory frameworks, compliance requirements | Regulatory reform, legal framework assignments |
| **Environmental** | Climate, natural resources, environmental regulations | Agriculture, energy, infrastructure assignments |

### STEEPLE Analysis
**Use for**: comprehensive environment scanning that adds ethical considerations to PESTLE.
Add one row to the existing PESTLE table:
| **Ethical** | Community consent, safeguarding standards, environmental justice, indigenous rights, anti-corruption norms | Development projects, extractive industries, community-based interventions |
Reference STEEPLE instead of PESTLE when the ToR involves community-level interventions, safeguarding, or ethics-sensitive sectors.

### Porter's Five Forces
**Use for**: market analysis, competition assessments, sector studies, private sector advisory.

1. Threat of new entrants
2. Bargaining power of suppliers
3. Bargaining power of buyers
4. Threat of substitute products or services
5. Competitive rivalry within the industry

### Value Chain Analysis
**Use for**: sector competitiveness studies, agricultural value chain assessments, industrial diagnostics.

- Map the full chain from inputs to end consumer
- Identify where value is created and where it is lost
- Pinpoint bottlenecks, inefficiencies, and opportunities for intervention
- Common in agriculture, manufacturing, and trade assignments

### TOWS Matrix
**Use for**: extending SWOT into actionable strategy options.
Build on a completed SWOT by generating four strategy types:
| Strategy | Combination | Question |
|---|---|---|
| **SO (Maxi-Maxi)** | Strengths × Opportunities | How can we use strengths to maximise opportunities? |
| **ST (Maxi-Mini)** | Strengths × Threats | How can we use strengths to minimise threats? |
| **WO (Mini-Maxi)** | Weaknesses × Opportunities | How can we minimise weaknesses by exploiting opportunities? |
| **WT (Mini-Mini)** | Weaknesses × Threats | How can we minimise weaknesses and avoid threats? |
Always present TOWS as the "So what?" after SWOT — it converts analysis into strategy.

### SOAR Analysis
**Use for**: positive/appreciative organisational assessments, strategic planning, rebranding.
| Dimension | Focus |
|---|---|
| **Strengths** | What are we doing well? What are our greatest assets? |
| **Opportunities** | What are the best possible market/sector opportunities? |
| **Aspirations** | What do we care deeply about? What is our preferred future? |
| **Results** | How will we know we are succeeding? What measurable outcomes? |
Use when the ToR calls for a forward-looking, strengths-based assessment rather than a deficit-focused diagnostic.

### McKinsey 7-S Framework
**Use for**: organisational effectiveness assessments, institutional capacity reviews, change readiness.
Seven interdependent factors that must be aligned:
| Hard Elements | Soft Elements |
|---|---|
| **Strategy** — the plan | **Shared Values** — core beliefs and culture |
| **Structure** — how the org is organised | **Style** — leadership and management approach |
| **Systems** — processes and procedures | **Staff** — people and their capabilities |
| | **Skills** — competencies and capabilities |
Misalignment between any elements explains organisational underperformance. Commonly used in institutional capacity assessments for government agencies and development organisations.

### Value Proposition Canvas
**Use for**: product/service design, market positioning, customer-centric strategy.
Two components:
- **Customer Profile**: customer jobs (what they need to do), pains (frustrations, risks), gains (desired outcomes)
- **Value Map**: products/services offered, pain relievers (how you address pains), gain creators (how you deliver gains)
Useful in private sector advisory, market development, and enterprise development assignments.

## Diagnostic and Assessment Tools

### Gap Analysis
**Use for**: any assignment that compares a current state to a desired state.

| Area | Current State | Desired State | Gap | Priority | Recommended Action |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Gap analysis is the backbone of most consulting methodology — phase 1 assesses current state, phase 2 defines desired state, the gap drives the recommendations.

### Benchmarking
**Use for**: efficiency reviews, performance assessments, institutional comparisons.

Types of benchmarking to reference in proposals:

| Type | Compares Against | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Internal** | Other units within the same organisation | Branch-to-branch performance comparison |
| **Competitive** | Direct peers or competitors | University-to-university administrative cost comparison |
| **Functional** | Best practice in a specific function, regardless of sector | Comparing procurement processes against ISO 20400 |
| **International** | Peer institutions in other countries | Comparing Uganda's revenue authority against Kenya's and Rwanda's |

Deloitte pattern: reference proprietary benchmark databases as a tool the firm brings — not just experience but comparative data.

### Maturity Model Assessment
**Use for**: institutional capacity assessments, digital readiness, process maturity evaluations.

Standard five-level maturity scale:

| Level | Name | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Initial** | Processes are ad hoc and reactive |
| 2 | **Managed** | Basic processes established, but not standardised |
| 3 | **Defined** | Processes documented and standardised across the organisation |
| 4 | **Measured** | Processes monitored with metrics and performance data |
| 5 | **Optimised** | Continuous improvement embedded, processes regularly refined |

Adapt the levels and naming to the specific domain (e.g., IT maturity, data governance maturity, gender mainstreaming maturity).

### Root Cause Analysis
**Use for**: problem diagnostics, system failure assessments, process improvement.

Tools:
- **5 Whys**: iterative questioning to reach the root cause
- **Fishbone (Ishikawa) diagram**: categorises causes under People, Process, Technology, Policy, Environment
- **Pareto analysis**: 80/20 rule — identify the 20% of causes producing 80% of the problems

### Hypothesis-Driven Issue Tree
**Use for**: structured problem decomposition in diagnostic and advisory assignments.
1. State the overriding question at the top
2. Break into mutually exclusive, collectively exhaustive (MECE) sub-questions
3. For each sub-question, identify the data needed and method to answer it
4. Structure the methodology phases around the tree branches
5. As evidence is gathered, eliminate branches and converge on root cause

This is the standard consulting problem decomposition approach used by McKinsey, BCG, and Bain. It signals intellectual rigour and structured thinking in methodology sections.

## Process and Operations Tools

### Business Process Mapping
**Use for**: process improvement, system requirements gathering, efficiency reviews.

- Map current state (as-is) processes using flowcharts or swimlane diagrams
- Identify bottlenecks, redundancies, handoff failures, and manual workarounds
- Design future state (to-be) processes
- Quantify the improvement: time saved, steps eliminated, error rate reduced

### RACI Matrix
**Use for**: governance design, organisational clarity, role definition.

| Activity | Responsible | Accountable | Consulted | Informed |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| [Activity name] | [Who does it] | [Who owns it] | [Who is asked] | [Who is told] |

Essential in methodology sections for project governance and in team composition sections for role clarity.

### Balanced Scorecard
**Use for**: performance management frameworks, strategic planning, M&E design.

| Perspective | Objective | Measure | Target |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Financial** | [e.g., reduce cost per transaction] | [Cost per transaction] | [20% reduction] |
| **Customer/Stakeholder** | [e.g., improve service satisfaction] | [Satisfaction score] | [80%+] |
| **Internal Process** | [e.g., reduce processing time] | [Average days to process] | [3 days] |
| **Learning and Growth** | [e.g., increase staff competence] | [% staff certified] | [90%] |

## Financial and Economic Analysis Tools

### Cost-Benefit Analysis (CBA)
**Use for**: options appraisal, investment justification, policy analysis, feasibility studies.

| Item | Year 1 | Year 2 | Year 3 | Year 4 | Year 5 | Total |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Benefits** | | | | | | |
| [Benefit 1] | | | | | | |
| **Costs** | | | | | | |
| [Cost 1] | | | | | | |
| **Net benefit** | | | | | | |
| **NPV** | | | | | | |
| **BCR** | | | | | | |

Include discount rate, Net Present Value (NPV), and Benefit-Cost Ratio (BCR). State the discount rate used and why.

### Total Cost of Ownership (TCO)
**Use for**: ICT system evaluations, technology options appraisal, procurement advisory.

- Capital expenditure (hardware, software, implementation)
- Recurring costs (licences, hosting, maintenance, support staff)
- Hidden costs (training, change management, downtime, opportunity cost)
- Comparison across options over a five-year horizon

### Return on Investment (ROI)
**Use for**: business case development, investment proposals, impact assessment.

ROI = (Net Benefits / Total Costs) × 100

Always present ROI alongside payback period — how many months until the investment pays for itself.

## Decision-Making and Prioritisation Tools

### Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis (MCDA)
**Use for**: options appraisal, site selection, technology selection, policy prioritisation.

| Criterion | Weight | Option A | Score | Option B | Score | Option C | Score |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| [Criterion 1] | [%] | [Rating] | [Weighted] | [Rating] | [Weighted] | [Rating] | [Weighted] |

Rules: criteria and weights agreed with the client before scoring. Transparent and auditable.

### Prioritisation Matrix (Impact-Effort)
**Use for**: recommendation prioritisation, action planning, quick-win identification.

| | Low Effort | High Effort |
|---|---|---|
| **High Impact** | Quick wins — do first | Major projects — plan carefully |
| **Low Impact** | Fill-ins — do if time permits | Avoid — low return on investment |

Deloitte pattern: compile a "catalogue of opportunities", then prioritise using a criteria matrix before developing business cases for the top-priority items.

### Options Appraisal
**Use for**: any assignment requiring a recommended course of action from multiple alternatives.

Standard structure:
1. Define the options (including "do nothing" as a baseline)
2. Define evaluation criteria (agreed with the client)
3. Assess each option against the criteria
4. Present the comparison with a recommended option and rationale

## Using Tools in Proposals

### In the Conceptual Approach
Name the frameworks that will structure the analysis: "Our approach applies a PESTLE analysis to map the external environment, followed by a gap analysis to compare current state against regional best practice."

### In the Phase Descriptions
Reference tools as activities: "During Phase 1, we will conduct a maturity model assessment of the client's data governance practices across the five dimensions of the Data Management Maturity Model."

### In the Deliverables Table
Tools produce deliverables: "Current State Assessment Report (including SWOT analysis, process maps, and benchmarking results)."

### McKinsey Pattern
Name the tools as proprietary where possible: "Using our [Firm Name] Diagnostic Framework, we will assess..." — even a standard gap analysis becomes more credible when it has a name and a structured process.

Follow east-african-english standards throughout.
