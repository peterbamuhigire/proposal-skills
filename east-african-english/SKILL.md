## Core Characteristics

1. **Clear and direct.** Sentences are straightforward, grammatically careful, and logically structured. No slang in professional communication.
2. **Formal and respectful.** Politeness is essential. Communication shows courtesy and humility.
3. **British English spelling.** organisation, programme, centre, colour, travelling (double "l").
4. **Professionally indirect.** Avoid bluntness. Soften directives with courteous phrasing.
5. **Measured confidence.** Confident without arrogance. No dramatic or exaggerated language.

## British English Standards

### Spelling

Always use British spelling:

| Correct | Incorrect |
|---------|-----------|
| organisation | organization |
| programme | program |
| centre | center |
| colour | color |
| travelling | traveling |
| specialise | specialize |
| honour | honor |
| favourite | favorite |
| analyse | analyze |
| defence | defense |
| licence (noun) | license (noun) |
| catalogue | catalog |
| enquiry | inquiry |

### Dates

Write dates in day-month-year order:

- 17 February 2026
- 17th February 2026

Never use month-first American format (February 17, 2026).

## Tone by Country Context

When the client is based in a specific country, adjust the tone slightly:

### Uganda — Warm and Relational

- Very polite and appreciative
- Frequent use of "kindly"
- Emphasis on harmony and goodwill

> We highly appreciate the opportunity to submit this proposal and look forward to contributing to the Authority's reform programme.

### Kenya — Confident and Business-Oriented

- Efficient and practical
- Clear timelines and expectations
- Professional firmness

> Our team will mobilise within two weeks of contract signing and deliver the inception report by 21 February 2026.

### Tanzania — Calm and Measured

- Formal and slightly conservative
- Respectful and patient rhythm
- Influenced by Swahili sentence structure

> We kindly request the Evaluation Committee's guidance on the preferred format for the deliverables outlined in the Terms of Reference.

When no specific country is indicated, use the **neutral East African style** — a balanced blend of all three.

## Courteous Phrases for Proposals

These are natural and expected in East African professional writing:

- We kindly request…
- We would like to draw attention to…
- We wish to highlight…
- We are pleased to present…
- We have the honour to submit…
- Should the Evaluation Committee require any further clarification, we remain at your disposal.
- We look forward to the opportunity to serve [Client Name].
- We appreciate the confidence placed in our firm.

## Vocabulary Standards

### Preferred Professional Words

Use simple, professional vocabulary:

- facilitate, implement, undertake, liaise, coordinate
- engage, support, enhance, review, confirm
- advise, revert (meaning "respond" — widely accepted in East Africa)
- significant, important, strategic, beneficial, valuable
- deploy, mobilise, commence, conclude

### Words and Phrases to Avoid

Never use exaggerated marketing language in proposals:

| Avoid | Use Instead |
|-------|-------------|
| groundbreaking | significant |
| revolutionary | innovative |
| game-changing | strategic |
| amazing | commendable |
| awesome | excellent |
| incredible | remarkable |
| unleash | enable |
| skyrocket | improve significantly |
| supercharge | strengthen |
| crushing it | performing well |
| world-class (unless substantiated) | proven, established, leading |
| best-in-class | highly experienced, well-established |

Also avoid:

- American slang and casual abbreviations (FYI, ASAP, BTW)
- Dramatic emotional tone
- Complex academic vocabulary that obscures meaning
- Excessive exclamation marks (none in proposals)
- AI-sounding vocabulary (delve, tapestry, landscape as metaphor, leverage, navigate as metaphor, foster, realm, harness, synergy, embark, robust, vibrant, holistic, seamless)

Instead of abbreviations, write in full:

> Please respond as soon as possible.

## Sentence Style

### Use Full, Balanced Sentences

- No fragments or telegram-style copy
- Moderate length — not too short, not too complex
- Logical structure with clear subject-verb-object order

Good:

> The team has completed the initial assessment and will submit the final report by the end of the week.

### Professional Indirectness

Soften directives and assertions. Never sound blunt or commanding.

| Too Blunt | East African Style |
|-----------|--------------------|
| We are the best firm for this. | We believe our experience positions us well to deliver this assignment. |
| The client's current system is failing. | The current system presents opportunities for significant improvement. |
| This timeline is unrealistic. | We respectfully note that the proposed timeline may benefit from additional consideration. |
| Other firms lack our expertise. | Our team brings a combination of skills that is directly relevant to this assignment. |

### Proposal-Specific Style

- **First-person plural throughout**: "We propose…", "Our team will…", "We have successfully…"
- **Active voice preferred**: "We will conduct stakeholder workshops" not "Stakeholder workshops will be conducted"
- **Quantify wherever possible**: "We trained 127 staff across four departments" not "We trained many staff"
- **Name the client**: use the client's actual name, not "the client" — it shows the proposal was written for them
- **Reference the ToR**: "As outlined in Section 3.2 of the Terms of Reference" — shows careful reading

## Openings and Closings (for cover letters and correspondence)

### Openings

- Dear Sir/Madam,
- Dear Mr. Otieno,
- The Chairperson, Contracts Committee,
- The Procurement Officer,

### Closings

- Yours faithfully, (when you do not know the recipient)
- Yours sincerely, (when you know the recipient)

Often followed by:

> We look forward to the opportunity to contribute to [Client Name]'s objectives and remain at your disposal for any further clarification.

## Reference Paragraph — Neutral East African Proposal Style

Use this as a benchmark for tone and rhythm:

> We have the honour to submit this technical proposal in response to the Terms of Reference for the Development of an Integrated Financial Management Information System for the Ministry of Finance, Planning and Economic Development. Our firm brings over fifteen years of experience in public financial management systems across East Africa, having successfully implemented similar platforms for the Uganda Revenue Authority and the Kenya National Treasury. We are confident that our proposed approach, combined with our team's deep understanding of government financial processes, will deliver a system that meets the Ministry's requirements and supports its reform agenda.
