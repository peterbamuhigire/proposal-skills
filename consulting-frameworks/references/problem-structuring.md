# Problem Structuring Frameworks

Frameworks for decomposing consulting problems into structured, MECE analytical components. These are the foundational tools for designing any methodology's conceptual framework.

---

## 1. Eight First-Principles Strategies for Problem Structuring

Eight distinct lenses for generating MECE top-level structures. Any one can be applied to any problem:

| # | Strategy | Description | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Process Steps** | Map the flow of how a good is produced or service rendered | Operations, value chain, efficiency |
| 2 | **Components** | Identify physical/digital/human components needed | Infrastructure, system design |
| 3 | **Segmenting** | Break by business segments, products, or customers | Portfolio analysis, market assessment |
| 4 | **Matrix Approach** | Combine two perspectives into a grid (segments × criteria) | Option evaluation, comparison |
| 5 | **Problem Drivers** | List variables that directly influence the outcome | Root cause analysis, KPI design |
| 6 | **Mathematical Representation** | Express the target as a formula | Financial targets, KPI-driven scoping |
| 7 | **Using Opposites** | Create exhaustive coverage through antonyms (internal/external, monetary/non-monetary) | Ensuring MECE compliance |
| 8 | **Stakeholder Approach** | Structure around key stakeholders | Multi-party assignments |

**Proposal application:** When designing a methodology's conceptual framework, select the most appropriate structuring strategy. The matrix approach is especially powerful for evaluation frameworks in proposals.

## 2. The Eight-Bucket Analytical System

A repertoire of eight analytical lenses. For any proposal, select three to four most relevant buckets as methodology phases:

1. **Market Attractiveness** — Market size, growth rate, average profit margins, major trends, new technologies, new regulations, market maturity, market convergence with adjacent markets.

2. **Competitive Landscape** — Competitors, market share distribution, competitor product lines, competitor capabilities, points of differentiation, barriers to entry (economic and non-economic).

3. **Company Attractiveness / Capabilities** — Product line breadth, product differentiation, market share, profitability, distribution channels, partnerships, buying power, go-to-market strategy, geographic presence, growth trajectory.

4. **Customer Segmentation and Needs** — Segment identification, segment characteristics, needs and preferences per segment, how customers are changing, profitability per segment.

5. **Financial Considerations** — Revenue elements, cost elements, pricing strategy, breakeven timeline, cost of acquisition, financial exit strategy, profitability analysis.

6. **Synergies** — Revenue synergies (cross-selling, new customer bases, shared infrastructure), cost synergies (headcount reduction, overhead consolidation, increased buying power), realisability, timeline.

7. **Risks and Mitigations** — Risk identification, likelihood and impact assessment, mitigation strategies. Applicable to any engagement.

8. **Create Your Own Bucket** — A custom analytical lens specific to the assignment that does not fit neatly into buckets 1–7.

### How to use in methodology design

- **Step 1:** Run through all eight and select three to four most relevant to the assignment objective.
- **Step 2:** For each selected bucket, define two to three specific sub-activities tailored to the client context.
- **Step 3:** Sequence the selected buckets into methodology phases.

This guarantees a custom methodology for every proposal while ensuring no major analytical dimension is overlooked.

## 3. Issue Tree / Logic Tree Construction

The core consulting problem decomposition method:

1. **Define the top-level objective** (from the ToR)
2. **Decompose into three to four major branches** (the methodology phases)
3. **Each branch has two to three sub-branches** (the key activities)
4. **Branches must be MECE** — Mutually Exclusive (no overlap) and Collectively Exhaustive (no gaps)
5. **Every branch must be relevant** to the top-level objective

### MECE compliance checks

- **Mutually Exclusive:** If two phases could be merged without losing analytical value, they overlap. Restructure.
- **Collectively Exhaustive:** If an evaluator can identify a gap in coverage ("but what about X?"), something is missing. Add it.

### Mapping to methodology phases

- The top-level objective = the assignment objective from the ToR
- Major branches = methodology phases
- Sub-branches = key activities within each phase
- The logic tree is built FIRST, then sequenced into a timeline

## 4. Hypothesis-Driven Analysis

The consulting standard for structuring analytical work:

1. Form an initial hypothesis based on available information
2. Structure analysis to test the hypothesis
3. Gather data that confirms or refutes
4. Refine the hypothesis based on evidence
5. Converge on a conclusion

**Methodology application:** Frame each methodology phase as testing a specific hypothesis branch. This transforms a methodology from a list of activities into an analytical narrative with intellectual momentum.

**Language pattern:** "Our initial hypothesis, informed by [source], is that [problem] is driven primarily by [suspected cause]. Phase 1 will test this through [method]. If confirmed, Phase 2 will develop [solution]. If our analysis reveals alternative drivers, we will adapt accordingly."

## 5. Three Idea Expansion Techniques

For developing depth beneath any top-level methodology structure:

| Technique | Direction | Method |
|---|---|---|
| **Expansion** | Top-down | Take each bucket, create 3–5 sub-areas, expand each into concrete actions |
| **Extremes** | Lateral | Imagine the extreme action, then dial back to feasible variations |
| **Linking** | Bottom-up/sideways | Start with one idea; go down (detail), up (generalise), or sideways (adjacent) |

**Key principle:** You only need ONE idea as a starting point. Then go down, go up, or go sideways to create a full framework.

**Proposal application:** When a methodology section feels thin, apply these techniques to develop richer sub-activities. "Extremes" is particularly useful for generating the full range of options before narrowing to recommendations.

## 6. Seven Structuring Pairs for Qualitative Analysis

Instant analytical organisation for any qualitative assessment:

| Pair | Use When |
|---|---|
| **Internal / External** | Analysing factors affecting an organisation |
| **Short-term / Long-term** | Evaluating impacts or recommendations across time horizons |
| **Economic / Non-economic** | Distinguishing financial from non-financial factors (governance, capacity, reputation) |
| **Quantitative / Qualitative** | Separating measurable metrics from judgement-based assessments |
| **Revenue-side / Cost-side** | Any financial problem |
| **Benefits / Costs** | Evaluating any initiative or policy |
| **Tangible / Intangible** | Separating quantifiable impacts from non-quantifiable ones (brand, reputation, relationships) |

**Methodology language:** "Phase 2 will assess barriers to entry, examining both economic barriers (capital requirements, economies of scale) and non-economic barriers (regulatory compliance, institutional knowledge, brand recognition)."
