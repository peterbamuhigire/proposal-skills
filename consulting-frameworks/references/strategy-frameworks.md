# Strategy Frameworks

Frameworks for strategy, market entry, growth, competitive positioning, and acquisition assignments. Use when the ToR involves strategic advisory, market analysis, or institutional transformation.

---

## 1. Market Entry Methodology

Use when evaluating whether a client should enter a new market, launch a new service line, or expand geographically.

| Phase | Analytical Focus | Key Questions |
|---|---|---|
| Market Attractiveness Assessment | Size, growth rate, average margins | Is this market large enough and growing? |
| Company Capabilities Assessment | Leverageable capabilities, relevant experience, risk tolerance | Can the client actually compete? |
| Competitive Landscape Analysis | Market concentration, competitor differentiation, barriers to entry | How hard is it to enter and survive? |
| Financial Feasibility | Profitability projections, breakeven timeline | Will it be profitable, and when? |

---

## 2. Competitive Response Framework

Use when a competitor takes a specific action (new entrant, price drop, product launch) and the client must decide how to respond.

| Component | Key Questions |
|---|---|
| **Threat sizing** | How large and direct is the threat? Does the competitor have differentiation? |
| **Customer response** | How will customers react? How likely are they to switch? |
| **Competitor response** | Will other competitors also react? How? |
| **Strategic options** | Do nothing / Match / Counter-attack / Differentiate / Exit |

**Methodology language:** "The methodology sequences threat assessment before strategy development — sizing the competitive threat, modelling customer and market response, then evaluating strategic options."

---

## 3. Build vs. Buy vs. Partner Framework

Three modes of acquiring new capability or entering a new market:

| Mode | Advantages | Disadvantages |
|---|---|---|
| **Build internally** | Full control, no integration risk | Slow, requires investment, hiring |
| **Partnership / JV** | Access to partner's capabilities, shared risk | Shared profits, less control, dependency |
| **Acquire** | Immediate capabilities, brand, customer base | High cost, integration risk, cultural clash |

**Methodology language:** "The options appraisal will evaluate three capability acquisition modes — build, partner, and acquire — against [criteria], with a recommended pathway and implementation sequence."

## 4. Adjacent Market Proximity Model

Structure adjacent market opportunities by distance from core:

| Tier | Description | Feasibility | Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Core-adjacent** | Same domain, different product | Highest | Lowest |
| **Product-adjacent** | Same product type, different domain | High | Low-Medium |
| **Sector-adjacent** | Same broad sector, different product type | Medium | Medium |
| **Sector-distant** | Same sector, entirely different business model | Lowest | Highest |

**Principle:** Closest adjacencies are most feasible and likely to succeed. Evaluate from inside out.

**Methodology language:** "Growth opportunities will be mapped by proximity to the core business, with closer adjacencies evaluated first for feasibility and risk-adjusted return."

## 5. Merger and Acquisition Evaluation Methodology

Use when evaluating a potential acquisition, merger, partnership, or institutional consolidation.

| Phase | Analytical Focus | Key Questions |
|---|---|---|
| Target Attractiveness Assessment | Profitability, differentiated capabilities, brand strength | Is the target worth acquiring? |
| Market Attractiveness Assessment | Market size, growth rate, average margins of target's market | Is the target's market attractive? |
| Synergy Analysis | Revenue synergies, cost synergies, realisability, timeline | What value does the combination create? |
| Financial Evaluation | Acquisition price fairness, breakeven analysis, exit strategy | Does the deal make financial sense? |

---

## 6. Pricing Strategy Methodology

Use when the assignment involves pricing a new product, service, or offering.

| Phase | Analytical Focus | Key Questions |
|---|---|---|
| Customer Willingness-to-Pay Analysis | Survey/focus group findings, ability to pay, subsidy factors | How much will customers actually pay? |
| Competitor Pricing Benchmarking | Competitor prices, product attributes, relative positioning | Where do competitors price, and why? |
| Cost-Plus Profitability Analysis | Production costs, margin targets, breakeven requirements | What price covers costs and meets targets? |
| Alternative Pricing Strategy Evaluation | Discounting, bundling, subscription, tiered pricing | Which pricing model maximises revenue? |

---

## 7. Profitability Diagnosis Methodology

Use when diagnosing why a client is losing money, why performance has declined, or how to improve financial results.

| Phase | Analytical Focus | Key Questions |
|---|---|---|
| Financial Decomposition | Revenue (quantity × price) vs. costs (variable + fixed) | Where exactly is money being lost? |
| Competitive Landscape Review | Competitor actions, new entrants, competitor profitability | Is this company-specific or market-wide? |
| Customer Segment Analysis | Preference shifts, purchasing behaviour, customer attrition | Are customers leaving or changing? |
| Market Trends Review | New technologies, regulations, market structural changes | Are external forces driving the decline? |
