# Operations and Diagnostic Frameworks

Frameworks for operations improvement, process reengineering, multi-stakeholder assignments, and option appraisal. Use when the ToR involves operational efficiency, institutional assessment, or structured decision-making among alternatives.

---

## 1. Process Bottleneck Framework

For cases involving processes, efficiency, or capacity problems, decompose the end-to-end process and examine each step for constraints:

| Step | Action | Key Question |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Map the full process chain from input to output | What are the sequential steps? |
| 2 | For each step, assess whether it is the constraint | Is this step limiting overall throughput? |
| 3 | Identify the bottleneck step | Where does the system slow down? |
| 4 | Generate improvement options specific to that step | What interventions address the constraint? |

**Proposal methodology language:** "Our approach maps the end-to-end process from [input] to [output], systematically assessing each step to identify the binding constraint on throughput before designing targeted interventions."

Links to Theory of Constraints in `../../references/consulting-delivery-excellence.md` §15 and `../../business-analysis-tools/references/strategic-analysis-frameworks.md`.

## 2. Stakeholder-Based Framework

Structure the analysis around each stakeholder group and the levers they control. Use for healthcare, education, public sector, and multi-party institutional assignments.

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Identify all relevant stakeholders in the ecosystem |
| 2 | For each stakeholder, identify the levers they control |
| 3 | Assess what actions each stakeholder can take to address the problem |
| 4 | Prioritise stakeholders by impact potential |

**Template (drug adoption example):**
- Patients — awareness, willingness to request
- Physicians — awareness, willingness to prescribe
- Insurers — reimbursement policy, formulary inclusion
- Company — production capacity, distribution readiness

### Multi-Stakeholder Root Cause Analysis

A refinement that structures causes by stakeholder rather than by type:
- **Provider/operator reasons** — capability gaps, over-referral, misaligned incentives
- **Customer/beneficiary reasons** — demand patterns, preference shifts, demographic mix
- **Company/institutional reasons** — poor guidelines, insufficient scale, missing incentives

**Methodology language:** "Root cause analysis will be structured across three stakeholder dimensions: [provider behaviour], [beneficiary behaviour], and [institutional/policy factors]."

## 3. Criteria-Based Option Evaluation

For any assignment requiring selection among alternatives — site selection, technology choice, policy options, institutional reform pathways.

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Define evaluation criteria BEFORE assessing options |
| 2 | Assess each option against each criterion |
| 3 | Weight criteria by importance (agreed with the client) |
| 4 | Select the option that performs best across weighted criteria |

**Methodology language:** "We will evaluate [options] against [N] criteria: [list]. For each criterion, we will assess which option performs best and why, using a weighted scoring matrix agreed with the client before evaluation begins."

---

## 4. Situation Assessment Checklist

A diagnostic checklist for the inception/scoping phase of any assignment:

- What does the organisation do?
- How is it performing in its market/sector?
- What major actions has it taken recently?
- How is the broader market/sector performing?
- What is the competitive/institutional landscape?
- What have competitors/peers been doing differently?
- What are the stated objectives (and are there unstated ones)?

---

## 5. Market Structure Analysis

Key concepts for understanding the competitive environment:

- **Fragmented vs. concentrated markets** — determines competitive intensity, barriers to entry, and strategic positioning options
- **Market share analysis** — Revenue in market / total market size
- **Barriers to entry** — capital, technical knowledge, brand, distribution channels, economies of scale, technology, regulation, product differentiation

### Value Chain Analysis

- Map the supplier-manufacturer-distributor-retailer chain
- Identify where the client sits and where value is created or lost
- Assess vertical integration opportunities
