# SaaS Sales Method Fundamentals — Proposal Extraction (2026)

Internal synthesis for the proposal-skills engine. Source: Winning by Design, *The SaaS Sales Method Fundamentals: How to Have Customer Conversations*. Use as audit trail and implementation source, not for redistribution.

## Source Awareness

The book is a fundamentals manual on the SaaS sales conversation: communication discipline (their WbD Communication Technique), email (RRR — relevant, respectful, recent), voicemail, live calls (open, objections, rejections, offers of value), social engagement, messaging, and structured online meetings (with TAB-based presenting). It is the underlying communication layer beneath the closing motion in the AE book.

## Proposal-Relevant Synthesis

### Conversation Discipline as Discovery Quality
Proposals improve when the underlying discovery conversation is structured. The principle: every customer interaction must be relevant to the buyer's current situation, respectful of their time, and recent enough to reflect what changed. The proposal team should record discovery conversations against a defined template (situation, pain, impact, decision process, criteria, timing) — this becomes the input to BRIEF.md and to the methodology's diagnosis phase.

### Email and Asynchronous Engagement
For SaaS implementation engagements, the agency is often selling the *capability to run lifecycle communications* as a deliverable. The book's structured email pattern maps directly to the engine's proposal of a post-implementation email program: relevance signal, value-bearing insight, clear next step, and a measurable engagement loop. The engine should be able to scope and price this as a deliverable.

### Structured Meeting and Presentation
The TAB-based presenting model (move between Talk, Action, Build steps) is a useful pattern for proposing how the engagement team will run executive steering committees, demo sessions, and pilot reviews. Translate to: every recurring SaaS implementation meeting in the work plan has a stated purpose, a named artefact produced, and a decision required by close.

### Objections, Rejections, and Offers of Value
The fundamentals book separates an *objection* (a real concern that can be addressed) from a *rejection* (no current fit) — and treats every interaction as an opportunity to "offer something of value" rather than ask for more time. For proposals this means: an objection-handling matrix must distinguish addressable concerns from non-fit signals, and every follow-up touch must carry a fresh proof point, not a status check.

### Follow-Up as Process
Follow-up is treated as a system, not a virtue. The implication for SaaS proposals: scope the agency's account-management approach during the implementation as a named function with cadence, owners, and artefacts. Clients pay premium fees when they see follow-up infrastructure, not just promises.
