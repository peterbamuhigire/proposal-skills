Almost every East African consulting assignment includes a training or capacity building component — clients and donors want sustainable outcomes, not dependency on consultants. Proposals must show a structured approach to building the client's internal capability to maintain and build on the project's results after the consultants leave.

## When to Read This Skill

- The assignment includes training, mentoring, coaching, or skills transfer
- The ToR mentions "capacity building", "knowledge transfer", "training of trainers", or "institutional strengthening"
- Drafting methodology sections that need to describe how client staff will be skilled up
- When the ToR asks for a standalone training plan or capacity building strategy

## Approach

### Training Needs Assessment (TNA)

Before designing any training programme, assess the gap:

- **Current state**: what do staff currently know and do?
- **Desired state**: what must they know and do after the assignment?
- **Gap**: the difference drives the training design

Methods for TNA:

- Skills survey or self-assessment questionnaire
- Key informant interviews with managers
- Direct observation of current processes
- Review of job descriptions and performance data

### Training Design Principles

- **Adult learning**: participants learn best through practice, not lectures. Design for 30% theory, 70% hands-on
- **Context-specific**: use the client's own data, systems, and processes in training exercises — not generic case studies
- **Modular**: break training into self-contained modules that can be delivered flexibly
- **Assessed**: every training module must include a practical assessment or demonstration of competence
- **Documented**: all training materials remain with the client (user guides, job aids, quick-reference cards, video tutorials)

### Training Delivery Models

| Model | Best For | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| **Classroom training** | Large groups, foundational knowledge, standardised content | 1–5 days per module |
| **On-the-job mentoring** | Applied skills, system usage, process execution | Ongoing during implementation |
| **Training of Trainers (ToT)** | Sustainability — client staff become internal trainers | 3–10 days, followed by supervised delivery |
| **Coaching** | Senior staff, leadership skills, strategic thinking | Regular sessions over weeks or months |
| **E-learning / self-paced** | Geographically dispersed teams, refresher training | Flexible |
| **Shadowing** | Knowledge transfer from consultant to counterpart | Throughout the assignment |

### Training of Trainers (ToT)

The gold standard for sustainable capacity building in East African consulting:

1. Select trainers from within the client organisation — people with credibility, communication skills, and permanence
2. Train them on the content (technical knowledge)
3. Train them on delivery (facilitation skills, adult learning methods)
4. Supervised practice — trainers deliver to a pilot group with consultant observation and feedback
5. Certification — trainers assessed and certified to deliver independently
6. Materials handover — all training materials, facilitator guides, and assessment tools transferred

### Knowledge Transfer Plan

For every piece of work the consultant does, define:

| Deliverable / Skill | Transfer Method | Client Counterpart | Timeline | Verification |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| [System administration] | Shadowing + hands-on practice | [Name / role] | Weeks 4–8 | Counterpart performs task independently |

## Training Logistics

For proposals, include practical details that show the firm has thought through delivery:

- Venue requirements (client premises, off-site, virtual)
- Equipment and materials (laptops, projectors, printed manuals)
- Participant selection criteria and numbers
- Training schedule within the overall work plan
- Attendance tracking and certification

## Generating a Standalone Section

When the ToR asks for a dedicated capacity building plan, generate a document covering:

1. Training needs assessment methodology
2. Capacity building strategy and approach
3. Training programme design (modules, content, duration, delivery method)
4. Training of Trainers plan (if applicable)
5. Knowledge transfer plan with counterpart assignments
6. Training materials list and handover plan
7. Assessment and certification approach
8. Post-training support and follow-up

Follow east-african-english standards throughout.
