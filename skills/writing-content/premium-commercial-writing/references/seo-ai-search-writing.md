# SEO And AI-Search Writing

Parent skill: [Premium Commercial Writing](../SKILL.md).

Use this only for public-facing articles, web documents, knowledge pages, landing-page copy, case studies, and thought leadership. Do not force SEO into confidential procurement submissions unless the proposal is for SEO, content, or discoverability work.

## Search Intent First

Identify the main intent before drafting:

| Intent | Reader Need | Writing Response |
|---|---|---|
| Informational | Understand a topic or problem | Clear answer, definitions, examples, practical implications. |
| Commercial | Compare approaches or providers | Criteria, trade-offs, proof, risks, and decision guidance. |
| Transactional | Take action or request help | Clear offer, trust signals, process, next step. |
| Navigational | Find a named firm, service, or document | Accurate names, structure, and internal links. |

## AI-Search Friendliness

AI summaries favour content that is easy to extract, verify, and cite. Structure public content so it has:

- A direct answer in the opening section.
- Descriptive headings that match real questions.
- Clear entity names: service, sector, country, audience, tool, method, and organisation.
- Short definitions where terms may be ambiguous.
- Examples, comparisons, and decision criteria.
- Factual claims that can be traced to evidence or named experience.
- A concise summary or checklist near the end.

## SEO Basics

- Use one primary keyword cluster and two to four secondary terms.
- Put the primary term in the title, opening, at least one heading, and conclusion where natural.
- Avoid repeating the exact phrase when a synonym or specific subtopic is clearer.
- Match title, meta description, headings, and body content to the same reader intent.
- Add internal links to relevant service, case-study, about, contact, or related article pages.
- Use descriptive image alt text that explains the image and, where natural, includes the topic.

## Authority Signals

- Show first-hand expertise through named experience, process detail, constraints, and lessons.
- Explain trade-offs instead of presenting one-size-fits-all advice.
- Include dates when recency matters.
- Separate facts, recommendations, and assumptions.
- Avoid unsupported superlatives and invented statistics.

## Article Structure

Recommended structure:

1. Title that combines search clarity with a human angle.
2. Opening answer or concrete scenario.
3. Why the issue matters now.
4. Main explanation or framework.
5. Examples, comparisons, or mistakes to avoid.
6. Practical checklist or next steps.
7. Natural close and call to action.

## Do Not

- Keyword-stuff.
- Write generic introductions that delay the answer.
- Use headings that sound clever but hide the topic.
- Optimise for search terms the organisation cannot credibly serve.
- Let AI-search structure flatten the writer's judgement or voice.
