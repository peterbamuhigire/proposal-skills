# AfDB Safeguards and Financial Terms

Reference material for the afdb sector skill. Covers the Integrated Safeguards System (ISS 2023), all 10 Operational Safeguards, project categorisation, ESIA requirements, ADB/ADF lending terms, climate finance, and gender mainstreaming requirements.

Sources: AfDB Integrated Safeguards System (ISS, 2023), AfDB Environmental and Social Assessment Procedures (ESAP), AfDB Financial Products factsheets, AfDB Climate Change and Green Growth Strategy.

---

## 1. Integrated Safeguards System (ISS) — Overview

The ISS is AfDB's framework for managing environmental and social risks in all Bank-funded operations. The 2023 revision expanded the system from 5 to 10 Operational Safeguards, aligning more closely with other MDB frameworks while retaining AfDB-specific requirements.

**Core principles:**
- Do no harm — avoid, minimise, mitigate, or compensate for adverse impacts
- Proportionality — safeguard requirements proportionate to risk
- Borrower responsibility — the borrower implements safeguards; AfDB provides oversight
- Consultation and disclosure — meaningful stakeholder engagement throughout the project cycle
- Adaptive management — safeguards updated as project evolves

## 2. The 10 Operational Safeguards (OS)

### OS 1: Environmental and Social Assessment

**Purpose:** Identify, assess, and manage E&S risks and impacts.

**Key requirements:**
- Screening and categorisation (Category 1-4) at concept stage
- Full ESIA for Category 1; targeted assessment for Category 2; screening report for Category 3
- Environmental and Social Management Plan (ESMP) for Categories 1 and 2
- Environmental and Social Management System (ESMS) for Category 4 (financial intermediaries)
- Cumulative impact assessment where multiple projects affect the same area
- Analysis of alternatives — including the "no project" alternative
- Climate risk screening mandatory for all projects

**ESIA content requirements (Category 1):**
1. Project description and rationale
2. Policy, legal, and institutional framework
3. Baseline environmental and social conditions
4. Assessment of potential impacts (positive and negative)
5. Analysis of alternatives
6. Environmental and Social Management Plan (ESMP)
7. Monitoring plan
8. Stakeholder engagement plan and consultation records
9. Grievance redress mechanism

### OS 2: Involuntary Resettlement — Land Acquisition, Population Displacement, and Compensation

**Purpose:** Avoid or minimise involuntary resettlement; where unavoidable, ensure fair compensation and livelihood restoration.

**Key requirements:**
- Resettlement Action Plan (RAP) for significant displacement
- Abbreviated RAP for minor displacement (fewer than 200 people)
- Replacement cost valuation — no deduction for depreciation
- Compensation before displacement
- Livelihood restoration plan for economically displaced persons
- Vulnerable groups (women, elderly, disabled, indigenous) given special attention
- Monitoring and evaluation of resettlement outcomes

### OS 3: Biodiversity, Renewable Resources, and Ecosystem Services

**Purpose:** Conserve biodiversity and maintain ecosystem services.

**Key requirements:**
- No conversion or degradation of critical natural habitats
- Mitigation hierarchy: avoid → minimise → restore → offset (biodiversity offsets as last resort)
- Net gain in critical habitats; no net loss in natural habitats
- Sustainable management of living natural resources
- Assessment of ecosystem services affected

### OS 4: Pollution Prevention and Control, Hazardous Materials, and Resource Efficiency

**Purpose:** Avoid or minimise pollution and promote efficient use of resources.

**Key requirements:**
- Apply pollution prevention and control technologies
- Comply with relevant ambient quality standards (IFC/WHO guidelines where national standards are less stringent)
- Waste management hierarchy: avoid → reduce → reuse → recycle → treat → dispose
- Hazardous materials management plan
- Greenhouse gas accounting for projects with significant emissions
- Resource efficiency measures (energy, water, raw materials)

### OS 5: Labour Conditions, Health and Safety

**Purpose:** Protect workers' rights, health, and safety.

**Key requirements:**
- Terms and conditions of employment documented and communicated
- Non-discrimination and equal opportunity
- Workers' organisations and collective bargaining rights respected
- No child labour or forced labour
- Occupational health and safety management system
- Worker grievance mechanism
- Standards apply to direct workers, contracted workers, and primary supply workers
- Community health and safety considerations

### OS 6: Indigenous Peoples

**Purpose:** Ensure free, prior, and informed consent (FPIC) for projects affecting indigenous peoples.

**Key requirements:**
- Identification of indigenous peoples in the project area
- Social assessment with participation of affected communities
- Free, prior, and informed consent for projects affecting their lands, resources, or cultural heritage
- Indigenous Peoples Development Plan
- Benefit-sharing arrangements
- Culturally appropriate grievance mechanism

### OS 7: Cultural Heritage

**Purpose:** Protect tangible and intangible cultural heritage.

**Key requirements:**
- Chance find procedures in all civil works contracts
- Assessment of impacts on cultural heritage
- Consultation with affected communities
- No removal of cultural heritage unless all alternatives exhausted
- Intangible cultural heritage (practices, traditions, knowledge) considered

### OS 8: Climate Change and Disaster Risk

**Purpose:** Integrate climate change adaptation and mitigation; reduce disaster risk.

**Key requirements:**
- Climate risk and vulnerability assessment for all projects
- Climate adaptation measures incorporated into project design
- GHG emissions quantification for significant emitting projects
- Disaster risk assessment where relevant
- Climate co-benefits tracked and reported
- Consistency with national climate commitments (NDCs)

### OS 9: Gender Equality

**Purpose:** Mainstream gender equality and women's empowerment.

**Key requirements:**
- Gender analysis in project preparation
- Gender Action Plan for projects with gender dimensions
- Sex-disaggregated indicators in results framework
- Gender-based violence risk assessment and mitigation
- Women's meaningful participation in consultation and decision-making
- Equal access to project benefits

### OS 10: Stakeholder Engagement and Information Disclosure

**Purpose:** Ensure meaningful stakeholder engagement throughout the project cycle.

**Key requirements:**
- Stakeholder Engagement Plan (SEP) — proportionate to project risk
- Identification and analysis of stakeholders (including vulnerable and marginalised groups)
- Meaningful consultation — timely, accessible, culturally appropriate
- Disclosure of project information in local languages
- Grievance redress mechanism — accessible, transparent, responsive
- Ongoing engagement during implementation
- Documentation of engagement activities and how stakeholder input influenced project design

## 3. Project Categorisation

All AfDB-funded projects are categorised at the concept stage:

| Category | Risk Level | Assessment Required | Disclosure |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Category 1** | High | Full ESIA + ESMP | ESIA disclosed for 120 days before Board |
| **Category 2** | Moderate | Targeted ESIA or ESMP | ESMP disclosed for 30 days before Board |
| **Category 3** | Low | Screening report | No separate disclosure requirement |
| **Category 4** | Financial intermediary | ESMS for the intermediary | ESMS disclosed before Board |

**Category 1 triggers:**
- Large-scale infrastructure (dams, highways, power plants)
- Significant resettlement (>200 people physically displaced)
- Projects in or near critical natural habitats, protected areas
- Projects affecting indigenous peoples' lands
- Projects with significant GHG emissions

**Category 2 examples:**
- Small-scale infrastructure
- Limited resettlement
- Projects near but not in sensitive areas
- Institutional development and capacity building

**Category 3 examples:**
- Technical assistance and advisory services
- Training programmes
- Institutional strengthening
- Policy studies

**Proposal implication:** Most consulting assignments (TA, institutional strengthening, studies) are Category 3 or the project they support is already categorised. When the assignment involves safeguard compliance work (preparing ESIAs, RAPs, ESMPs), demonstrate expertise with the correct category requirements.

## 4. ESIA Process

For Category 1 and 2 projects, the ESIA process follows AfDB requirements:

**Screening** → **Scoping** → **Impact Assessment** → **Mitigation** → **ESMP** → **Disclosure and Consultation** → **Appraisal** → **Implementation** → **Monitoring**

**Disclosure requirements:**
- Category 1: full ESIA disclosed on AfDB website for 120 calendar days before Board presentation
- Category 2: ESMP disclosed for 30 calendar days
- All documents available in local languages at local venues

**Consultation requirements:**
- At least two rounds of public consultation for Category 1
- One round for Category 2
- Vulnerable groups (women, youth, disabled, elderly) must be specifically engaged
- Consultation records must document how stakeholder input influenced project design

## 5. ADB (Sovereign) Lending Terms

The African Development Bank (ADB) window provides sovereign-guaranteed lending to creditworthy RMCs:

**Standard Sovereign Loan:**
- **Currency:** UA (Unit of Account, equivalent to SDR) or major currencies
- **Interest rate:** Variable rate based on SOFR/EURIBOR + lending spread
- **Maturity:** Up to 25 years (20 years for middle-income countries)
- **Grace period:** Up to 7 years (5 years for middle-income countries)
- **Commitment fee:** 0.25% per annum on undisbursed balance
- **Front-end fee:** 0.25% of loan amount (one-time)

**Enhanced Variable Spread (EVS) Loan:**
- Variable spread over reference rate
- Borrower can fix the rate through an embedded option
- Maturity up to 20 years

---

## 6. ADF Lending Terms

The African Development Fund (ADF) provides concessional resources to low-income RMCs:

**ADF Loan:**
- **Interest rate:** 0% (service charge of 0.75% per annum)
- **Maturity:** 40 years
- **Grace period:** 10 years
- **Commitment fee:** 0.50% per annum on undisbursed balance

**ADF Grant:**
- No repayment required
- For the most debt-distressed countries or specific thematic areas
- Same procurement rules apply as for loans

**Blend countries:** Some RMCs are eligible for both ADB and ADF resources based on their GNI per capita and creditworthiness assessment.

## 7. Climate Finance and Green Growth

AfDB has set ambitious climate finance targets:

**Climate Action Plan:**
- Target: 40% of annual approvals to be climate finance
- All projects screened for climate risk
- Climate co-benefits tracked in results framework

**Climate screening requirements:**
- Mandatory for all projects
- Assessment of physical climate risks (flooding, drought, extreme heat)
- Assessment of transition risks (policy, technology, market changes)
- Adaptation measures incorporated into project design
- GHG emissions estimated for significant emitting projects

**Green bond programme:** AfDB issues green bonds to finance climate-related projects.

**Proposal implication:** When writing methodology for AfDB-funded assignments, include climate considerations — even if the project is not primarily about climate. AfDB evaluators look for climate-smart approaches.

## 8. Gender Mainstreaming

AfDB's Gender Strategy requires gender mainstreaming across all operations:

**Gender Marker System:**
- **GG (Gender Designed):** gender equality is the primary objective
- **GM (Gender Mainstreamed):** gender equality is a significant objective
- **SGI (Some Gender Informed):** minor gender elements
- **NGE (No Gender Elements):** no gender dimension (unusual — most projects must include gender)

**Requirements for proposals:**
- Gender analysis during project preparation
- Sex-disaggregated indicators in the results framework
- Gender Action Plan where relevant
- GBV risk assessment for projects with civil works or influx of workers

**Proposal implication:** Include gender-responsive elements in methodology. Show how the assignment will benefit women and girls, use sex-disaggregated data, and address gender barriers. This is now scored, not optional.

## 9. Financial Management Requirements

For consultants working on AfDB-funded projects:

**Disbursement methods:**
- Direct payment (AfDB pays consultant directly)
- Reimbursement (borrower pays, then claims from AfDB)
- Special Account / Designated Account (revolving fund)
- Statement of Expenditure (SOE) for smaller amounts

**Audit requirements:**
- Annual project audit by independent auditors acceptable to AfDB
- Audit report submitted within 6 months of financial year end
- AfDB may require separate audit of consultant's expenditures

**Tax exemption:**
- Consultants on AfDB-funded contracts are typically exempt from local taxes
- Specific arrangements vary by country and financing agreement
- Ensure tax treatment is clarified during contract negotiations

## 10. Procurement and Safeguard Integration

When the consulting assignment involves both procurement and safeguard elements:

**Procurement plans must include:**
- ESIA/ESMP preparation costs
- Monitoring and evaluation of safeguard implementation
- Grievance mechanism operating costs
- Stakeholder engagement costs
- Resettlement costs (if applicable)

**Timeline integration:**
- ESIA must be disclosed before Board presentation
- RAP must be implemented before displacement begins
- ESMP implementation begins at project effectiveness
- Monitoring continues through project completion and beyond (for resettlement)

## 11. AfDB Results Framework

All AfDB-funded projects use a results-based approach:

**Results chain:**
- **Inputs** → **Activities** → **Outputs** → **Outcomes** → **Impact**

**Key performance indicators tracked by AfDB at corporate level (examples):**
- People with new or improved electricity connections
- Kilometres of roads constructed or rehabilitated
- People with improved access to transport
- People trained (disaggregated by sex)
- Jobs created (disaggregated by sex)

**Proposal implication:** Align project-level results framework with AfDB's corporate results framework. Show how the assignment contributes to AfDB's High 5 priorities:
1. Light up and power Africa
2. Feed Africa
3. Industrialise Africa
4. Integrate Africa
5. Improve the quality of life for the people of Africa
