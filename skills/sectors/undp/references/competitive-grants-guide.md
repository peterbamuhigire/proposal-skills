# Competitive Grants Guide — UNDP-Managed Re-Granting to CSOs

Detailed guide for UNDP-managed competitive grant calls where UNDP acts as fund manager for a donor (EU, bilateral, etc.) and re-grants to civil society organisations. This is the most structured UNDP grant modality, with published scoring criteria and numerical evaluation.

---

## How It Works

1. Donor (e.g., EU) provides funds to UNDP under a programme agreement
2. UNDP issues a Call for Proposals to eligible CSOs
3. CSOs submit applications by the deadline
4. Local Selection Committee scores proposals against published criteria
5. Regional Selection Committee makes final funding decisions
6. Selected CSOs sign a Low Value Grant Agreement with UNDP
7. Results published on UNDP country office website

---

## Application Package — Standard Components

| Component | What It Contains | Critical Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Annex 1: Project Proposal** | The substantive proposal — problem, methodology, results, sustainability | This is where 90% of the score comes from |
| **Annex 2: Budget Breakdown** | Line-item budget with expenditure plan by month/quarter | Must exclude VAT where UNDP exemption applies |
| **Annex 3: Declaration of Double Funding** | Statement that the same activities are not funded by another source | Standard compliance document |
| **Annex 4: Declaration of Partnership** | Roles and responsibilities of consortium partners (if applicable) | Only required for consortium applications |

### Supporting Documents

- Valid certificate of registration (lead + all partners)
- Organisation statute (lead + all partners)
- Final annual financial reports for previous 3 years (balance sheet and income statement)
- Annual narrative report for previous year
- Employment contracts of core project team OR Letters of Intent + CVs
- Tax/contribution compliance certificate (issued by tax authority, current to month before call publication)

**Missing any document = disqualification.** A 5-day cure period may be offered for incomplete submissions, but do not rely on it — incomplete submissions signal poor quality to evaluators.

## Evaluation Scoring — 1000-Point Scale

### Overview

| # | Criterion | Weight | Max Points |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Qualifications and experience of lead candidate and partners | 30% | 300 |
| 2 | Relevance of project proposal, methodology, approach, and implementation plan | 50% | 500 |
| 3 | Sustainability of project proposal | 10% | 100 |
| 4 | Economy and relevance of budget | 10% | 100 |
| | **TOTAL** | **100%** | **1000** |

### Detailed Sub-Criteria

#### Criterion 1: Qualifications and Experience (300 points)

| Sub-criterion | Max Points | Scoring Bands |
|---|---|---|
| **1.1 Project management experience** | 70 | 1-2 years = 30; 3-4 years = 50; 5+ years = 70 |
| **1.2 Previous experience in call-relevant areas** | 90 | 1 project in 3 years = 35; 2 projects = 70; 3+ projects = 90 |
| **1.3 Team size and qualifications** | 60 | 2-3 qualified persons = 30; 4-5 = 45; 6+ = 60 |
| **1.4 Annual turnover (last 3 years)** | 50 | EUR 10k-15k = 20; 15k-20k = 35; 20k+ = 50 |
| **1.5 Partner relevance** | 30 | New partnership = 5; 2+ years similar work = 10; 4+ years = 20; 6+ years = 30 |

**Strategy notes:**
- Include all qualified team members to reach the 6+ threshold (60 pts vs. 30 pts for a small team)
- Document at least 3 relevant projects in the last 3 years for maximum score (90 pts vs. 35 pts for just 1 project)
- Emphasise long-standing partnerships — 6+ year partnerships score 6x more than new ones
- Financial reports must demonstrate the turnover thresholds — ensure they are clear and auditable

#### Criterion 2: Relevance, Methodology, Approach, Implementation (500 points)

| Sub-criterion | Max Points | Scoring Bands |
|---|---|---|
| **2.1 Approach and strategy** | 120 | Somewhat correspond = 50; Mostly = 80; Fully = 120 |
| **2.2 Activities contribute to expected results** | 150 | Somewhat = 40; Mostly = 90; Fully = 150 |
| **2.3 Risk management and monitoring** | 50 | Somewhat defined = 10; Mostly = 30; Fully = 50 |
| **2.4 Gender equality perspective** | 100 | Somewhat included = 40; Mostly = 75; Fully = 100 |
| **2.5 Systemic change potential / bonus criteria** | 80 | Up to 3 of 6 criteria met = 50; 3-6 criteria met = 80 |

**Strategy notes — this is where proposals are won or lost (500/1000 points):**

- **2.1 Approach:** Explicitly link every strategic element to the call's thematic priorities. Use the call's own language. A proposal that "somewhat corresponds" scores 50; "fully" scores 120 — a 70-point gap.
- **2.2 Activities → Results:** Build an explicit results chain: Activity → Output → Outcome → Impact. If the evaluator cannot trace the logic from activities to results, you land in "somewhat" (40 pts) instead of "fully" (150 pts). This single sub-criterion has the largest point spread in the entire evaluation.
- **2.3 Risk management:** Many CSOs skip this entirely or write one sentence. A clear risk register with 5-6 risks, likelihood, impact, and specific mitigations moves you from 10 pts to 50 pts — a 5x improvement for relatively little writing effort.
- **2.4 Gender:** This is worth 100 points as a standalone sub-criterion. Gender cannot be a paragraph at the end. It must be woven throughout: gender analysis informing design, gender-specific activities, sex-disaggregated indicators, budget lines for gender activities.
- **2.5 Bonus criteria:** Address all 6 explicitly. The jump from meeting ≤3 criteria (50 pts) to meeting 3-6 (80 pts) is 30 points.

#### Criterion 3: Sustainability (100 points)

| Band | Points |
|---|---|
| Somewhat sustainable | 30 |
| Mostly sustainable | 75 |
| Fully sustainable | 100 |

**Strategy:** Name specific post-project arrangements: institutional embedding, government commitment, alternative funding, trained local capacity, community ownership mechanisms.

#### Criterion 4: Budget Economy and Relevance (100 points)

| Band | Points |
|---|---|
| Somewhat realistic | 40 |
| Mostly realistic | 70 |
| Fully realistic | 100 |

**Strategy:**
- Administrative costs must stay under 20% of total
- All amounts exclude VAT (UNDP exemption)
- Every line item should be justifiable — no padding
- Co-financing strengthens budget credibility
- Expenditure plan must align with the implementation timeline

## The Three-Tier Band System — Scoring Psychology

Unlike World Bank's 0-4 granular scale, UNDP competitive grants use three-tier bands:

| Band | Evaluator Conclusion | Typical Score | Strategy |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Somewhat** | "They addressed it, but not convincingly" | 30-40% of max | Avoid at all costs |
| **Mostly** | "Good enough, but gaps remain" | 60-75% of max | Acceptable but not winning |
| **Fully** | "Comprehensive, specific, convincing" | 100% of max | Target for every criterion |

**Critical insight:** There is no partial credit within bands. A proposal that is 80% of "fully" still scores "mostly." Write to clearly and unambiguously cross the "fully" threshold. Be explicit, specific, and complete.

## Eligibility and Partnership Rules

### Lead Applicant Must:
- Be registered as a CSO in the relevant country
- Have valid registration certificate
- Have filed financial reports for the previous 3 years
- Have a current tax compliance certificate

### Consortium Rules:
- Lead applicant signs the grant agreement and bears full responsibility
- Partners receive and use funds per the Declaration of Partnership
- Same applicant can lead maximum 1 project AND partner on 1 other simultaneously
- "Collaborators" (non-partner supporters) **cannot** receive project funds — distinguish carefully

### Duration and Budget:
- Minimum duration: typically 4 months
- Maximum duration: typically 12-15 months
- Grant range: varies by call (e.g., EUR 5,000-35,000)
- Total pool: stated in the call (e.g., EUR 70,000)

## Selection Process

1. **Administrative Verification** — pass/fail checklist of required documents
2. **Individual Scoring** — each committee member scores independently using the evaluation table
3. **Collective Scoring** — committee agrees collective scores, signs collective evaluation table
4. **Ranking** — proposals ranked by total score (highest first)
5. **Final Decision** — Regional Selection Committee may apply "fair distribution" principle across UNDP offices
6. **Results Published** — on UNDP country office website

### Committee Integrity:
- Each member signs individual evaluation table
- Conflict of interest declarations required
- Attempting to collect confidential information or influence evaluators = disqualification

## Common Mistakes That Cost Points

| Mistake | Impact |
|---|---|
| Gender treated as an afterthought paragraph | Loses up to 100 points on criterion 2.4 alone |
| No risk register | Loses 40 points (10 vs. 50 on criterion 2.3) |
| Activities listed without results chain | Drops from 150 to 40 on criterion 2.2 |
| Small team (2-3 people) | Scores 30 vs. 60 on criterion 1.3 |
| Only 1 relevant past project | Scores 35 vs. 90 on criterion 1.2 |
| New partnership with no track record | Scores 5 vs. 30 on criterion 1.5 |
| Budget includes VAT | May trigger administrative disqualification |
| Administrative costs exceed 20% | May trigger administrative disqualification |
| Missing supporting documents | Administrative disqualification — game over |
