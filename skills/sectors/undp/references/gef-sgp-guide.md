# GEF Small Grants Programme (SGP) Guide

Detailed guide for preparing project proposals under the GEF Small Grants Programme, administered by UNDP globally. Covers the standard project proposal template, mandatory indicators, dual objectives requirement, co-financing, and strategic priorities.

---

## What Is GEF SGP?

- **Global Environment Facility Small Grants Programme** — implemented by UNDP in 125+ countries
- Provides grants to CSOs and community-based organisations for environmental and sustainable development projects
- Typical grant range: US$5,000-50,000 (varies by country)
- Projects generally capped at 2 years
- Currently in GEF-7 Operational Phase with specific strategic initiatives

## Proposal Template Structure

**Strict page limit: 10 pages maximum** (including charts/diagrams) plus up to 10 pages of attachments.

### Cover Page

| Field | What to Include |
|---|---|
| General information | Country, project number (assigned by UN), title, date |
| Applicant organisation | Name, year established, address, principal officer, project contact |
| Project location | Description + map with latitude/longitude coordinates |
| GEF SGP classification | Strategic initiative + primary focal area |
| Duration | Start date + number of months |
| Finance | GEF SGP request + co-financing + total |

### Strategic Initiatives (Select One)

1. **Enabling conditions** to respect rights of indigenous peoples in FOLU (Forest and Other Land Use) sector
2. **Piloting indigenous community-led** forest and land use measures

### Primary Focal Areas (Select One)

- Land Degradation / Sustainable Land and Forest Management
- Climate Change Mitigation / Adaptation

### Social Inclusion Categories (Grant Maker Plus)

Check all that apply: Indigenous Peoples, Youth, Women, Persons with Disabilities.

## Section A: Project Proposal — 10 Required Sub-Sections

### A.1 Project Summary
One paragraph covering rationale, objective, key results, target site/community. This is the evaluator's first impression — make it specific and compelling.

### A.2 Project Rationale
- Challenges being addressed
- Link to SGP Country Programme Strategy (explicit cross-reference required)
- Link to national REDD+ programme and FOLU sector of NDC
- Current situation analysis with evidence

**Strategy:** Link to three frameworks explicitly — SGP Country Programme Strategy, national REDD+, and FOLU/NDC. Evaluators check for these alignments.

### A.3 Project Objectives, Results/Outputs, Activities

**Critical requirement — dual objectives:**
Every project must have BOTH:
1. An **environmental** objective (conservation, restoration, sustainable management)
2. A **development/livelihood** objective (improved livelihoods, food security, income)

Missing either objective is a structural gap that cannot be compensated.

Must include:
- Primary objective statement (both dimensions)
- Measurable results with quantified targets
- Specific outputs under each result
- Detailed activities under each output
- Implementation plan with timeline (table format recommended)

### A.4 Community and Stakeholder Participation Plan

Participation must span ALL THREE phases:
1. **Planning/design** — how communities were involved in identifying the problem and designing the project
2. **Implementation** — specific roles for community members in carrying out activities
3. **Monitoring and evaluation** — how communities will participate in tracking results and evaluating outcomes

**Strategy:** "Community consultation during design" is insufficient. Show genuine co-design with evidence (meeting records, community validation, participatory assessments).

### A.5 Knowledge Management

A standalone required section — not an afterthought. Must include:
- How knowledge and lessons learned will be captured
- How they will be shared and disseminated
- Target audiences for knowledge products
- Specific knowledge outputs (reports, case studies, toolkits, videos)

### A.6 Inclusion — Women, Youth, and Persons with Disabilities

Must be reflected in:
- **Budget** — dedicated line items for inclusion activities
- **Results** — specific outcomes for women, youth, persons with disabilities
- **Benefits** — how these groups specifically benefit from the project

**Special priority:** At least 30% of funded projects must be implemented by indigenous women and youth. Proposals led by or heavily involving these groups have a structural advantage.

### A.7 Impacts on Biodiversity and Natural Resources

Potential adverse impacts assessment:
- What negative environmental effects could the project cause?
- How will they be avoided or mitigated?
- If no adverse impacts, state explicitly with reasoning

### A.8 Number of Beneficiary Families

Quantify direct beneficiaries (families, not just individuals) where relevant.

### A.9 Impact — Sustainability, Scale-up, and Replication

Three time horizons:
- **Before project:** What conditions are in place for success?
- **During project:** What sustainability mechanisms are being built?
- **After project:** What ensures continuation? Can the approach be replicated elsewhere?

### A.10 Innovation

What is genuinely new:
- New thinking about the problem
- New ways of organising communities
- New connections between stakeholders
- New products, services, or delivery methods

**Strategy:** "Innovation" does not mean "technology." Community-based innovations in governance, resource management, or social organisation count. Be specific about what is new and why it matters.

## Section B: Project Risks, Monitoring and Evaluation

### Risk Screening

Assess risks across four categories:
1. **Project risks** — implementation, capacity, financial
2. **Contextual risks** — political, security, market
3. **Environmental risks** — climate, natural disasters
4. **Social risks** — conflict, displacement, exclusion

## Mandatory Indicators Framework

### Global Environmental Indicators (Select Minimum 1, Maximum 3)

| Code | Indicator | Unit |
|---|---|---|
| 3.1 | Area of degraded agricultural lands restored | Hectares |
| 4.1 | Area of landscapes under improved management for biodiversity | Hectares |
| 4.3 | Area under sustainable land management in production systems | Hectares |
| 5 | Area of marine habitat under improved practices | Hectares |

### Mandatory Socio-Economic Indicators

- Number of direct beneficiaries with improved livelihoods
- Number of direct beneficiaries from SGP intervention
- Disaggregated by: Women, Indigenous Peoples, Youth, Persons with Disability
- Gender gap closing: access/control of natural resources, participation in governance, socio-economic benefits

### Optional Additional Indicators

16 optional indicators covering biodiversity, land degradation, climate change, international waters, and cross-cutting social inclusion. Select those most relevant to the project.

## Eligibility Requirements

### Eligible Entity Types

| Type | Description |
|---|---|
| **IPO** | Indigenous Peoples' Organisation |
| **CBO** | Community-Based Organisation registered as legal body |
| **LNGO** | Local NGO registered with relevant ministry |
| **Women's Group** | Registered women's organisation |
| **Youth Group** | Registered youth organisation |

### Special Requirements (Vary by Call)

- Registration with relevant government ministry
- Proven experience with indigenous/community work in FOLU sector
- For CBOs: majority of members must be from the target community
- Unregistered communities can submit jointly with a registered organisation
- Official government document recognising targeted communities (where applicable) — **missing = instant disqualification**

## NC and NSC Support

The National Coordinator (NC) and National Steering Committee (NSC) are expected to provide support during proposal preparation:
- Guidance on alignment with SGP Country Programme Strategy
- Technical advice on proposal design
- Information on co-financing opportunities
- Feedback on draft proposals before formal submission

**Strategy:** Engage the NC early. Their guidance is not optional — it is expected and can significantly strengthen the proposal.

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Impact |
|---|---|
| Only environmental objective, no livelihood dimension | Structural gap — fails dual objective requirement |
| Community participation only in design, not in M&E | Misses a required element |
| No knowledge management plan | Missing a required section |
| Exceeding 10-page limit | Proposal may be returned or truncated |
| No co-financing shown | Signals weak commitment and poor sustainability |
| Vague innovation section | Misses a scored criterion |
| No sex-disaggregated indicators | Fails mandatory indicator requirements |
| Missing eligibility documents | Instant disqualification |
