# PPDA Evaluation Forms, Contract Management and Procurement Procedures

Reference material extracted from official Uganda Public Procurement and Disposal of Public Assets Authority (PPDA) standard forms. All forms are issued under the Public Procurement and Disposal of Public Assets Act, 2003 (Cap 205).

---

## FORM 14 — Evaluation Report Under the Technical Compliance Evaluation Method

**Regulation:** 12(5)
**Applies to:** Supplies, Works, Non-Consultancy Services
**Procurement methods:** Open domestic/international bidding, restricted domestic/international bidding, quotations, direct procurement

### Evaluation Methodology — Four-Stage Procedure

1. **Preliminary Examination** (Pass/Fail) — Determines eligibility of bidders and assesses administrative compliance to the invitation. Non-eligible and non-compliant bids are eliminated from further evaluation.

2. **Detailed Evaluation** (Responsive/Non-Responsive) — Determines the technical responsiveness of eligible, compliant bids to the technical specification and terms and conditions in the invitation. Bids that are not substantially responsive are eliminated from further evaluation.

3. **Financial Comparison** — Examines and ranks financial bids. The lowest price eligible, compliant, substantially responsive bid is ranked first and declared the best evaluated bid.

4. **Post-Qualification** — Determines whether the best evaluated bidder has the capacity and resources to effectively execute the procurement.

### Key Tables

**Table 1 — Preliminary Examination and Assessment of Eligibility**
- Columns: Bidder 1 through 5
- Rows: Eligibility Criteria (as per bidding document) + Administrative Compliance Criteria
- Each cell marked: C (Compliant) or NC (Non-Compliant)
- Final row: Conclusion — C/NC per bidder
- Result: Eliminates non-eligible/non-compliant bidders with count and names

**Table 2 — Summary of Detailed Technical Evaluation**
- Only bids that are eligible and compliant
- Columns: No., Name of bidder, Technical Responsiveness (R/NR), Reasons for responsiveness/non-responsiveness (including nonmaterial nonconformities, errors and omissions accepted and how quantified)
- Key: R = Responsive, NR = Non-Responsive
- Result: Eliminates non-responsive bidders with count and names

**Table 3 — Summary of Financial Comparison**
- Only bids that are eligible, compliant and substantially responsive
- Columns: No., Name of bidder, Bid currency, Bid Total, Corrections made, Discounts, Adjustments made, Corrected amount, Exchange rate, Converted total, Margin of preference, Evaluated total, Rank
- Evaluation currency stated
- Working spreadsheets required for split awards or conditional discounts for multiple lots

### Process Requirements
- Evaluation Committee approved by Contracts Committee with named members (designation and department)
- Record of sale/issue of bidding documents (Form 8)
- Record of minutes of pre-bid meeting (Form 10) where applicable
- Record of bids received (Form 11)
- Record of bid opening (Form 12)
- Public bid opening required
- Disagreement by Evaluation Committee must be documented with details of issues, discussions, alternative views and names
- All evaluation committee members must sign the report

### Recommendations Section
Must state:
1. Best evaluated bid (name of bidder, currency and amount)
2. Whether negotiations are/are not required
3. Total proposed contract price
4. Total acquisition cost estimate including all incidental costs

## FORM 15 — Combined Technical and Financial Evaluation Report Under Quality and Cost Based Evaluation Method

**Regulation:** 12(5)
**Applies to:** Textbooks, Information Technology Systems, Design and Build procurements (Supplies/Works/Non-Consultancy Services)
**Method:** Quality and Cost Based Selection (QCBS)

### Purpose
This form is used AFTER the technical evaluation (Form 16) is approved by the Contracts Committee and financial bids are opened. It records the financial comparison and the combined scoring.

### Financial Comparison Process

1. Contracts Committee approves technical evaluation report and authorises opening of financial bids
2. Public opening of financial bids at stated location, date and time
3. Financial bids opened, stamped and signed by Chairperson
4. Price and technical score read out
5. Opening recorded using Form 17

### Financial Scoring Formula

**Standard method:**
- Lowest evaluated price receives a score of **100**
- Other bids scored inversely proportional: `(Lowest price / Bid price) x 100 = Financial Score`
- Alternative methodology may be stated in the invitation

### Combined Scoring

**Total Score = Weighted Technical Score (WTS) + Weighted Financial Score (WFS)**

- Technical weight: stated as percentage (e.g. 70%, 80%)
- Financial weight: stated as percentage (e.g. 30%, 20%)
- Technical + Financial weights must equal 100%
- Maximum possible total score: 100
- Bidder with highest total score ranked first

### Key Tables

**Table 1 — Summary of Financial Comparison**
- Columns: No., Name of Bidder, Bid Currency, Bid Total, Discounts, Adjustments Made, Corrected Amount, Exchange Rate, Converted Total, Margin of Preference, Evaluated Total, Financial Score
- May be replaced with a spreadsheet with automatic calculations

**Table 2 — Summary of Technical and Financial Evaluation**
- Columns: No., Name of bidder, Technical score (Max 100), Technical weighting (___%), Weighted technical score (WTS), Financial score (Max 100), Financial weighting (___%), Weighted financial score (WFS), Total score (WTS + WFS, Max 100), Rank

### Post-Qualification
- Applies if required
- Best evaluated bidder assessed against qualification criteria in bidding document
- Finding: qualified or not qualified

### Annexes Required
- Technical evaluation report under QCBS (Form 16)
- Form 17: Record of financial bid opening
- Table 1: Summary of financial comparison
- Table 2: Technical and financial evaluation summary
- Minutes of evaluation committee meeting

## FORM 16 — Technical Evaluation Report Under Quality and Cost Based Evaluation Method

**Regulation:** 26(1)
**Applies to:** Textbooks, Information Technology Systems, Design and Build procurements (Supplies/Works/Non-Consultancy Services)
**Method:** Quality and Cost Based Selection (QCBS)

### Two-Envelope Submission
- Outer envelope contains two separately sealed envelopes: technical bid and financial bid
- Only technical bids opened initially; financial bids put in secure storage
- Technical bids opened, stamped and signed by Chairperson

### Evaluation Method — Four-Stage Procedure

1. **Preliminary Examination** (Pass/Fail) — Eligibility and administrative compliance. Non-eligible and non-compliant bids eliminated.

2. **Detailed Technical Evaluation** (Merit Point System) — Technical quality assessed against criteria in the Terms of Reference using a merit point system. Each bid compared to minimum technical qualifying mark; those not meeting it are eliminated.

3. **Financial Bids Opened** — Only for bidders who are substantially responsive and met the minimum qualifying mark. Financial comparison determines financial score.

4. **Combined Scoring** — Technical and financial scores combined using weightings from invitation. Highest total score ranked first, recommended for contract award subject to negotiations.

### Technical Scoring Structure

**Table 2 — Summary of Detailed Technical Evaluation**
- Criteria structure: Main Criteria (A, B, C, D, E) with Sub-criteria (A1, A2, A3, A4; B1, B2, B3, B4; etc.)
- Maximum score column for each criterion
- **Total maximum score: 100**
- Scores entered as averages from individual evaluator score sheets
- Final row: **Minimum qualifying mark** — Pass/Fail determination per bidder
- Up to 6 bidders can be scored in the standard template

**Table 2A — Detailed Technical Evaluation of Proposal (per bidder)**
- Same criteria structure as Table 2
- Shows scores from each individual evaluator and the average
- One Table 2A prepared for each bidder

**Table 2B — Evaluator's Score Sheet**
- Same criteria structure
- Individual evaluator scores per criterion
- Up to 5 evaluators with calculated average
- One Table 2B per evaluator

### Preliminary Examination Table (Table 1)
- Same structure as Form 14 Table 1
- Eligibility Criteria: C/NC per bidder
- Administrative Compliance Criteria: C/NC per bidder
- Conclusion: C/NC per bidder

### Strengths and Weaknesses
The report must document strengths and weaknesses for each bidder in a structured table:
- Columns: Number, Name of consultant, Strengths, Weaknesses

### Recommendation
States the minimum technical qualifying mark and recommends which bidders' financial bids should be opened (those who passed).

### Annexes Required
- Form 8: Record of sale or issue of bidding documents
- Form 10: Record of minutes of pre-bid meeting
- Form 11: Record of bids received
- Form 12: Record of opening of technical bids
- Table 1: Preliminary examination
- Table 2: Summary of detailed technical evaluation
- Table 2A: Detailed technical evaluation per bidder
- Table 2B: Score sheet per evaluator
- Minutes of evaluation committee meeting

## FORM 24 — Technical Evaluation Report for Consultancy Services

**Regulations:** 62(4), 64(6)
**Applies to:** Consultancy Services specifically
**Methods covered:** Quality and Cost Based Selection (QCBS), Quality Based Selection (QBS), Least Cost Selection (LCS), Fixed Budget Selection (FBS), Single/Sole Source

### Procurement Methods for Consultancy Services
- Open domestic/international bidding
- Restricted domestic/international bidding
- Quotation method
- Direct procurement
- Open domestic/international bidding with publication of notice inviting expression of interest

### Two-Envelope System for Consultancy Services
- Technical and financial proposals in separately sealed envelopes
- Only technical proposals opened initially
- Financial proposals put into secure storage
- Technical proposals stamped and signed by Chairperson
- Opening recorded using Form 23

### Method A: Quality and Cost Based Selection (QCBS)

**Four-stage procedure:**
1. **Preliminary Examination** (Pass/Fail) — Eligibility and administrative compliance
2. **Detailed Technical Evaluation** (Merit Point System) — Technical quality scored against criteria in ToR; compared to minimum technical qualifying mark; those below eliminated
3. **Financial Proposals Opened** — Only for substantially responsive consultants meeting minimum mark
4. **Combined Scoring** — Technical and financial scores weighted and combined; highest total score ranked first

**Recommendation:** States minimum qualifying mark and names consultants whose financial proposals should be opened.

### Method B: Quality Based Selection (QBS)

**Three-stage procedure:**
1. **Preliminary Examination** (Pass/Fail) — Eligibility and administrative compliance
2. **Detailed Technical Evaluation** (Merit Point System) — Quality scored; substantially responsive proposals ranked by technical score
3. **Financial Proposal of Highest Technical Score Opened** — Only the top-ranked consultant's financial proposal opened; consultant recommended for award subject to negotiations

**Recommendation:** Names the consultant with highest technical score whose financial proposal should be opened.

### Method C: Least Cost Selection (LCS)

**Three-stage procedure:**
1. **Preliminary Examination** (Pass/Fail)
2. **Detailed Technical Evaluation** (Merit Point System) — Minimum qualifying mark applies
3. **Financial Proposals Opened** — Ranked by lowest price; lowest priced consultant ranked first

**Recommendation:** Names consultants whose financial proposals should be opened.

### Method D: Fixed Budget Selection (FBS)

**Three-stage procedure:**
1. **Preliminary Examination** (Pass/Fail)
2. **Detailed Technical Evaluation** (Merit Point System) — Minimum qualifying mark applies
3. **Financial Proposals Opened** — Proposals exceeding budget rejected; highest technical score among those within budget ranked first

**Recommendation:** Names consultants whose financial proposals should be opened.

### Method E: Single/Sole Source Consultants

**Three-stage procedure:**
1. **Preliminary Examination** — Administrative compliance to RFP
2. **Detailed Technical Evaluation** — Quality scored using merit point system
3. **Financial Comparison** — Financial aspects examined; total price determined

**Recommendation:** Best evaluated proposal named with total evaluated price; states whether negotiations required; total proposed contract price and acquisition cost estimate.

### Technical Scoring Tables for Consultancy Services

**Table 1 — Preliminary Examination and Assessment of Eligibility**
- Eligibility Criteria rows (as per proposal document): C/NC per consultant
- Administrative Compliance Criteria rows: C/NC per consultant
- Conclusion: C/NC
- Up to 5 consultants

**Table 2 — Summary of Detailed Technical Evaluation**
- Criteria: Main Criteria (A-E) with Sub-criteria
- Maximum Score column
- Scores per consultant (up to 6)
- **Total maximum score: 100**
- **Minimum qualifying mark:** Pass/Fail determination

**Table 2A — Detailed Technical Evaluation per Consultant**
- Same criteria structure
- Shows individual evaluator scores and average
- Up to 5 evaluators
- One table per consultant being evaluated

**Table 2B — Evaluator's Score Sheet**
- Individual evaluator scores per criterion per consultant
- One per evaluator

### Strengths and Weaknesses Documentation
Required for each evaluation method variant — structured table with:
- Serial number, Name of consultant, Strengths, Weaknesses

### Negotiations Section (Section 12)
When negotiations are required:
- Justification must be in line with PPDA Act
- Negotiations plan outlined in Table 3
- Negotiations team named with positions/departments and Chairperson identified

**Table 3 — Negotiation Plan**
- Columns: Issue, Objectives, Negotiation Parameters

### Annexes Required
- Form 20: Record of issue of EoI/RFP/addenda
- Form 21: Record of minutes of pre-proposal meeting
- Form 22: Record of proposals
- Form 23: Record of opening of EoI/technical proposals
- Table 1: Preliminary examination
- Table 2: Summary of detailed technical evaluation
- Table 2A: Detailed technical evaluation per consultant
- Table 2B: Score sheet per evaluator
- Table 3: Negotiation Plan

## FORM 26 — Evaluation Report for Consultancy Services (Financial Comparison)

**Regulations:** 72(6), 73(6), 74(13)
**Applies to:** Consultancy Services — used after technical evaluation (Form 24) is approved
**Methods covered:** QCBS, QBS, LCS, FBS

### Purpose
Records the financial comparison phase after the Contracts Committee has approved the technical evaluation report and authorised the opening of financial proposals.

### Financial Proposal Opening
1. Public opening at stated location, date and time
2. Financial proposals opened, stamped and signed by Chairperson
3. Price and technical score read out
4. Opening recorded using Form 25

### Financial Comparison by Method

**QCBS (Section 3A-3B):**
- Corrections for arithmetic errors, discounts, adjustments, currency conversion, margin of preference
- Lowest evaluated price scored **100**
- Others scored inversely proportional: `(Lowest price / Proposal price) x 100 = Financial Score`
- Combined score = (Technical Score x Technical Weight%) + (Financial Score x Financial Weight%)
- Highest total score ranked first

**QBS (Section 4A):**
- Only the highest-ranked technical consultant's financial proposal is opened
- Arithmetic errors corrected
- Financial comparison summarised in Table 1
- Consultant recommended for award subject to negotiations

**LCS (Section 5A):**
- Corrections for arithmetic errors, discounts, adjustments, currency conversion, margin of preference
- All proposals ranked by price; lowest priced ranked first

**FBS (Section 6A):**
- Corrections for arithmetic errors, discounts, adjustments, currency conversion, margin of preference
- Proposals exceeding budget rejected
- Remaining proposals ranked by technical score; highest technical score ranked first

### Key Tables

**Table 1 — Summary of Financial Comparison**
- Columns: No., Name of consultant, Proposal currency, Proposal total, Corrections made, Discounts, Adjustments made, Corrected amount, Exchange rate, Converted total, Margin of preference, Evaluated total, Financial score

**Table 2 — Summary of Technical and Financial Evaluation (QCBS)**
- Columns: No., Name of consultant, Technical score (Max 100), Technical weighting (___%), Weighted technical score (WTS), Financial score (Max 100), Financial weighting (___%), Weighted financial score (WFS), Total score (WTS + WFS, Max 100), Rank

**Table 3 — Negotiations Plan**
- Subject of Procurement
- Name of Consultant
- Columns: Issue, Objectives, Negotiation Parameters

### Recommendations
Must state:
1. Best evaluated proposal (name, currency, amount)
2. Whether negotiations are/are not required; if required, negotiation plan attached as Table 3
3. Total proposed contract price
4. Total acquisition cost estimate including incidental costs

### Annexes
- Technical evaluation report (Form 24)
- Form 25: Record of financial proposal opening
- Table 1: Summary of financial comparison
- Table 2: Technical and financial evaluation summary
- Table 3: Negotiation Plan

## Performance Securing Declaration

**Applies to:** All contract types post-award
**Purpose:** Replaces traditional performance bank guarantee with a declaration

### Key Provisions

The bidder/provider declares:
1. Understanding that a Performance Securing Declaration valid until successful completion of contract must be submitted
2. **Acceptance of suspension penalty:** If the provider fails to perform contractual obligations or triggers termination due to default, they shall be **suspended by the Authority from being eligible for bidding in any public procurement or disposal process of Government for a period of three years**

### Validity Period
The declaration ceases to be valid upon:
- Completion of all obligations under the contract and remediation of all snags by end of Defects Liability Period/Warranty period; OR
- Issuance of the Completion Certificate for all works at end of Defects Liability Period

### Signatories
- Authorised signature with designation
- Full name of person signing
- On behalf of the Provider (full company name)
- Dated

- Provides one common standard with basic contractual provisions and safeguards required by Government of Uganda (GOU)
- Simplifies drafting of Standard Request for Quotations (SRFQ)
- Minimises Contracts Committee approval time
- Reduces bidders' time and effort in bid preparation
- Facilitates evaluation and comparison of bids

### Customisation vs Drafting Rules
- **Customisation:** One-off entry of PDE's contact details, logo, identification — approved by CC
- **Drafting:** Modifying customised SBD for a particular procurement requirement
- **Critical rule:** Section 5 (General Conditions of Contract) text must remain unchanged
- PDE shall not alter content, substance, style or format without prior written PPDA approval
- Do not delete section breaks, page breaks, headers or footers
- Notes in Italics in SBDs are for Bidders; Notes in Bold in User Guide are for PDEs

### Request for Quotations Process
- Sent to shortlisted bidders
- Must indicate important bid evaluation criteria
- Must include procurement schedule
- Must be accompanied by Bid Securing Declaration

## Bidder Strategy Notes — What Evaluators Are Looking For

### At Preliminary Examination Stage (Pass/Fail)
Evaluators check strict compliance. Any "NC" on eligibility or administrative compliance eliminates the bid entirely. Ensure:
- All eligibility documents are present and valid
- All administrative requirements met (correct format, signed, sealed, on time, bid security included)
- Every criterion in the bidding document addressed

### At Detailed Technical Evaluation Stage (Merit Points)
- Criteria and weightings are exactly as stated in the bidding document/ToR
- Up to 5 main criteria (A-E) each with up to 4 sub-criteria
- Total always out of 100
- Minimum qualifying mark is stated in invitation — proposals below this are eliminated regardless of price
- Multiple evaluators score independently; average is used
- Strengths and weaknesses of each proposal are formally documented
- Large differences between evaluators' scores must be justified

### At Financial Comparison Stage
- Arithmetic errors are corrected (this can change your ranking)
- Discounts and adjustments applied
- Currency conversion to common evaluation currency
- Margin of preference applied where applicable
- For QCBS: lowest price gets 100; your score = (lowest/yours) x 100

### At Combined Scoring Stage (QCBS)
- High technical score can overcome higher price
- The weighting split (e.g. 80/20 vs 70/30) dramatically affects strategy
- With 80/20 technical/financial split: technical quality is four times more important than price
