## Permitted Use

When book or EPUB paths are supplied, use them only for high-level source inspiration:

- Identify broad concepts, recurring themes, and practical writing principles.
- Convert ideas into original, operational repository guidance.
- Use short internal notes for synthesis, then write fresh instructions in the repository's own style.
- Prefer universal professional writing patterns over source-specific phrasing.

## Prohibited Use

- Do not copy protected text into repository files.
- Do not include long paraphrases that preserve the source's sequence, examples, or distinctive language.
- Do not quote from books unless the user explicitly asks and copyright limits allow it.
- Do not reproduce tables, frameworks, or named examples in a way that substitutes for the original work.
- Do not imply that the repository contains source-authorised excerpts unless permission is documented.

## High-Level Synthesis Themes

The premium writing layer may safely use general professional principles such as:

- Reader-first structure: start with the decision, problem, or answer.
- Argument discipline: claim, mechanism, evidence, control, and implication.
- Commercial clarity: connect value, risk, quality, and price.
- Narrative logic: current state, complication, proposed answer, proof, and next step.
- Proof-based persuasion: use outcomes, examples, constraints, and trade-offs.
- Editorial polish: concise sentences, concrete nouns, strong headings, and unnecessary-word removal.
- Discoverability: match public content to reader intent, entity clarity, and answer-first structure.
