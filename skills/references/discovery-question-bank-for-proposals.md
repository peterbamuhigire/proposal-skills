# Discovery Question Bank for Proposals

Derived from local HTML extractions on sales qualification, lean customer development, impact mapping, service mapping, UX writing, and design decision facilitation. Use these questions to clarify briefs and strengthen proposal assumptions.

## Outcome and Value

- What must be measurably different after this assignment?
- Which problem is most expensive, risky, or visible today?
- What baseline metrics exist for time, cost, revenue, quality, adoption, complaints, or compliance?
- What decision will the final deliverable help management make?
- What happens if the organisation delays the assignment by six months?

## Buyer and Decision Path

- Who will score the proposal, approve the award, manage delivery, and use the output?
- Which evaluation criteria matter most beyond compliance?
- What concerns would make a technically strong proposal lose confidence?
- Who must defend the award decision internally?
- What previous supplier experiences shape expectations?

## Current Pain and User Reality

- Where do users, staff, customers, or beneficiaries currently get stuck?
- Which handoffs, waits, errors, approvals, or workarounds cause the most frustration?
- What has already been tried, and why did it fall short?
- Which service moments create complaints, drop-off, rework, or reputational risk?
- What evidence exists from analytics, support logs, interviews, audits, or observations?

## Scope and Constraints

- What is definitely in scope, out of scope, and optional?
- Which client inputs are required before work can start?
- What approvals, policies, data access, licences, hosting, or procurement rules may constrain delivery?
- Which deadlines are fixed and which are negotiable?
- What should not be disrupted during implementation?

## Technology, Data, and AI

- What systems, data sources, APIs, tools, and user roles are involved?
- Who owns data quality, access, privacy, retention, and audit decisions?
- What service levels, security requirements, integrations, and reporting are mandatory?
- For AI, what are the accepted use cases, test cases, human approval points, and failure boundaries?
- What ongoing model, content, platform, or dependency maintenance is expected?

## Commercial and Support

- What budget range or rate ceiling should the proposal respect?
- What price comparisons will the client make?
- Which payment milestones are acceptable?
- What support period is expected after launch or handover?
- Which items should be priced separately: licences, hosting, travel, content, data cleanup, training, support, or optimisation?

## Proposal Use

Turn answers into:

- assumptions and exclusions
- risk register entries
- methodology phases
- acceptance criteria
- financial proposal drivers
- clarification questions
- executive-summary value statements
