# Vertical SaaS Positioning — Public Sector

Positioning brief for SaaS implementation and SaaS product-development proposals in public sector and government. Covers ministries, revenue authorities, regulators, statutory agencies, parastatals, municipal authorities, and donor-funded programmes.

## Sub-Vertical Map

| Sub-vertical | Typical Buyer | Typical Pain Pattern |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue authority | Commissioner + ICT Director | Tax compliance, taxpayer experience, integration with payment systems, audit |
| Ministry / department | Permanent Secretary + Director ICT | Citizen-service digital channels, inter-ministry integration, MIS reporting |
| Statutory agency | CEO + Director ICT | Sector-specific MIS, regulator-of-regulator reporting, public accountability |
| Municipal authority | Town Clerk + Director ICT | Revenue collection (rates, fees), citizen services, urban-planning data |
| Parastatal | MD + CIO | Operations modernisation, governance, financial reporting |
| Donor-funded programme | Programme Director + M&E lead | Beneficiary tracking, donor reporting, sustainability, capacity transfer |
| Public service commission | CEO + HR Director | Workforce management, examinations, recruitment |

## Procurement Frameworks

- **PPDA Uganda** — open, restricted, request-for-quotations, request-for-proposals, framework contracts; specific evaluation methods (least-cost selection, quality-and-cost-based selection).
- **PPRA Tanzania, PPRA Kenya** — broadly similar with national variations.
- **World Bank procurement** — TECH-1 through TECH-6, FIN-1 through FIN-4, eligibility rules, anti-corruption clauses.
- **AfDB procurement** — similar to World Bank with regional variations.
- **UNDP procurement** — RFP, ITB, RFQ frameworks; long-term agreements; multi-country contracts.
- **National sector procurement** — health, education, infrastructure ministries often have additional sector-specific procurement rules.

## Common Integration Reality

- IFMIS (Integrated Financial Management Information System) — Oracle E-Business, SAP, Tally-derived, custom builds; varies by country.
- HRIS — IPPD (Kenya), HCM (various).
- Identity systems — national ID systems (NIRA Uganda, Huduma Namba Kenya, BVN/NIN Nigeria), digital ID where rolled out.
- Payment integration — mobile-money, e-government payment gateways, treasury single account.
- Sector-specific MIS — HMIS (DHIS2), EMIS, judicial case management, land registration.
- Statistics systems — survey platforms, census, registry-based.

## Proof Points the Agency Should Cite

- Citizen-experience digital service launches with measured uptake.
- Revenue-collection modernisation with collection-rate uplift.
- Inter-ministry integrations with measured cycle-time improvements.
- Donor-reporting automations with audit-trail evidence.
- Capacity-transfer outcomes where the agency successfully handed operating responsibility to a government counterpart team.

## Price Ceiling Notes

- Public-sector procurement typically has published evaluation methods (often quality-and-cost-based selection, sometimes least-cost). Price is one of several scoring components, not the only one.
- Donor-funded engagements have eligible-cost rules and published rate ceilings; respect these or risk disqualification.
- Multi-year retainers possible through framework contracts.
- Withholding tax and currency rules vary by country; state assumptions explicitly.

## Win Themes for This Vertical

- **Capacity transfer as a deliverable** — the agency commits to a measurable transfer of operating capability to a government counterpart team, not just training.
- **Sustainability beyond donor cycles** — the operating model survives the engagement and the funding cycle.
- **Compliance with procurement rules** — every form, every annex, every signature in the exact format the framework requires.
- **Inter-ministry coordination realism** — the agency understands that government integrations require political coordination, not just technical integration.
- **Audit-grade evidence** — every claim is backed by evidence the Auditor-General would accept.

## Anti-Patterns Specific to This Vertical

- Submitting a private-sector style proposal that ignores the procurement framework.
- Treating capacity transfer as a training module.
- Promising regulator approvals the agency cannot influence.
- Assuming cloud-only architecture when government policy requires on-shore data.
- Ignoring the political timeline (elections, budget cycle, financial year-end).
- Pricing without acknowledging the long approval cycle through the line ministry, accounting officer, and procurement function.

## Discovery Questions Specific to Public Sector

In addition to the standard SaaS discovery question bank:

- Which procurement framework applies and what is the evaluation method?
- What is the financial year cycle and how does it affect the engagement timeline?
- Which line ministry and accounting officer are involved in approvals?
- Which national systems must the SaaS integrate with (IFMIS, HRIS, national ID, payment gateway)?
- What is the government's stance on cloud hosting for this category of data?
- What capacity-transfer outcomes does the buyer expect, and to whom?
- What sustainability arrangements exist beyond the engagement period?
- Which oversight bodies (Auditor-General, parliamentary committees, donor) will scrutinise the engagement?
- What is the political timeline (election, budget speech, sector reform deadline) that affects priorities?
