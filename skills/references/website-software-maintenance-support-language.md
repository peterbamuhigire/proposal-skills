# Website and Software Maintenance Support Language

Derived from local HTML extractions on customer service commitments, UX writing, SaaS maintenance, application feedback, web forms, and operational support.

## Support Scope Categories

- Corrective maintenance: bug fixes, broken links, form errors, display defects, access issues, and incident correction.
- Preventive maintenance: backups, security updates, dependency checks, monitoring, log review, and vulnerability response.
- Adaptive maintenance: browser, device, API, platform, payment, hosting, or regulatory changes.
- Perfective maintenance: usability improvements, content updates, analytics-driven optimisation, performance tuning, and backlog items.
- AI operations: evaluation refresh, prompt/model updates, data drift checks, usage monitoring, cost review, fallback testing, and human-review adjustment.

## SLA Language

| Severity | Example | Response Target | Resolution Approach |
|---|---|---|---|
| Critical | Service unavailable, payment failure, security incident | Acknowledge urgently within agreed cover hours | Diagnose, apply workaround, escalate, and update at agreed intervals. |
| High | Major feature blocked for many users | Same business day or agreed target | Fix or workaround with client verification. |
| Medium | Functional issue with limited workaround | Within agreed support window | Schedule fix in maintenance cycle. |
| Low | Content, cosmetic, or minor usability issue | Next planned update | Add to backlog or content-update queue. |

## Proposal Commitments

- "All support requests will be logged, classified, assigned, and closed only after verification."
- "Where immediate resolution is not possible, we will provide a practical workaround and a clear next-update time."
- "Escalations will move from first-line diagnosis to specialist review and management visibility according to severity."
- "Monthly support reporting will summarise issues, response performance, recurring causes, and recommended improvements."

## Client Responsibilities

- Timely access to hosting, domains, CMS, analytics, source systems, and third-party accounts.
- Named focal point for approvals and prioritisation.
- Content, legal, policy, and brand decisions where the supplier is not the owner.
- Prompt review of incident updates, test releases, and closure confirmations.

## Pricing Notes

- Price support separately from build scope.
- Separate included fixes from new features.
- State cover hours, channels, severity levels, response targets, excluded licences, and third-party costs.
- Include a retainer or support bank where demand is uncertain.

## Red Flags

- "Free support" with no end date, scope, or response target.
- Hosting, licences, backups, security, or content updates treated as implied.
- No escalation path for critical incidents.
- No maintenance allowance for AI, integrations, plugins, APIs, or changing third-party platforms.
