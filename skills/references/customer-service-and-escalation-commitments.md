# Customer Service and Escalation Commitments

Derived from local HTML extractions on customer service language, active listening, follow-up, referral, escalation, complaint handling, and recovery. Use this to write professional service commitments in proposals.

## Service Communication Principles

- Acknowledge the issue promptly and specifically.
- Explain what is known, what is not yet known, and what will happen next.
- Give a realistic next-update time and keep it.
- Use warm handoffs when another person or team must take over.
- Close the loop only after the client confirms the issue is resolved or the agreed action is complete.

## Escalation Model

| Step | Commitment |
|---|---|
| Intake | Log request, capture impact, classify severity, and confirm receipt. |
| Diagnosis | Review evidence, reproduce issue where applicable, and identify workaround. |
| Specialist Review | Route to the correct technical, content, policy, or management owner. |
| Client Update | Provide status, next action, owner, and next-update time. |
| Resolution | Apply fix, confirm service restoration, and document root cause if material. |
| Closure | Verify with the client, record lessons, and add preventive action where needed. |

## Complaint Recovery Language

- "We will acknowledge the concern, confirm the facts, agree the corrective action, and keep the client informed until closure."
- "Where our work has caused an issue, we will state this clearly and focus on correction, prevention, and transparent follow-up."
- "Where the issue depends on a third party, we will coordinate the handoff and keep the client updated rather than leaving them to chase the supplier alone."

## Known Problem Language

- "If a known issue affects users, we will communicate the impact, workaround, expected next update, and resolution path."
- "For critical incidents, management visibility and technical escalation will run in parallel."

## Boundaries

- Set limits professionally when requests fall outside scope, support hours, agreed channels, or lawful/ethical conduct.
- Offer a practical next step: change request, paid support ticket, scheduled enhancement, or management review.
- Do not promise authority, access, or timing that the support model cannot deliver.
