# SaaS Business Case and ROI Template

Business-case template for SaaS implementation and SaaS product-development proposals. Used inside:

- The Executive Summary's "Value at Stake" block.
- The Financial Proposal's price-defence narrative.
- The standalone business-case annex when procurement requires it.
- The pre-BAFO conversation with the buyer's CFO or Finance Director.

The business case has two flavours depending on whether the client buys SaaS for internal use or for resale.

## Anti-Patterns

- Single-point estimates without sensitivity — buyer's CFO will reject.
- Benchmark comparisons without internal-control evidence (A/B, holdout, propensity-matched).
- Investor-grade vocabulary (Rule of 40, magic number) for non-investor-grade buyers.
- Conflating internal-use TCO with resale unit economics.
- Quoting LTV without naming the gross margin and churn assumption underneath.
- "Payback within 12 months" with no per-quarter mechanism stated.

## Usage Notes

- Use Flavour A for internal-use SaaS (most government, NGO, and many private-sector clients adopting SaaS for operations).
- Use Flavour B when the client is building or rebuilding a revenue-generating SaaS product (banks launching SaaS to corporate clients, insurers launching broker portals as SaaS, telcos launching SaaS for SMEs, EdTech, FinTech).
- Use both flavours, side by side, when the engagement spans both (e.g., a bank adopting a SaaS core for internal operations and simultaneously building a SaaS product for its corporate customers).
