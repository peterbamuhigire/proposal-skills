# Service Design Methodology Module

Derived from local HTML extractions on mapping experiences, service design thinking, stakeholder mapping, journey maps, service blueprints, co-creation, UX writing, and implementation planning.

## When to Use

Use this module in proposals for customer experience, citizen services, digital services, clinics, support centres, onboarding, complaint handling, case management, portals, websites, SaaS, and AI-enabled services.

## Proposal Phases

| Phase | Purpose | Core Artefacts |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Frame the Service Challenge | Define outcomes, actors, channels, constraints, and success measures. | Service brief, stakeholder map, research plan. |
| 2. Understand the Current Journey | Gather evidence from users, staff, records, analytics, support logs, and observation. | Journey map, touchpoint inventory, pain-point register. |
| 3. Blueprint the Service System | Connect frontstage experience to backstage process, data, roles, policies, and tools. | Service blueprint, handoff map, failure-point analysis. |
| 4. Co-Create Improvements | Work with users and delivery teams to generate practical options. | Workshop outputs, prioritised opportunity list, concept cards. |
| 5. Prototype and Validate | Test risky service moments before committing to full rollout. | Prototype, test findings, revised service concept. |
| 6. Implement and Embed | Translate design into operating model, training, content, systems, metrics, and governance. | Implementation roadmap, training plan, measurement dashboard. |

## Artefact Standards

- Journey maps must show actors, stages, touchpoints, emotions, pain points, evidence, and opportunities.
- Service blueprints must show frontstage actions, backstage activities, systems, data, policies, handoffs, and support roles.
- Co-creation outputs must be converted into decisions, not left as workshop notes.
- Prototypes must test assumptions that affect cost, adoption, risk, or service quality.

## Acceptance Criteria

- Reduced wait time, handoffs, errors, complaints, drop-off, or duplicated effort.
- Improved completion, resolution, satisfaction, staff confidence, compliance, or transparency.
- Clear ownership for each service moment and escalation path.
- Measurable baseline and review cadence.

## Proposal Language

- "We will treat the service as an end-to-end experience, connecting what users see with the backstage processes, data, policies, and roles that make the service work."
- "The journey map will not be a presentation artefact only; it will drive the service blueprint, implementation roadmap, and measurable improvement backlog."

## Red Flags

- Journey mapping without implementation ownership.
- Workshops without evidence from actual users or frontline staff.
- Digital redesign that ignores support, content, handoffs, and policy constraints.
- Service promises with no staffing, budget, training, or measurement plan.
