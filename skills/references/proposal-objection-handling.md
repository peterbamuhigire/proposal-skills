# Proposal Objection Handling

Derived from local HTML extractions on sales objections, customer service recovery, risk perception, design-decision facilitation, and premium pricing. Use this to handle concerns ethically inside proposals, clarification responses, presentations, and negotiation notes.

## Response Pattern

1. Acknowledge the concern without defensiveness.
2. Clarify the comparison, assumption, or risk behind it.
3. Reframe the issue in terms of value, risk, timing, quality, or total ownership.
4. Provide evidence: method, comparable experience, acceptance criteria, QA, or support.
5. Offer a trade-off or decision path.
6. Confirm the next step.

## Common Objections

| Objection | Ethical Response |
|---|---|
| Price is high | Compare price with value, risk, rework, support, and total ownership; offer scope options rather than hidden discounts. |
| Timeline is long | Show critical path, review gates, dependencies, and what can be compressed only with added cost or reduced assurance. |
| Timeline is too short | State delivery risk and propose phased scope, MVP, pilot, or staged acceptance. |
| Team seems small | Explain role allocation, senior review, specialist inputs, and escalation access. |
| Team seems expensive | Tie seniority to risk reduction, faster decisions, fewer defects, and stronger handover. |
| Technology choice is risky | Explain selection criteria, alternatives considered, integration path, maintainability, security, and exit options. |
| AI quality is uncertain | Use test cases, evaluation thresholds, human review, monitoring, fallback, and update cadence. |
| Local context may affect delivery | Address language, infrastructure, approvals, public holidays, field access, regulations, and stakeholder expectations. |
| Support is unclear | Define channels, severity levels, response targets, responsibilities, exclusions, and escalation. |
| Procurement needs comparability | Provide compliant tables, assumptions, option boundaries, and evidence that supports scoring. |

## Price Objection Language

- "The fee is tied to the level of discovery, QA, senior input, and post-delivery support required to achieve the stated outcome."
- "If the priority is to reduce cost, the safest route is to adjust scope or assurance level explicitly rather than underfund the same scope."
- "The lower-cost alternative should be compared against the risk of rework, delayed launch, lower adoption, and additional client management effort."

## Risk Objection Language

- "We agree that this is a material risk. Our approach controls it through [mitigation], [approval gate], and [fallback]."
- "The proposal separates the high-risk assumptions early so the client can make an informed decision before full implementation."

## Red Lines

- Do not dismiss valid concerns.
- Do not promise impossible speed, quality, or support.
- Do not imply that choosing a cheaper supplier is irresponsible.
- Do not use fear where a clear trade-off would be more honest.
