# Premium Rate Justification Framework

Derived from local HTML extractions on marketing measurement, brand promise, sales objection handling, impact mapping, engineering strategy, SaaS operations, and customer service. Use this to justify premium rates without hype.

## Premium Value Stack

Premium pricing is defensible when the proposal proves one or more of these value layers:

- Strategic value: revenue, growth, compliance, trust, operational control, or market position.
- Risk reduction: fewer failures, better governance, stronger QA, safer launch, and clearer escalation.
- Speed with control: faster decision-making without skipping discovery, testing, or approval gates.
- Senior expertise: experienced judgement on architecture, service design, AI, pricing, change, or delivery.
- Total cost of ownership: maintainability, documentation, training, support, security, and upgrade path.
- Measurement capability: baselines, success metrics, review cadence, and value realisation tracking.
- Service quality: responsiveness, recovery, communication, and long-term relationship management.

## Proof Ladder

| Level | Proof Required |
|---|---|
| Claim | State the premium benefit in client terms. |
| Mechanism | Explain how the method, team, tool, or process creates that benefit. |
| Evidence | Show comparable results, benchmarks, prototypes, audits, or validated assumptions. |
| Control | Show QA, governance, risk, support, and acceptance criteria. |
| Commercial Link | Connect the fee to the value protected or created. |

## Pricing Options

Use options when the client needs choice but the firm must protect quality.

| Option | Use When | Include |
|---|---|---|
| Essential | Budget is tight and risk is moderate. | Minimum viable scope, core deliverables, limited support. |
| Recommended | Client needs a credible outcome. | Full discovery, delivery, QA, handover, support, and measurement. |
| Enhanced | Stakes are high or speed/risk matters. | Senior review, expanded testing, faster cadence, stronger support, extra optimisation. |

## Price Defence Language

- "The fee reflects the level of discovery, senior review, testing, and handover required to make the deliverable usable after submission."
- "A lower-cost version is possible if we reduce scope, assurance level, review cycles, or support. We would not recommend reducing the controls that protect launch quality."
- "The commercial comparison should include not only build cost, but also rework risk, adoption effort, support burden, and total cost of ownership."

## Discount Rules

- Do not discount the same scope without changing a commercial variable.
- Acceptable trade-offs: reduced scope, longer timeline, fewer workshops, smaller support window, fewer revisions, client-supplied content/data, or faster payment.
- Never remove risk controls quietly; state the consequence.

## Review Questions

- What measurable value does the client gain or protect?
- Which work would fail first if the budget were cut?
- Does the price include enough discovery, QA, support, and management?
- Can procurement defend the price against a cheaper bid?
- Are exclusions and client responsibilities clear?
