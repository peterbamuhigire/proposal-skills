# SaaS Customer Success Engagement Package

Customer success and adoption scope for SaaS implementation proposals. Used inside the Methodology, Work Plan, Financial Proposal, and the Trust and Compliance commitments section. The customer-success engagement is what separates a SaaS implementation that lands from one that delivers — and where premium agencies defend premium fees.

## Why Customer Success Belongs in the Proposal, Not the Sales Pitch

- The buyer's CFO asks: "What happens after go-live?" — the customer success package is the answer.
- Adoption risk is the single largest driver of value destruction in SaaS engagements.
- Net retention and expansion revenue are downstream of customer success quality.
- Premium fees are defensible when the post-go-live engagement is named, staffed, and measurable.

## Customer Success Engagement Package — Standard Structure

### 1. Onboarding (Weeks 0–6)

| Activity | Owner | Output |
|---|---|---|
| Welcome and orientation | Customer Success Manager (CSM) | Welcome pack, account map |
| Discovery refresh | CSM + Solution lead | Updated value-at-stake document |
| Account map: sponsors, champions, blockers | CSM | Account map artefact |
| Success plan v1 | CSM + Sponsor | Success plan signed |
| Activation playbook for the buyer's team | CSM | Activation runbook |
| First admin training | Configuration lead | Trained admins (named) |

### 2. Activation (Weeks 4–12)

| Activity | Owner | Output |
|---|---|---|
| First-value milestone defined and measured | CSM | TTV achieved or escalated |
| In-product behavioural telemetry instrumented | Solution lead | Adoption dashboard live |
| Frontline-user training waves | Training lead | Training completion records |
| Adoption nudges (lifecycle email, in-app, manager comms) | CSM + Lifecycle program | First-value engagement metrics |
| Issue triage runbook in use | Support lead | Triage SLA met |

### 3. Success Plan and Operating Cadence (ongoing)

| Cadence | Audience | Purpose | Output |
|---|---|---|---|
| Weekly during implementation | Sponsor + delivery lead | Status, risks, decisions | Weekly status note |
| Monthly during stabilisation | Sponsor + CSM | Adoption metrics, escalations | Monthly success review |
| Quarterly | Steering committee | Value realisation, expansion, renewal posture | QBR pack |
| Annually | Sponsor + CFO + CSM | Strategic review, contract renewal | Renewal proposal |

### 4. Expansion Plays

For each tier-2 capability and each adjacent business unit, the customer-success engagement carries a defined expansion play:

| Trigger | Play | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| Adoption threshold met (e.g., 80% active users) | Capability-expansion conversation | CSM + Account director |
| Adjacent business unit signals interest | Cross-sell discovery | Account director |
| Usage approaching tier ceiling | Tier-upgrade conversation | CSM |
| Annual contract anniversary 90 days out | Renewal and expansion proposal | Account director |
| Major regulatory change in the vertical | Compliance-feature expansion | Domain SME + Account director |

### 5. Save Plays (Churn-Risk Response)

For each defined churn-risk signal, the customer-success engagement carries a defined save play:

| Risk Signal | Save Play | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| Sponsor departs the buyer organisation | New-sponsor outreach within 5 working days | Account director |
| Active-user count drops > 20% in a month | Adoption diagnostic and recovery plan within 10 working days | CSM |
| Support tickets spike on a single issue | Engineering-priority escalation + customer comms | Support lead + Solution lead |
| QBR signals dissatisfaction | Executive sponsor outreach within 5 working days | Managing partner / Account director |
| Renewal negotiations stalling | BAFO trade-and-anchor strategy | Account director |

### 6. Health Scoring

A composite customer health score tracked in the CRM and reviewed monthly:

| Component | Weight | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Usage and engagement | 30% | Product telemetry |
| Adoption breadth (features, business units) | 20% | Product telemetry + account map |
| Support sentiment | 15% | Support tickets + CSAT |
| Sponsor stability | 15% | Account map maintenance |
| Renewal posture | 10% | Account director assessment |
| Expansion progress | 10% | Pipeline movement |

The composite drives a Red / Amber / Green status, with named recovery actions and owners for Amber and Red.

### 7. Customer Success Roles

| Role | Responsibility | Effort |
|---|---|---|
| Customer Success Manager (CSM) | Day-to-day owner of the customer relationship, success plan, adoption | 0.2–0.5 FTE per account at enterprise scale |
| Customer Success Engineer | Technical depth on configuration, integration questions, expansion enablement | Shared across accounts |
| Account Director | Commercial owner: renewal, expansion, escalation | 0.1–0.2 FTE per enterprise account |
| Executive Sponsor (Agency Side) | Symbolic and substantive: present at QBR, available for escalation | 4–8 hours per quarter per major account |
| Customer Success Operations | Tooling, telemetry, reporting, playbook maintenance | Shared function |

### 8. Customer Success Tooling Stack

| Function | Tool Category | Note |
|---|---|---|
| CRM | Account + opportunity + activity tracking | Single source of truth |
| Customer success platform | Health scoring, playbook execution, activity reminders | Optional for SMB, essential for enterprise |
| Product analytics | Usage and adoption telemetry | Mandatory |
| Support / ticketing | Ticket queue, SLA tracking | Mandatory |
| Communication | Email, in-app, SMS as applicable | Lifecycle program |
| Survey / NPS | Periodic customer sentiment | Quarterly minimum |

## Commercial Treatment in the Financial Proposal

- Customer success engagement priced as a named workstream, not bundled invisibly.
- Tier-appropriate intensity: Light Touch for SMB, Standard for mid-market, High Touch for enterprise.
- Annual renewal review point — the customer-success engagement can be re-scoped each year.
- Optional retainer of customer-success engineering hours for configuration help.

## Anti-Patterns

- Treating customer success as an unfunded warranty — leads to under-staffing and adoption failure.
- Single CSM owning 30+ accounts without a tiering model.
- Success plans that name no measurable outcomes.
- QBRs that are demos with new charts rather than honest value-realisation reviews.
- No defined save play for the predictable churn signals (sponsor change, usage drop).
- Bundling support and success — they have different goals and need different staffing.
