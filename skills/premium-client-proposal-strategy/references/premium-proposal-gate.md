# Premium Proposal Gate

Use this gate for high-value proposals where the firm must win on value, trust, and strategic fit rather than low price.

## Buyer Logic

- Name the real decision makers and what each must believe to say yes.
- Separate evaluator compliance criteria from executive buying criteria.
- Identify risk: implementation failure, reputational exposure, budget scrutiny, weak adoption, poor evidence, or supplier unreliability.
- State the next decision the proposal must earn: shortlist, meeting, negotiation, award, pilot, or board approval.

## Executive Positioning

- Open with the client's problem and desired outcome, not the firm's biography.
- State the governing idea in one sentence.
- Make the value proposition specific: measurable improvement, risk reduction, speed, control, compliance, revenue, or confidence.
- Use proof that matches the buyer's risk level: comparable work, metrics, named methodology, senior team, governance, and sample deliverables.

## Methodology

- Each phase must produce a reviewable artefact.
- Explain why the phase exists, what will be done, and what value it creates.
- Include discovery and diagnosis before prescription where the problem is complex.
- For growth, premium positioning, marketing, pricing, or sales assignments, include buyer research, offer review, pricing review, channel plan, sales process, pilot/testing, and measurement cadence.

## Commercial Strategy

- Price senior expertise, discovery, analysis, facilitation, validation, revisions, QA, and handover explicitly.
- Do not reduce price without reducing scope, timeline, service level, risk, or payment terms.
- Tie payment milestones to valuable deliverables.
- Avoid vague "discounts"; use commercial options with clear trade-offs.

## Language

- Sound calm, specific, and expert.
- Avoid hype, begging language, generic adjectives, and unsupported superiority claims.
- Use the client's language for the problem and the firm's language for the method.
- Make invisible service quality tangible: process, cadence, governance, evidence, escalation, and deliverables.
