# Growth, Profit, and Disruption Proposal Patterns

Source set: *Growth Engineering*, *Remarkable Business Growth*, *Make Phenomenal Profits*, *Dare to Disrupt*, and *Think Deeper*.

Use this reference when a proposal concerns growth strategy, revenue acceleration, product-led growth, AI-enabled products, commercial transformation, pricing/profit improvement, innovation, disruption, or business-model redesign.

## Proposal Translation

These books strengthen proposals by shifting language from activity to commercial system:

- not "develop a marketing strategy" but "build a measurable acquisition, conversion, retention, referral, and revenue system";
- not "support digital transformation" but "redesign the operating model, customer journey, data capture, and execution cadence";
- not "improve profitability" but "identify the pricing, packaging, retention, frequency, mix, and margin levers with the highest profit impact";
- not "be innovative" but "create a disciplined mechanism that turns ideas into tested offers, decisions, and operating changes."

## Diagnostic Lenses

| Lens | Core question | Evidence to request |
|---|---|---|
| Growth engineering | What user behaviours, data events, experiments, and product loops drive growth? | Product analytics, cohort retention, conversion events, experiment logs |
| Remarkable growth system | Is growth systemised across strategy, marketing, sales, delivery, service, systems, and metrics? | Sales process, CRM, service metrics, dashboards, content, pipeline reports |
| Profit levers | Which small changes in price, packaging, mix, conversion, retention, frequency, or cost compound profit? | Unit economics, margins, price tests, retention data, channel economics |
| Disruption readiness | Is the client improving the old model or changing the basis of competition? | Business model, customer journey, leadership, culture, technology, operating model |
| Deeper thinking | Which assumptions should be challenged before recommending action? | Assumption map, role-reversal review, competitor response, future-state scenarios |

## Methodology Building Blocks

### Growth Strategy Engagements

- Map the current growth engine: target segment, positioning, acquisition, activation, conversion, retention, referral, and revenue.
- Build a KPI tree from North Star metric to channel and product behaviours.
- Identify growth constraints: weak positioning, wrong segment, poor activation, churn, channel dependency, no follow-up, weak service delivery, or poor data.
- Design experiments with hypothesis, audience, metric, duration, decision rule, and owner.
- Produce a growth operating cadence: dashboard, meeting rhythm, backlog, owners, and review cycle.

### Profit Improvement Engagements

- Decompose profit into volume, price, mix, margin, conversion, purchase frequency, retention, and cost-to-serve.
- Test pricing and packaging options: monthly versus annual, bundle versus single, entry tier versus premium tier.
- Identify 15 percent improvement opportunities across multiple levers instead of relying on one unrealistic breakthrough.
- Link customer experience to retention and referral economics.

### Disruption and Transformation Engagements

- Separate evolutionary improvement from real transformation.
- Assess leadership, culture, people, systems, customer experience, technology, and execution discipline.
- Identify how the client could change the rules of its category: access model, pricing model, delivery model, support model, speed, convenience, or ownership model.
- Translate creative ideas into experiments, pilots, investment cases, and operating changes.

## Executive Summary Language

Use this shape when the proposal is commercial or growth-oriented:

"[Client] does not need another list of campaigns. It needs a measurable growth system that connects customer insight, offer design, acquisition, conversion, retention, service delivery, and management dashboards. Our proposed approach will identify the highest-leverage growth and profit constraints, test priority options, and leave [client] with an operating cadence for sustained revenue improvement."

## Red Team Checks

- Does the proposal name the growth engine, or only list activities?
- Does the methodology include experiments and decision rules?
- Does it connect growth claims to metrics, cohorts, conversion, retention, and profit?
- Does it distinguish incremental improvement from disruption?
- Does it challenge assumptions before recommending a solution?
- Does the proposal show how the client will keep improving after the engagement?
