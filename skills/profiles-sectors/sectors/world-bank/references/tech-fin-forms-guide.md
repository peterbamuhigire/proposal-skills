# TECH and FIN Forms Guide — World Bank Standard RFP for Consulting Services

Complete guide to each standard form in a World Bank consulting proposal. Each form maps to a proposal section skill; this reference covers the World Bank-specific requirements and best practices for each form.

---

## Technical Proposal Forms

### TECH-1: Technical Proposal Submission Form

**What it is:** The cover letter / submission statement.

**Must include:**
- Confirmation of compliance with all RFP requirements
- Declaration of absence of conflict of interest
- Statement that the proposal is valid for the period stated in the Data Sheet
- Authorised signatory with name, title, and date

**Maps to:** `01-cover-letter/SKILL.md`

**Best practice:** Keep concise. This is a compliance document, not a selling document. However, ensure every required declaration is present — missing declarations can cause rejection at preliminary examination.

---

### TECH-2A: Organisation of the Firm

**What it is:** Brief description of the consulting firm.

**Must include:**
- Legal status and ownership structure
- Year of establishment
- Number of staff (total and by discipline)
- Office locations relevant to the assignment
- Brief description of core service areas
- Association arrangements (if submitting as a JV or with sub-consultants)

**Maps to:** `04-firm-profile/SKILL.md`

**Scoring notes:** This is part of the "Specific Experience of the Firm" criterion (typically 10-20 points). Keep the organisational description focused on relevance to this assignment. A 50-page corporate brochure scores no higher than a tight 2-page profile that demonstrates direct capability.

---

### TECH-2B: Consultant's Experience

**What it is:** List of relevant assignments completed in the last 5-10 years.

**Must include for each assignment:**
- Assignment name and country
- Client name and contact details (for reference checks)
- Approximate contract value
- Duration (start and end dates)
- Number of professional staff-months provided
- Name of associated consultants (if JV)
- Brief description of the assignment and your firm's role
- Narrative description of actual services performed

**Maps to:** `05-relevant-experience/SKILL.md`

**Scoring strategy:**
- Select projects most analogous to THIS assignment — same type of work, same sector, similar complexity
- Prioritise recent projects (last 5-7 years)
- State quantified outcomes for every project (the outcomes rule)
- Include projects in the same country or East African region — regional experience is scored
- 8-12 projects is typically sufficient; quality over quantity
- Repeat clients signal quality — note if the same client engaged you multiple times

---

### TECH-3A: Comments on the Terms of Reference

**What it is:** The consultant's technical commentary on the ToR.

**Must include:**
- Demonstration of understanding of the assignment
- Identification of any gaps, ambiguities, or issues in the ToR
- Suggested improvements or clarifications
- Any additional tasks or deliverables the consultant recommends

**Maps to:** `03-understanding-of-assignment/SKILL.md`

**Scoring strategy — this is where easy points are won or lost:**
- **Never** just write "We agree with the ToR" — this scores 1-2
- Identify 3-5 substantive technical observations
- Structure each comment as: **ToR Reference → Observation → Recommendation**
- Show that you understand the assignment at a deeper level than the ToR itself
- Propose improvements that add value without expanding scope unreasonably
- If the ToR has unrealistic timelines, flag them professionally with alternatives

---

### TECH-3B: Comments on Counterpart Staff and Facilities

**What it is:** The consultant's assessment of what the client needs to provide.

**Must include:**
- Specific counterpart staff requirements (roles, time commitment, access)
- Data and documentation the client must make available
- Office space, equipment, and facilities needed
- Transport and logistics support expected
- Any assumptions about client-side availability

**Best practice:** Be specific and realistic. Vague requests ("the client will provide necessary support") leave gaps. Specific requests ("a dedicated counterpart officer available 3 days/week with access to the MIS database") demonstrate planning maturity.

---

### TECH-4: Description of Approach, Methodology, and Work Plan

**What it is:** The heart of the technical proposal. Typically carries **30-50% of all technical points.**

**Must include:**
- **Technical approach** — the conceptual framework and analytical logic
- **Methodology** — specific methods, tools, techniques for each task
- **Work plan** — sequence of activities, timeline, milestones
- **Organisation and staffing** — how the team is structured and deployed

**Maps to:** `06-methodology/SKILL.md`

**This is the section that wins or loses the proposal.** See the evaluation scoring guide (`references/evaluation-scoring-guide.md`) for detailed scoring strategy.

**Critical quality tests:**
1. Is it tailored to THIS assignment or could it apply to any similar ToR?
2. Does every phase produce a named deliverable?
3. Are tools and frameworks named specifically (not "we will analyse")?
4. Are risks identified and mitigated?
5. Does it show innovation or added value beyond the ToR minimum?
6. Is it consistent with TECH-5 (team), TECH-7 (staffing schedule), and TECH-8 (work schedule)?

---

### TECH-5: Team Composition and Task Assignments

**What it is:** Summary table of all proposed key professional staff with their roles.

**Must include:**
- Name of each key professional
- Position/title in the project
- Specific tasks assigned (by ToR task number where possible)
- Input in person-months (home and field separately)

**Maps to:** `07-team-composition/SKILL.md`

**Scoring notes:** Evaluators cross-reference this table with TECH-4 (methodology) and TECH-6 (CVs). Inconsistencies between the three are penalised. The team composition must logically match the methodology — if Phase 2 requires a financial analyst, that person must appear in TECH-5 with the right input.

---

### TECH-6: Curriculum Vitae (CV) of Proposed Professional Staff

**What it is:** One form per key professional. This is typically part of the **highest-weighted criterion (30-60 points).**

**Must include:**
- Full name, date of birth, nationality
- Education (degrees, institutions, years)
- Professional certifications and memberships
- Countries of work experience
- Languages and proficiency levels
- Employment record (employer, positions, dates)
- Detailed assignment record with:
  - Assignment name, country, client
  - Position held and tasks performed
  - Duration
- Signed certification that information is correct
- Statement of availability

**Maps to:** `07-team-composition/SKILL.md`

**Scoring strategy — evaluators score three sub-criteria for each person:**

| Sub-criterion | Weight | What Scores High |
|---|---|---|
| **General qualifications** | 20-30% | Advanced degree, relevant certifications, 15+ years, breadth of experience |
| **Adequacy for the assignment** | 50-60% | Specific projects matching the ToR tasks for THIS position. The evaluator draws a line from CV projects to ToR tasks — the more direct the match, the higher the score. |
| **Regional/country experience** | 10-20% | Named projects in the same country or East Africa. Local language ability if relevant. |

**Common mistakes that cost points:**
- CVs with 20 years of experience but none directly matching the specific ToR tasks → score 1-2 on "adequacy"
- Junior staff assigned to complex technical tasks → score 1 on overall team composition
- CV does not state availability → evaluator cannot confirm the person will be there
- Assignments listed without outcomes or specific role description → evaluator cannot assess relevance

---

### TECH-7: Staffing Schedule

**What it is:** Matrix showing person-months per position per month/phase.

**Must include:**
- Each key professional listed
- Input in person-months broken down by month or phase
- Distinction between home-based and field-based input
- Total person-months per position and per phase
- Overall total person-months

**Maps to:** `08-work-plan/SKILL.md`

**Consistency rule:** TECH-7 must be consistent with TECH-4 (methodology phases), TECH-5 (task assignments), TECH-8 (work schedule), and the financial proposal (FIN-3 remuneration). Any inconsistency will be noted and may reduce the methodology or team score.

---

### TECH-8: Work Schedule (Gantt Chart)

**What it is:** Visual timeline of all activities and deliverables.

**Must include:**
- All methodology phases and activities
- Duration of each activity (bar chart)
- Deliverable submission points (milestones)
- Client review and approval periods
- Key meetings (inception, mid-term, final presentation)

**Maps to:** `08-work-plan/SKILL.md`

**Best practice:**
- Mark the critical path
- Show dependencies between activities
- Allow realistic time for client review (typically 2-3 weeks per major deliverable)
- Include fieldwork logistics time (travel within East Africa)
- Be consistent with the contract duration stated in the Data Sheet

## Financial Proposal Forms

Financial proposals are submitted in a **separate sealed envelope** and opened only after technical evaluation.

### FIN-1: Financial Proposal Submission Form

**What it is:** Cover letter stating the total financial proposal amount.

**Must include:**
- Total amount in the currency specified in the Data Sheet
- Confirmation of compliance with financial requirements
- Authorised signatory

### FIN-2: Summary of Costs

**What it is:** High-level cost summary.

**Must include:**
- Total remuneration costs
- Total reimbursable expenses
- Total miscellaneous expenses
- Grand total
- Taxes (if applicable, as specified in the Data Sheet)

### FIN-3: Breakdown of Remuneration

**What it is:** Staff costs by position.

**Must include for each staff member:**
- Name and position
- Input in person-months (must match TECH-7)
- Billing rate (per person-month or per day, as specified)
- Total remuneration per person
- Subtotals by staff category (international, national)

**Consistency rule:** Person-months in FIN-3 must exactly match TECH-7. Any discrepancy raises questions and may be flagged during evaluation.

### FIN-4: Breakdown of Reimbursable Expenses

**What it is:** All non-staff direct costs.

**Typical line items:**
- International travel (flights, per trip)
- Local travel (vehicle hire, fuel, domestic flights)
- Per diem / daily subsistence allowance
- Accommodation
- Communication costs
- Report production and printing
- Survey/data collection costs
- Equipment rental
- Visa and work permit costs

**Best practice for East African assignments:**
- Use realistic per diem rates (reference UN DSA rates for the country)
- Include domestic travel costs for multi-site fieldwork
- Budget for workshop/meeting costs if the methodology includes stakeholder events
- Include contingency for currency fluctuation if pricing in USD but incurring local costs

### FIN-5: Miscellaneous Expenses

**What it is:** Any costs not covered in FIN-3 or FIN-4.

**May include:**
- Translation and interpretation services
- Software licences
- Insurance
- Bank charges
- Any other assignment-specific costs

## Pricing Strategy Under QCBS

| Factor | Guidance |
|---|---|
| **Do not underprice** | Abnormally low proposals are rejected. Price must be defensible. |
| **Technical quality matters more** | At 80/20, a 10-point technical advantage is worth 40 points of financial advantage. Invest in quality. |
| **Be internally consistent** | Person-months in FIN-3 = TECH-7 = TECH-5. Billing rates should reflect the seniority of proposed staff. |
| **Price competitively, not cheapest** | The optimal QCBS strategy is to be within 10-15% of the median price while maximising technical score. |
| **Lump-sum vs. time-based** | For lump-sum contracts, the total price is fixed — get the scope right. For time-based, actual inputs are reimbursed — budget realistically. |
