# Contract-Management and Payment Linkage

Reference material for the ppda-uganda sector skill. Closes the financial-accountability gap in a Form 49 Contract Management Plan: how milestone payments, retention, performance and advance-payment security, and funds-flow reconciliation are structured so that what is delivered, what is certified, and what is paid stay aligned. Applies to PPDA central, local-government, and (in adapted form) donor-funded contracts.

Sources: PPDA Act (Cap. 205, as amended) and Regulations; PPDA Form 49 (Contract Management Plan); Local Governments (Financial and Accounting) Regulations 2007 (SI 25/2007) commitment/vote control; donor disbursement and fiduciary frameworks. ## 1. The gap this closes

A Form 49 Contract Management Plan typically lists deliverables, dates, and a payment schedule, but is often weak on the **financial-control linkage** — whether each payment is backed by a certified milestone, an uncommitted balance in the quarter, retained security against defects, and a reconciled funds-flow position. This reference supplies that linkage so the contract-management plan is financially accountable, not merely a schedule.

---

## 2. Milestone-based payment schedules

- Tie **every payment to a specific, verifiable deliverable or milestone**, not to elapsed time alone. For consultancy, link percentage or lump-sum instalments to accepted outputs (inception report, draft, final); for works, to certified physical progress; for supplies, to inspection/installation certificates.
- Each milestone needs an **acceptance gate**: who certifies, against what acceptance criteria, on what form. Payment is triggered by the **certificate**, not by the calendar.
- At local-government level, a certified milestone is **necessary but not sufficient**: the payment must also fit an **approved commitment requisition and the uncommitted balance in the quarter's expenditure limit** (Commitment Control System). Phase milestones so each falls in a quarter that can carry its committed cost.

| Contract type | Typical payment linkage |
|---|---|
| **Consultancy (lump sum)** | Instalments on accepted deliverables (e.g. inception / interim / final) |
| **Consultancy (time-based)** | Certified timesheets/inputs, with output gates |
| **Supplies** | Inspection, delivery, installation certificates; stage-payment certificates |
| **Works** | Interim payment certificates on certified progress; final on taking-over |

---

## 3. Retention

- A **retention** percentage is withheld from each interim payment (commonly applied to works and some supply/installation contracts) and held until completion to secure performance and defect rectification.
- Retention is released against the **defects-liability / warranty** milestones: a first portion at taking-over / completion, the balance at the end of the defects-liability period on a clean defects position.
- Record retention withheld and released as a running balance in the contract-management plan, reconciled to the payment certificates so the held amount is auditable.

---

## 4. Security instruments

| Instrument | What it secures | Note |
|---|---|---|
| **Performance security** | The provider's performance of the contract | Under PPDA, frequently a **Performance Securing Declaration** (default-triggered 3-year suspension from government procurement) rather than a bank guarantee; valid until obligations and defects liability are complete |
| **Advance-payment guarantee** | Recovery of any advance paid | Required where an advance is made; the advance is recovered by deduction from subsequent certificates |
| **Bid security / securing declaration** | Bid validity (pre-award) | Belongs to the bidding phase, not contract management |

- **Advance-payment ceiling:** advances above **30% of the contract sum** require approval from the Permanent Secretary / Secretary to the Treasury (central PPDA). Structure any advance request within this limit unless that approval is genuinely anticipated, and pair it with an advance-payment guarantee and a recovery schedule.

---

## 5. Funds-flow reconciliation against milestones

The accountability test is that **delivered = certified = committed = paid**, with no leg out of step:

1. **Delivered** — milestone evidenced and accepted against criteria.
2. **Certified** — the correct officer issues the certificate; at LG, internal audit confirms value for money.
3. **Committed** — an approved commitment requisition exists and the quarter's uncommitted balance covers the certificate (CCS); the vote carries the cost.
4. **Paid** — settlement by crossed cheque / bank transfer (no cash at LG); retention withheld; advance recovered; security still valid.

Maintain a **funds-flow reconciliation** that lines up, per milestone: amount due, retention withheld, advance recovered, net paid, cumulative paid vs contract sum, and remaining committed/uncommitted budget. A milestone that is certified but not committed (no quarterly limit) is the classic late failure — flag it before it is signed, not after.

For donor-funded contracts, add the donor disbursement leg (reimbursement / advance / direct-payment method) and reconcile drawdowns to certified milestones; the **stricter of the donor rule and the PPDA/LG rule governs**.
