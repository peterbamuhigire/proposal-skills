# Local-Government Procurement (Uganda)

Parent skill: [Uganda PPDA Procurement Framework](../SKILL.md).

Reference material for the ppda-uganda sector skill. Covers how procurement works at district, municipal, city, sub-county, and town-council level, where it varies from the central PPDA process, and where the financial-control regime intersects with procurement. Use when the Procuring and Disposing Entity (PDE) is a local government rather than a central ministry or agency.

Sources: PPDA Act (Cap. 205, as amended by the PPDA (Amendment) Act 2021) and the **PPDA Regulations 2023**, which since **5 February 2024** govern procurement for BOTH central and local government (the former Local Governments (PPDA) Regulations 2006 were revoked); the Local Governments (Financial and Accounting) Regulations 2007, SI 25/2007 (financial-control interface) under the Local Governments Act 1997. Procurement thresholds and methods live in the PPDA Regulations — **not** in SI 25/2007.

**Status note:** The Local Governments (PPDA) Regulations 2006 were **revoked**. Since **5 February 2024**, local-government procurement is governed by the same consolidated **PPDA Regulations 2023** (gazetted 8 December 2023) as central government, under the PPDA Act 2003 (as amended by the PPDA (Amendment) Act 2021), with the retained PPDA Regulations 2014 and the PPDA Guidelines 2024. Do not cite the 2006 instrument as operative. Statutory monetary thresholds change by regulation/guideline — verify the current bands against the PPDA instrument in force at bid date and cite the one relied upon.

## 1. How LG procurement differs from central PDEs

The five-body PDE structure (Accounting Officer, Contracts Committee, PDU, User Department, Evaluation Committee) and the three-stage evaluation still apply. The district-level process is a **variation** of the central PPDA process, not a separate regime. Key differences:

| Element | Central PDE | Local Government |
|---|---|---|
| **Accounting Officer** | Vote accounting officer (Permanent Secretary / head of entity) | **Chief Administrative Officer (CAO)** for a district; **Town Clerk** for a municipal / city / town council |
| **Contracts Committee lawyer** | One member must be a lawyer | LG Contracts Committees are **exempt** from the must-have-a-lawyer rule |
| **Approving authority** | Secretary to the Treasury approves CC | LG appointment/approval routed through the LG structure |
| **Funding base** | Consolidated Fund warrants | Central transfers (conditional/unconditional/equalisation grants), local revenue, and donor funds — each with its own conditions |
| **Oversight** | PPDA Authority; vote-level internal audit | PPDA Authority **plus** the LG Public Accounts Committee and a statutory internal audit reporting directly to council |

## 2. Governance roles at LG level

- **Accounting Officer (CAO / Town Clerk):** overall responsibility for the procurement and disposal process; establishes and works with the Contracts Committee; certifies availability of funds; signs contracts only after CC award (except genuine emergencies); personally accountable for financial transactions of the council.
- **Contracts Committee:** adjudicates PDU recommendations, makes award decisions, approves evaluation/negotiation committees, bidding and contract documents, and the procurement method. LG CCs do not require a lawyer member.
- **Head of Finance / Chief Finance Officer:** receiver and paymaster; operates commitment control; **personally and pecuniarily liable for over-commitment** (LG Regs reg. 8); reports the financial position to the Executive Committee.
- **Internal Audit:** statutory, reports **directly to council** quarterly, and audits procurement for **value for money** — an explicit procurement check, not merely a financial one.
- **No councillor signs council financial documents:** no councillor, chairperson, or speaker may be a bank signatory or sign any council financial document. Council accounts are operated by the Chief Executive plus the Head of Finance (project/agency accounts add a vote controller as a third signatory). This is a hard segregation rule that bidders and contract managers must respect when collecting certificates and payments.

## 3. The procurement–finance interface (why LG bids fail late)

LG procurement is bounded by the financial-control regime in SI 25/2007. A technically winning bid still collapses if the commitment and vote-control rules are not satisfied.

### Commitment Control System (CCS)

- **No LPO, contract, or commitment may be raised without (a) an approved commitment requisition and (b) a sufficient uncommitted balance within that quarter's expenditure limit.** Quarterly expenditure/cash limits are issued early in each quarter; spending cannot exceed the released limit even where the annual budget shows the line.
- The Head of Finance is **personally liable for over-commitment**. This is the financial gate that sits behind every LPO and contract certificate.
- **Vote control:** each vote is controlled against its released provision; a vote controller signs against the vote. Procurement must not be split to defeat the appropriate method or to stay under a limit.
- **Vote on account** (≤25% of prior-year total revenue, valid three months — LG reg. 21) may fund procurement before estimates are approved; commitments must still fit the released amount.

### Payment mechanics

- Contract certificates and LPOs are settled by **crossed cheque** (or the current bank-transfer equivalent), not cash. Local-government revenue is **banked intact** and there are **no cash payments out of revenue collected** — providers should not expect cash settlement of certificates.
- **Internal audit confirms value for money** before/around payment; a payment lacking the supporting commitment, certificate, and VFM trail is challengeable and may trigger surcharge.
- **Surcharge / pecuniary liability (reg. 117–124):** officers who cause loss are personally and pecuniarily responsible; the Executive Committee recommends surcharge and the LG PAC determines it. This disciplines how certificates and variations are issued.

For the underlying financial-control substance (commitment control, vote control, surcharge, banking-intact, signatory rules), the authoritative engine reference is the finance engine at `C:\wamp64\www\chwezi-accounting-doctrine` — `doctrine/references/uganda-public-sector-pfm.md` (Local-government financial control section) and the skill `skills/12-public-sector-and-ipsas/government-procurement-and-fiscal-controls`.

## 4. Implications for bidders and proposal writers

1. **Identify the Accounting Officer correctly** — CAO for a district, Town Clerk for a municipal/city/town council. Address the submission and any complaint to the right office.
2. **Expect a VFM internal-audit check** on award and on payment; build a clean, certificate-ready deliverable and documentation trail.
3. **Phase your cash expectation to the quarter's released limit, not the annual budget.** A signed contract does not guarantee that funds are committed for the quarter; structure the payment schedule and any advance around quarterly releases (see `contract-management-and-payment-linkage.md`).
4. **Do not seek signatures from councillors** on financial documents — they cannot lawfully sign. Route certificates through the Chief Executive and Head of Finance.
5. **Confirm the governing instrument at bid date** (see the verification note above) and cite it, rather than assuming the 2006 or the 2023 text applies.
6. **Settlement is by crossed cheque / bank transfer**; reflect this and the no-cash rule in funds-flow assumptions.
