# Programme Design Framework

## Results Chain

| Layer | Design question | Evidence required |
|---|---|---|
| Inputs | What people, fee-days, tools, partners, and data exist? | Team matrix, budget, partner commitments, tooling readiness. |
| Activities | What will the team do with companies? | Call, screening, diagnostics, coaching, training, platform audit, export marketing, monitoring. |
| Outputs | What tangible products will be delivered? | Selected-company list, reports, action plans, dashboards, training materials, case notes. |
| Outcomes | What changes in company behaviour or capability are expected? | Improved platform, payment/logistics readiness, marketing conversion, unit economics discipline, export plan execution. |
| Impact | What wider programme result is served? | Increased viable cross-border digital trade in volume and value, where evidence supports attribution or contribution. |

Every arrow must state its assumption. Example: diagnostics lead to better TA only if companies provide reliable data and attend co-design sessions.

## Selection Framework

Eligibility is pass/fail:

- Registered and operating company.
- EAC-based and active in e-commerce or digital trade.
- Cross-border-active or export-ready with a proven home-market model.
- Willing to share diagnostic data under confidentiality terms.
- No conflict, sanctions, or integrity issue.

Award scoring is weighted:

- Business-model viability.
- Cross-border potential.
- Innovation and scalability.
- Traction and management capacity.
- Digital operations maturity.
- Inclusion/geographic balance.
- Fit with regional digital trade objectives.

## Diagnostic-to-TA Pipeline

1. Intake and eligibility screen.
2. Public-source due diligence.
3. Diagnostic interview and evidence request.
4. Domain scoring across digital operations, data, governance, marketing, logistics, customer management, finance, and compliance.
5. Unit-economics and margin model.
6. Binding-constraint statement.
7. Co-created TA package.
8. 90-day action plan.
9. Monitoring and follow-up.

## Learning and Scale-Up

- Capture one anonymised case note per assisted company.
- Aggregate lessons by theme, not by identifiable company weakness.
- Separate public learning products from confidential diagnostic data.
- Maintain a good-practice library: payment readiness, trust signals, logistics, conversion, governance, finance, compliance.
