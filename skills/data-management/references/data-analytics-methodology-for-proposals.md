# Data Analytics Methodology for Proposals

Use this reference when a proposal involves dashboards, MIS, surveys, routine monitoring,
business intelligence, AI analytics, data platforms, financial analysis, operational
analytics, or evidence-based decision support.

## Proposal Positioning

Strong proposals do not promise "data analytics" as a vague add-on. They show:

- the decision the analytics will support;
- the datasets required;
- the data quality controls;
- the method used;
- the dashboard, report, or decision workflow produced; and
- the governance and privacy controls that keep the system credible.

## Analytics Method Ladder

| Level | Proposal wording | Typical deliverable |
|---|---|---|
| Descriptive | "We will establish what happened and where performance varies." | KPI dashboard, baseline report, descriptive statistics |
| Diagnostic | "We will identify the drivers of performance gaps." | variance analysis, root-cause analysis, segmented drill-down |
| Predictive | "Where data history is adequate, we will estimate likely future outcomes." | forecast, risk score, anomaly alert |
| Prescriptive | "We will translate evidence into recommended actions under agreed constraints." | options appraisal, optimization, decision rules |

Do not promise predictive or prescriptive analytics unless the ToR, data availability, and
budget support it.

## Data Analytics Workstream

Use this workstream inside methodology sections:

1. Analytics question framing: agree the decisions, indicators, users, and action triggers.
2. Data inventory and access: identify source systems, files, owners, fields, and refresh
   frequency.
3. Data quality profiling: check completeness, validity, accuracy, consistency,
   timeliness, integrity, duplicates, and missingness.
4. Data preparation: clean, code, transform, join, anonymise, and document assumptions.
5. Analysis: run descriptive, diagnostic, predictive, or prescriptive methods as justified.
6. Visualization and reporting: build dashboards, tables, and narrative findings for the
   intended audience.
7. Handover: provide data dictionary, calculation logic, source files, dashboard guide, and
   governance recommendations.

## Proposal Commitments

For assignments involving personal, institutional, or sensitive data, commit to:

- role-based access controls;
- data minimisation;
- anonymisation or aggregation where possible;
- secure storage and transfer;
- documented data dictionary and indicator definitions;
- reproducible calculations; and
- client ownership of final datasets, scripts, dashboards, and documentation.

## Evaluation Language

Use language like:

"Our analytics approach will begin by defining the management decisions the data must
support. We will then profile the available datasets for completeness, validity,
consistency, and timeliness before selecting the appropriate analytical method. This
prevents the common risk of building dashboards or predictive models on data that can only
support descriptive reporting."

## Red Flags to Manage

- ToR asks for AI analytics but provides no data source.
- Client wants a dashboard but has no indicator definitions.
- Datasets come from multiple systems with no shared IDs.
- Survey sample is too small for the requested inference.
- Predictive analytics is requested but there is no historical data.
- Sensitive data is required but no privacy or consent pathway is defined.
